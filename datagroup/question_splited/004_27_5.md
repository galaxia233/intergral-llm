27.5 $\cos^{-1}(-\sqrt{3}/2)$ .

$\cos^{-1}(-\sqrt{3}/2)$ is the angle $\theta$ between 0 and $\pi$ for which $\cos \theta = -\sqrt{3}/2$ . It is seen from Fig. 27-3 that $\theta$ is the supplement of $\pi/6$ , that is, $\theta = 5\pi/6$ .
