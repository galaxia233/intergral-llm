21.36 Find the area in the first quadrant lying under the arc from the $y$ -axis to the first point where the curve $x + y + y^2 = 2$ cuts the positive $x$ -axis.

The curve hits the $y$ -axis when $x = 0$ , that is, $y^2 + y - 2 = 0$ , $(y + 2)(y - 1) = 0$ , $y = -2$ or $y = 1$ . It hits the $x$ -axis when $y = 0$ , that is, when $x = 2$ . Hence, the arc extends in the first quadrant from $(0,1)$ to $(2,0)$ , and the required area is $\int_0^1 (2 - y - y^2) dy = (2y - \frac{1}{2} y^2 - \frac{1}{3} y^3) \int_0^1 = 2 - \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{6}$ .
