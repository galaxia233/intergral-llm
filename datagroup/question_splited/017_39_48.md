39.48 Find the Maclaurin series for $\sin x$ cos $x$ .

$\sin x \cos x = \frac{1}{2} \sin 2x$ . Now, $\sin x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$ . So $\sin 2x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{2^{2n+1} x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$ , and, therefore, $\frac{\sin 2x}{2} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{2^{2n}}{(2n+1)!} x^{2n+1}$ .
