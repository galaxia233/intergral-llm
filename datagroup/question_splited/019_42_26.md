42.27 Find the slope of the tangent line to the curve that is the intersection of the sphere $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = 1$ with the plane $y = \frac{1}{2}$ , at the point $(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}, \sqrt{2}/2)$ .

Since $y$ is constant in the plane $y = \frac{1}{2}$ , the slope of the tangent line to the curve is $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}$ . By implicit differentiation of the equation $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = 1$ , we get $2x + 2z(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}) = 0$ . Hence, at the point $(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}, \sqrt{2}/2)$ , $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -x/z = -\frac{1}{2} / (\sqrt{2}/2) = -1 / \sqrt{2} = -\sqrt{2}/2$ .
