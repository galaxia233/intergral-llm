19.44 A particle moving along a straight line is accelerating at the rate of $3\mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}^2$ . Find the initial velocity if the distance traveled during the first 2 seconds is 10 feet.

$\begin{array}{l}v = \int 3dt = 3t + C.\quad C\text{is the initial velocity} v_0\text{when} t = 0.\end{array}$ Thus, $v = 3t + v_{0}$ . The position $s =$ $\int vdt = \frac{3}{2} t^2 +v_0t + C_1$ . Hence, $s(2) = \frac{3}{2}\cdot 4 + 2v_0 + C_1 = 6 + 2v_0 + C_1$ . On the other hand, $s(0) = C_1$ So, $10 = s(2) - s(0) = 6 + 2v_{0},\quad v_{0} = 2\mathrm{ft / s.}$
