20.91 Verify that the average value of a linear function $f(x) = ax + b$ over an interval $[x_1, x_2]$ is equal to the value of the function at the midpoint of the interval.

By definition, the indicated average value

$$
\begin{array}{l} \bar {f} = \frac {1}{x _ {2} - x _ {1}} \int_ {x _ {1}} ^ {x _ {2}} (a x + b) d x = \frac {1}{x _ {2} - x _ {1}} \left(a \frac {x ^ {2}}{2} + b x\right) \Bigg ] _ {x _ {1}} ^ {x _ {2}} \\ = \frac {1}{x _ {2} - x _ {1}} \left(a \frac {x _ {2} ^ {2}}{2} + b x _ {2} - a \frac {x _ {1} ^ {2}}{2} - b x _ {1}\right) \\ = \frac {1}{x _ {2} - x _ {1}} \left[ \frac {a}{2} \left(x _ {2} - x _ {1}\right) \left(x _ {2} + x _ {1}\right) + b \left(x _ {2} - x _ {1}\right) \right] \\ = a \left(\frac {x _ {2} + x _ {1}}{2}\right) + b = f \left(\frac {x _ {2} + x _ {1}}{2}\right) \\ \end{array}
$$
