43.45 Find the relative extrema of $f(x, y) = x^4 + y^3 - 32x - 27y - 1$ .

$f_{x} = 4x^{3} - 32, f_{y} = 3y^{2} - 27.$ Set $f_{x} = f_{y} = 0$ . Then $x^{3} = 8, y^{2} = 9$ , or $x = 2, y = \pm 3$ . Thus, the critical points are $(2,3)$ and $(2,-3)$ . Applying Problem 43.23, $f_{xy} = 0$ , $f_{xx} = 12x^{2}$ , $f_{yy} = 6y$ , $\Delta = 72x^{2}y$ . At $(2,3)$ , $\Delta = 72(4)(3) > 0$ , and $f_{xx} + f_{yy} = 48 + 18 > 0$ . So, there is a relative minimum at $(2,3)$ . At $(2,-3)$ , $\Delta = 72(4)(-3) < 0$ . Hence, there is no relative extremum at $(2,-3)$ .
