31.29 The region bounded by $y = x^2$ and $y = x$ (Fig. 31-7).

The area $A = \int_{0}^{1}(x - x^{2})dx = \frac{1}{2} x^{2} - \frac{1}{3} x^{3}\Bigg]_{0}^{1} = \frac{1}{2} -\frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6}$ . The moment about $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^1 x(x - x^2)dx = \left(\frac{1}{3} x^3 -\frac{1}{4} x^4\right)\Bigg]_0^1 = \frac{1}{3} -\frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{12}$ . Thus, $\bar{x} = \frac{\frac{1}{12}}{\frac{1}{6}} = \frac{1}{2}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis $M_x = \int_0^1 y(\sqrt{y} -y)dy = \left(\frac{2}{5} y^{5 / 2} - \frac{1}{3} y^3\right)\Bigg]_0^1 = \frac{2}{5} -\frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{15}$ . Thus, $\bar{y} = \frac{\frac{1}{15}}{\frac{1}{6}} = \frac{2}{5}$ .
