43.8 On a hill represented by $z = 8 - 4x^{2} - 2y^{2}$ , find the direction of the steepest grade at $(1,1,2)$ .

In view of Problem 43.2, we wish to find a vector $\mathbf{v}$ in the tangent plane to the surface at $(1,1,2)$ such that the perpendicular projection of $\mathbf{v}$ onto the $xy$ -plane is $\nabla z = (-8x, -4y) = (-8, -4)$ . Thus we must have $\mathbf{v} = (-8, -4, c)$ ; and the component $c$ may be determined from the orthogonality of $\mathbf{v}$ and the surface normal found in Problem 42.105:

$$
(- 8, - 4, c) \cdot (- 8, - 4, - 1) = 0 \quad \text {o r} \quad c = 8 0
$$

Note that the direction of $\mathbf{v}$ is the direction of steepest ascent at $(1,1,2)$ .
