34.57 If $h(u) = \mathbf{F}(u) \cdot \mathbf{G}(u)$ , show that $h'(u) = \mathbf{F}(u) \cdot \mathbf{G}'(u) + \mathbf{F}'(u) \cdot \mathbf{G}(u)$ , another analogue of the product formula for derivatives.

$h(u + \Delta u) - h(u) = \frac{\mathbf{F}(u + \Delta u)\cdot\mathbf{G}(u + \Delta u) - \mathbf{F}(u)\cdot\mathbf{G}(u)}{\Delta u}$

$$
\begin{array}{l} = \frac {\left[ \mathbf {F} (u + \Delta u) - \mathbf {F} (u) \right] \cdot \mathbf {G} (u + \Delta u) + \mathbf {F} (u) \cdot \left[ \mathbf {G} (u + \Delta u) - \mathbf {G} (u) \right]}{\Delta u} \\ = \frac {\mathbf {F} (u + \Delta u) - \mathbf {F} (u)}{\Delta u} \cdot \mathbf {G} (u + \Delta u) + \mathbf {F} (u) \cdot \frac {\mathbf {G} (u + \Delta u) - \mathbf {G} (u)}{\Delta u} \\ \rightarrow \mathbf {F} ^ {\prime} (u) \cdot \mathbf {G} (u) + \mathbf {F} (u) \cdot \mathbf {G} ^ {\prime} (u) \quad \text {a s} \quad \Delta u \rightarrow 0 \\ \end{array}
