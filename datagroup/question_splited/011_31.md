31.24 The region bounded by $y = x^2$ , $y = 0$ , and $x = 1$ (Fig. 31-3).

![](images/293627babd3f8a10a53c53a57e06f68d39d6b1121a61b9735ac31be88c03fca5.jpg)  
Fig. 31-3

The area $A = \int_0^1 x^2 dx = \frac{1}{3} x^3 \big]_0^1 = \frac{1}{3}$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^1 x \cdot x^2 dx = \frac{1}{4} x^4 \big]_0^1 = \frac{1}{4}$ . Hence, the $x$ -coordinate of the centroid is $\bar{x} = M_y / A = \frac{1}{4} / \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{4}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis is $M_x = \int_0^1 y(1 - \sqrt{y}) dy = \frac{1}{2} y^2 - \frac{2}{5} y^{5/2} \big]_0^1 = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{2}{5} = \frac{1}{10}$ . Hence, the $y$ -coordinate of the centroid is $\bar{y} = M_x / A = (\frac{1}{10}) / (\frac{1}{3}) = \frac{3}{10}$ . Thus, the centroid is $(\frac{3}{4}, \frac{3}{10})$ .
