30.16 $\int \frac{dx}{x(x^2 + 1)^2}.$

$\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 1)^2} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{Bx + C}{x^2 + 1} + \frac{Dx + E}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$ . Then $1 = A(x^2 + 1)^2 + x(x^2 + 1)(Bx + C) + x(Dx + E)$ . Let $x = 0$ . Then $1 = A$ . Equate coefficients of $x^4$ : $0 = A + B$ , $B = -A = -1$ . Equate coefficients of $x^3$ : $0 = C$ . Equate coefficients of $x^2$ : $0 = 2A + B + D$ , $D = -2A - B = -1$ . Equate coefficients of $x$ : $0 = C + E$ , $E = -C = 0$ . Thus, $\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 1)^2} = \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{x^2 + 1} - \frac{x}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$ . Hence

$$
\int \frac {d x}{x \left(x ^ {2} + 1\right) ^ {2}} = \ln | x | - \tan^ {- 1} x - \frac {1}{2} \frac {1}{x ^ {2} + 1} + C _ {1}.
$$
