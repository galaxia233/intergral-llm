43.12 If the electric potential $V$ at any point $(x, y)$ is $V = \ln \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ , find the rate of change of $V$ at $(3, 4)$ in the direction toward the point $(2, 6)$ .

A vector in the given direction is $(-1,2)$ , and a corresponding unit vector is $\mathbf{u} = (1 / \sqrt{5})(-1,2)$ . $\nabla V = \left(\frac{x}{x^2 + y^2},\frac{y}{x^2 + y^2}\right) = \left(\frac{3}{25},\frac{4}{25}\right)$ . Hence, the rate of change of $V$ in the specified direction is $\nabla V\cdot \mathbf{u} = \frac{1}{25} (3,4)\cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} (-1,2) = \frac{1}{25\sqrt{5}} (5) = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{25}$ .
