35.28 Show that the point with polar coordinates $(3, 3\pi/4)$ lies on the curve $r = 3\sin 2\theta$ .

Observe that $r = 3$ , $\theta = 3\pi / 4$ do not satisfy the equation $r = 3 \sin 2\theta$ . However (Problem 35.1), the point with polar coordinates $(3, 3\pi / 4)$ also has polar coordinates $(-3, 7\pi / 4)$ , and $r = -3$ , $\theta = 7\pi / 4$ satisfy the equation $r = 3 \sin 2\theta$ , since $3 \sin 2(7\pi / 4) = 3 \sin (7\pi / 2) = 3(-1) = -3$ .
