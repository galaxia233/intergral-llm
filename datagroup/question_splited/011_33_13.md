33.12 Prove by vector methods that an angle inscribed in a semicircle is a right angle.

Let $\angle QRP$ be subtended by a diameter of a circle with center $C$ and radius $r$ (Fig. 33-3). Let $\mathbf{A} = \overrightarrow{CP}$ and $\mathbf{B} = \overrightarrow{CR}$ . Then $\overrightarrow{QR} = \mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}$ and $\overrightarrow{PR} = \mathbf{B} - \mathbf{A}$ . $\overrightarrow{QR} \cdot \overrightarrow{PR} = (\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}) \cdot (\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{A}) = \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} - \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{B} - \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{A} = -r^2 + r^2 = 0$ (since $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{B} = r^2$ ). Hence, $QR \perp PR$ and $\angle QRP$ is a right angle.
