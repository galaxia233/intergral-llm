35.76 Find the arc length of the spiral $r = \theta$ from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = 1$ .

The general arc length formula is $\int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2}\sqrt{r^2 + (dr / d\theta)^2} d\theta$ . In this case, $dr / d\theta = 1$ , and we have $L = \int_0^1\sqrt{\theta^2 + 1} d\theta$ . Letting $\theta = \tan u$ , $d\theta = \sec^2 u du$ , we obtain $\int_0^{\pi /4}\sec^3 u du = \frac{1}{2} (\sec u\tan u + \ln |\sec u + \tan u|)\int_0^{\pi /4} = \frac{1}{2} [\sqrt{2} +\ln (\sqrt{2} +1)]$
