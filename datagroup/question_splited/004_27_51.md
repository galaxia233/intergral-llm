27.51 $\int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{6x - x^2}}.$   
By completing the square, $x^{2} - 6x = (x - 3)^{2} - 9$ . So $6x - x^{2} = 9 - (x - 3)^{2}$ . Let $u = x - 3$ , $du = dx$ . Then $\int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{6x - x^2}} = \int \frac{du}{\sqrt{9 - u^2}}$ . From the formula in Problem 27.41, we get $\sin^{-1}\frac{u}{3} + C = \sin^{-1}\frac{x - 3}{3} + C$ .
