27.18 $\cos (\sin^{-1}\frac{3}{5} +\sec^{-1}3)$

Let $\theta_{1} = \sin^{-1}\frac{3}{5}$ and $\theta_{2} = \sec^{-1}3$ . Since $\frac{3}{5}$ and 3 are positive, $\theta_{1}$ and $\theta_{2}$ are in the first quadrant. Then $\cos (\sin^{-1}\frac{3}{5} + \sec^{-1}3) = \cos (\theta_{1} + \theta_{2}) = \cos \theta_{1}\cos \theta_{2} - \sin \theta_{1}\sin \theta_{2} = \frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{3} - \frac{3}{5}(2\sqrt{2}/3) = \frac{4}{15} - 6\sqrt{2}/15 = (4 - 6\sqrt{2})/15$ .
