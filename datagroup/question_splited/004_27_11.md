27.11 $\sec^{-1}(-2\sqrt{3}/3)$ .

The value $\theta$ must be in the third quadrant. Since $\sec \theta = -2\sqrt{3}/3$ , $\cos \theta = -3/2\sqrt{3} = -\sqrt{3}/2$ . Thus (see Fig. 27-4), $\theta = \pi + \pi/6 = 7\pi/6$ .
