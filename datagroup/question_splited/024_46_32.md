46.32 Find a curve in the $xy$ -plane that passes through the origin and for which the tangent at $(x, y)$ has slope $x^2 + y$ .

$\frac{dy}{dx} = x^2 + y, \frac{dy}{dx} - y = x^2.$ Use an integrating factor. $\rho = \exp \left[\int (-1) dx\right] = e^{-x}.$ $\int \rho q(x) dx = \int e^{-x} x^2 dx = -e^{-x}(x^2 + 2x + 2)$ . (The last equation follows by integration by parts.) Hence, $y = e^{x}[-e^{-x}(x^{2} + 2x + 2) + C], y = -(x^{2} + 2x + 2) + Ce^{x}$ . Since the curve passes through $(0,0), 0 = -2 + C, C = 2$ . Thus, $y = -(x^{2} + 2x + 2) + 2e^{x}$ .
