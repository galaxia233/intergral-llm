40.34 Find the distance from the point $P(1, -1, 2)$ to the line connecting the point $Q(3, 1, 4)$ to $R(1, -3, 0)$ .

Let $\mathbf{A} = \overrightarrow{QP} = (-2, -2, -2)$ , $\mathbf{B} = \overrightarrow{QR} = (-2, -4, -4)$ . Then (see Fig. 40-4) the scalar projection of $\mathbf{A}$ on $\mathbf{B}$ is $s = \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} / |\mathbf{B}| = (4 + 8 + 8) / \sqrt{4 + 16 + 16} = \frac{10}{3}$ and the Pythagorean theorem gives $d^2 = |\mathbf{A}|^2 - s^2 = (4 + 4 + 4) - \frac{100}{9} = \frac{8}{9}$ or $d = 2\sqrt{2}/3$ .
