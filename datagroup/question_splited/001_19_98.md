19.98 Compare the values of $\int 2\cos x\sin x dx$ obtained by the substitutions (a) $u = \sin x$ and (b) $u = \cos x$ , and reconcile the results.

(a) Let $u = \sin x, \quad du = \cos x dx$ . Then $\int 2\sin x \cos x dx = 2\int u du = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} u^2 + C = \sin^2 x + C$ . (b) Let $u = \cos x, \quad du = -\sin x dx$ . Then $\int 2\sin x \cos x dx = -2\int u du = -2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} u^2 + C = -\cos^2 x + C$ . There is no contradiction between the results of (a) and (b). $\sin^2 x$ and $-\cos^2 x$ differ by a constant, since $\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1$ . So, it is not surprising that they have the same derivative.
