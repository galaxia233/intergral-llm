35.30 Sketch the graph of $r = 1 + \cos \theta$

See Fig. 35-3. At $\theta = 0$ , $r = 2$ . As $\theta$ increases to $\pi/2$ , $r$ decreases to 1. As $\theta$ increases to $\pi$ , $r$ decreases to 0. Then, as $\theta$ increases to $3\pi/2$ , $r$ increases to 1, and finally, as $\theta$ increases to $2\pi$ , $r$ increases to 2. After $\theta = 2\pi$ , the curve repeats itself. The graph is called a cardioid.

$$
\begin{array}{c c c c c c} \theta & 0 & \pi / 2 & \pi & 3 \pi / 2 & 2 \pi \\ \hline r & 2 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}
$$
