45.42 Evaluate $L = \int_{\mathcal{C}} y dx + z dy - x dz$ , where $\mathcal{C}$ is the line segment from $(0,1,-1)$ to $(1,2,1)$ .

Parametric equations for $\mathcal{C}$ are $x = t$ , $y = 1 + t$ , $z = -1 + 2t$ for $0 \leq t \leq 1$ . Hence, $L = \int_{0}^{1} \left( y \frac{dx}{dt} + z \frac{dy}{dt} - x \frac{dz}{dt} \right) dt = \int_{0}^{1} [1 + t + (-1 + 2t) - 2t] dt = \int_{0}^{1} t dt = \frac{1}{2} t^2 \bigg]_{0}^{1} = \frac{1}{2}$ .
