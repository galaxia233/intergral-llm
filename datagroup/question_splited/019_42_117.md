42.117 Find equations of the normal line to the surface $x^{2} + 4y^{2} = z^{2}$ at (3, 2, 5).

A normal vector is $(2x, 8y, -2z) = (6, 16, -10)$ , by Problem 42.109 [with $f(x, y, z) = x^2 + 4y^2 - z^2$ ]. Hence, equations for the normal line are $\frac{x - 3}{6} = \frac{y - 2}{16} = \frac{z - 5}{-10}$ , or, in parametric form, $x = 3 + 6t$ , $y = 2 + 16t$ , $z = 5 - 10t$ .
