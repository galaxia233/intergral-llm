44.78 Find the mass of a solid right circular cone $\mathcal{C}$ of height $h$ and radius of base $b$ whose density is numerically equal to the distance from its axis.

Let $\alpha = \tan^{-1}(b / h)$ . In spherical coordinates, the lateral surface is $\phi = \alpha$ . $M = \iint_{0} r dV = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\alpha} \int_{0}^{h \sec \phi} \rho \sin \phi \cdot \rho^2 \sin \phi d\rho d\phi d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\alpha} \frac{1}{4} \rho^4 \sin^2 \phi \big]_0^{h \sec \phi} d\phi d\theta = \frac{1}{4} h^4 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\alpha} \sec^4 \phi \sin^2 \phi d\phi d\theta = \frac{1}{4} h^4 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\alpha} \sec^4 \phi d\phi d\theta = \frac{1}{4} h^4 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\alpha} \tan^3 \phi \big]_0^{\alpha} d\theta = \frac{1}{4} h^4 \cdot \frac{1}{3} \tan^3 \alpha \int_{0}^{2\pi} d\theta = \frac{1}{12} h^4 (b / h)^3 \cdot 2\pi = \frac{1}{6} \pi hb^3$ .
