43.34 Find positive numbers $x, y, z$ such that $x + y + z = 20$ and $xyz^2$ is a maximum.

$xyz^{2} = (20 - y - z)yz^{2} = 20yz^{2} - y^{2}z^{2} - yz^{3}$ . We must maximize this function $f(y,z)$ under the conditions $y > 0$ , $z > 0$ , $y + z < 20$ . $f_{y} = 20z^{2} - 2yz^{2} - z^{3}$ , $f_{z} = 40yz - 2y^{2}z - 3yz^{2}$ . Set $f_{y} = 0$ , $f_{z} = 0$ . Then, $z^{2}(20 - 2y - z) = 0$ and $yz(40 - 2y - 3z) = 0$ . Since $y > 0$ and $z > 0$ , $20 - 2y - z = 0$ and $40 - 2y - 3z = 0$ . Solving these equations simultaneously, we obtain $y = 5$ , $z = 10$ . Hence, $x = 5$ . This point $(5,5,10)$ yields an absolute maximum (by an argument similar to that given in Problem 43.32).
