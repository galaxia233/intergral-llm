27.46 $\int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{x^2 - 4}}.$   
Let $x = 2u$ , $dx = 2du$ , $\sqrt{x^2 - 4} = \sqrt{4u^2 - 4} = \sqrt{4(u^2 - 1)} = 2\sqrt{u^2 - 1}$ . Then $\int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{x^2 - 4}} = \frac{2du}{2u \cdot 2\sqrt{u^2 - 1}} = \frac{1}{2}\int \frac{du}{u\sqrt{u^2 - 1}} = \frac{1}{2}\sec^{-1} u + C = \frac{1}{2}\sec^{-1}(x / 2) + C$ . This is a special case of the formula $\int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{x^2 - a^2}} = \frac{1}{a}\sec^{-1}\frac{x}{a} + C$ for $a > 0$ .
