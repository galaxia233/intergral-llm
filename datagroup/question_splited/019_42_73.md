42.73 Let $z = u^3 v^5$ , where $u = x + y$ and $v = x - y$ . Find $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}$ (a) by the chain rule, (b) by substitution and explicit computation.

(b) $z = (x + y)^{3}(x - y)^{5}$ . Hence,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} = (x + y) ^ {3} [ 5 (x - y) ^ {4} (- 1) ] + 3 (x + y) ^ {2} (x - y) ^ {5} \\ = (x + y) ^ {2} (x - y) ^ {4} [ - 5 (x + y) + 3 (x - y) ] \\ = (x + y) ^ {2} (x - y) ^ {4} (- 2 x - 8 y) = - 2 (x + 4 y) (x + y) ^ {2} (x - y) ^ {4}. \\ \end{array}
$$
