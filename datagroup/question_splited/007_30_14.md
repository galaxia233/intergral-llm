30.14 $\int \frac{dx}{(x^2 + 1)(x^2 + 4)}.$

Neither $x^2 + 1$ nor $x^2 + 4$ factors. Then $\frac{1}{(x^2 + 1)(x^2 + 4)} = \frac{Ax + B}{x^2 + 1} + \frac{Cx + D}{x^2 + 4}$ . Hence, $1 = (Ax + B)(x^2 + 4) + (Cx + D)(x^2 + 1)$ . Equate coefficients of $x^3$ : (*) $0 = A + C$ . Equate coefficients of $x$ : $0 = 4A + C$ . Subtracting (*) from this equation, we get $3A = 0$ , $A = 0$ , $C = 0$ . Equate coefficients of $x^2$ : $(**) 0 = B + D$ . Equate constant coefficients: $1 = 4B + D$ . Subtracting $(**)$ from this equation, we get $3B = 1$ , $B = \frac{1}{3}$ , $D = -\frac{1}{3}$ . Thus, $\frac{1}{(x^2 + 1)(x^2 + 4)} = \frac{1}{3} \frac{1}{x^2 + 1} - \frac{1}{3} \frac{1}{x^2 + 4}$ . Hence, $\int \frac{dx}{(x^2 + 1)(x^2 + 4)} = \frac{1}{3} \tan^{-1} x - \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \tan^{-1} (x / 2) + C_1 = \frac{1}{3} \tan^{-1} x - \frac{1}{6} \tan^{-1} (x / 2) + C_1$ .
