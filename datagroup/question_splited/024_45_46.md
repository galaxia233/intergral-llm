45.46 If $\mathcal{C}$ is the circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = 49$ , $\mathbf{F}(x, y) = (x^{2}, y^{2})$ , and $\mathbf{n}$ denotes the exterior unit normal vector, compute $\int_{\mathbb{C}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} ds$ .

$\int_{\mathcal{C}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} ds = \int_{\mathcal{C}} (x^2, y^2) \cdot \left( \frac{dy}{ds}, -\frac{dx}{ds} \right) ds, \quad \text{since} \quad \mathbf{n} = \left( \frac{dy}{ds}, -\frac{dx}{ds} \right). \quad \text{Hence, we obtain} \quad \int_{\mathcal{C}} x^2 dy - y^2 dx = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( x^2 \frac{dy}{dt} - y^2 \frac{dx}{dt} \right) dt, \quad \text{where} \quad \mathcal{C}$ is parametrized as $x = 7\cos t, y = 7\sin t, 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$ . Thus, we have $\int_{0}^{2\pi} [(7^2\cos^2 t)(7\cos t) - (7^2\sin^2 t)(-7\sin t)] dt = (7)^3 \int_{0}^{2\pi} (\cos^3 t + \sin^3 t) dt = (7)^3 \int_{0}^{2\pi} [(\cos t - \sin^2 t\cos t) + (\sin t - \cos^2 t\sin t)] dt = (7)^3 (\sin t - \frac{1}{3}\sin^3 t - \cos t + \frac{1}{3}\cos^3 t)]_0^{2\pi} = 0$ (by periodicity).
