34.97 Show that, for a curve $y = f(x)$ , the curvature is given by the formula $\kappa = y'' / [1 + (y')^2]^{3/2}$ . (We assume that $ds/dx > 0$ , that is, the arc length increases with $x$ .)

Since $y'$ is the slope of the tangent line, $\tan \phi = y'$ . Hence, differentiating with respect to $s$ , $\sec^2 \phi (d\phi / ds) = y'' / (ds / dx)$ . But, $\sec^2 \phi = 1 + \tan^2 \phi = 1 + (y')^2$ , and $ds / dx = [1 + (y')^2]^{1/2}$ . Thus,

$$
\kappa = \frac {d \phi}{d s} = \frac {y ^ {\prime \prime} / \left[ 1 + \left(y ^ {\prime}\right) ^ {2}\right]}{\left[ 1 + \left(y ^ {\prime}\right) ^ {2} \right] ^ {1 / 2}} = \frac {y ^ {\prime \prime}}{\left[ 1 + \left(y ^ {\prime}\right) ^ {2} \right] ^ {3 / 2}}
$$
