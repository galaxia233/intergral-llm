41.62 Determine whether the function

$$
f (x, y) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \frac {x y}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} & \text {i f} (x, y) \neq (0, 0) \\ 0 & \text {i f} (x, y) = (0, 0) \end{array} \right.
$$

is continuous at the origin.

As $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ along the line $y = mx$ , $f(x, y) = \frac{mx^2}{(1 + m^2)x^2} = \frac{m}{1 + m^2}$ . Hence, $\lim_{(x, y) \to (0, 0)} f(x, y)$ does not exist and, therefore, $f(x, y)$ is not continuous [and cannot be made continuous by redefining $f(0, 0)$ ].
