29.30 Find the arc length of the parabola $y = x^2$ from (0, 0) to (2, 4).

\(y^\prime = 2x\) So \(L = \int_0^2\sqrt{1 + (y')^2} dx = \int_0^2\sqrt{1 + 4x^2} dx.\) Let \(x = \frac {1}{2}\tan \theta ,dx = \frac {1}{2}\sec^2\theta d\theta ,\quad \sqrt{1 + 4x^2} =\) \(\sqrt{1 + \tan^2\theta} = \sec \theta .\) As \(x\) increases from O to 2, \(\theta\) increases from O to \(\alpha = \tan^{-1}4\) (Fig. 29-7). So, by Problem 28.40, \(\begin{array}{r}\int_0^2\sqrt{1 + 4x^2} dx = \frac {1}{2}\int_0^\alpha \sec^3\theta d\theta = \frac {1}{4} (\tan \theta \sec \theta +\ln |\sec \theta +\tan \theta |)\end{array} ]_0^\alpha = \frac {1}{4}\{\left[4\sqrt{17} +\ln (\sqrt{17} +\) 4)] - (0 + 0) \} = \sqrt{17} +\frac {1}{4}\ln (\sqrt{17} +4).\)
