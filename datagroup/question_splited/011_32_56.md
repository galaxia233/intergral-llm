32.57 Calculate $L\{t\}$

$$
\begin{array}{l} L \{t \} = \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} e ^ {- s t} t d t = \lim  _ {w \rightarrow + \infty} \int_ {0} ^ {w} e ^ {- s t} t d t = \lim  _ {w \rightarrow + \infty} \left(- \frac {1}{s} t e ^ {- s t} - \frac {1}{s ^ {2}} e ^ {- s t}\right) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {w} \\ = \lim  _ {w \rightarrow + \infty} \left(- \frac {1}{s} w e ^ {- s w} - \frac {1}{s ^ {2}} e ^ {- s w} + \frac {1}{s ^ {2}}\right) = 0 + 0 + \frac {1}{s ^ {2}} \\ \end{array}
$$

(Here, the integration was performed by parts: $u = t$ , $dv = e^{-st} dt$ .) Thus, $L\{t\} = 1 / s^2$ .
