35.72 Find the centroid of the region inside the first-quadrant loop of the rose $r = \sin 2\theta$ (Fig. 35-6).

The area $A$ is $\frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi /2}\sin^{2}2\theta d\theta = \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi /2}\frac{1}{2} (1 - \cos 4\theta)d\theta = \frac{1}{4} (\theta -\frac{1}{4}\sin 4\theta)\rbrack_{0}^{\pi /2} = \pi /8.$ The general formulas for the centroid $(\bar{x},\bar{y})$ are $A\bar{x} = \frac{1}{3}\int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2}r^3\cos \theta d\theta$ and $A\bar{y} = \frac{1}{3}\int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2}r^3\sin \theta d\theta ,$ where $A$ is the area. In this case, we have $(\pi /8)\bar{x} = \frac{1}{3}\int_{0}^{\pi /2}\sin^{3}2\theta \cos \theta d\theta = \frac{8}{3}\int_{0}^{\pi /2}\sin^{3}\theta \cos^{4}\theta d\theta = \frac{8}{3}\int_{0}^{\pi /2}(1 - \cos^{2}\theta)\cdot$ $\cos^4\theta \sin \theta d\theta = \frac{8}{3}\int_0^{\pi /2}(\cos^4\theta \sin \theta -\cos^6\theta \sin \theta)d\theta = \frac{8}{3} (-\frac{1}{5}\cos^5\theta +\frac{1}{7}\cos^7\theta)]_0^{\pi /2} = -\frac{8}{3} (-\frac{1}{5} +\frac{1}{7}) = \frac{16}{105}.$ Hence, $\bar{x} = 128 / 105\pi .$ By symmetry, $\bar{y} = 128 / 105\pi$
