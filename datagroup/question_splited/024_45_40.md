45.40 Use a line integral to find the mass of a wire running along the parabola $\mathcal{C}$ : $y = x^2$ from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$ , if the density (mass per unit length) of the wire at any point $(x,y)$ is numerically equal to $x$ .

$$
m = \int_ {\mathcal {C}} x d s = \int_ {0} ^ {1} x \sqrt {1 + \left(\frac {d y}{d x}\right) ^ {2}} d x = \int_ {0} ^ {1} x \sqrt {1 + 4 x ^ {2}} d x = \frac {1}{8} \cdot \frac {2}{5} (1 + 4 x ^ {2}) ^ {3 / 2} ] _ {0} ^ {1} = \frac {1}{1 2} [ (5) ^ {3 / 2} - 1 ] = \frac {1}{1 2} (5 \sqrt {5} - 1).
$$
