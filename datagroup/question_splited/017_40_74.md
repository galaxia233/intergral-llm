40.74 Show that the lines $\mathcal{L}_1$ : $x = x_0 + at$ , $y = y_0 + bt$ , $z = z_0 + ct$ and $\mathcal{L}_2$ : $x = x_1 + At$ , $y = y_1 + Bt$ , $z = z_1 + Ct$ are parallel if and only if $a, b, c$ are proportional to $A, B, C$ .

$\mathcal{L}_1$ is parallel to the vector $(a, b, c)$ . $\mathcal{L}_2$ is parallel to the vector $(A, B, C)$ . Therefore, $\mathcal{L}_1$ is parallel to $\mathcal{L}_2$ if and only if $(a, b, c)$ is parallel to $(A, B, C)$ , that is, if and only if $(a, b, c) = \lambda(A, B, C)$ for some $\lambda$ . The latter condition means that $a, b, c$ are proportional to $A, B, C$ .
