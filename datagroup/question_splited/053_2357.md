【2357】 求：

(1) $\lim_{x\to 0}x\int_{x}^{1}\frac{\cos t}{t^2}\mathrm{d}t;$

解（1）由于 $1 - \frac{t^2}{2} \leqslant \cos t \leqslant 1$ ，所以， $\int_{x}^{1} \frac{1 - \frac{t^2}{2}}{t^2} \mathrm{d}t \leqslant \int_{x}^{1} \frac{\cos t}{t^2} \mathrm{d}t \leqslant \int_{x}^{1} \frac{\mathrm{d}t}{t^2}$ ，

计算得 $-\frac{3}{2} + \frac{x}{2} + \frac{1}{x} \leqslant \int_{x}^{1} \frac{\cos t}{t^2} \mathrm{d}t \leqslant -1 + \frac{1}{x}$ . 又由于

$$
\lim  _ {x \to 0} x \Big (- \frac {3}{2} + \frac {x}{2} + \frac {1}{x} \Big) = 1 \quad \text {及} \quad \lim  _ {x \to 0} x \Big (- 1 + \frac {1}{x} \Big) = 1,
$$

故最后得到 $\lim_{x\to 0}x\int_{x}^{1}\frac{\cos t}{t^2}\mathrm{d}t = 1;$

(2) 由于 $t^2 < \sqrt{1 + t^4}$ , 所以， $\int_0^x \sqrt{1 + t^4} \mathrm{d}t \geqslant \int_0^x t^2 \mathrm{d}t = \frac{x^3}{3}$ , 从而，当 $x \to +\infty$ 时， $\int_0^x \sqrt{1 + t^4} \mathrm{d}t \to +\infty$ . 利用洛必达法则，得

$$
\lim  _ {x \rightarrow + \infty} \frac {\int_ {0} ^ {x} \sqrt {1 + t ^ {4}} d t}{x ^ {3}} = \lim  _ {x \rightarrow + \infty} \frac {\sqrt {1 + x ^ {4}}}{3 x ^ {2}} = \frac {1}{3};
$$

（4）由于 $f(t)$ 在 $t = 0$ 处右连续，故对任意的 $\varepsilon > 0$ ，存在 $\delta' > 0$ ，使当 $0 < t < \delta'$ 时，有 $|f(t) - f(0)| < \frac{a\varepsilon}{2}$ . 今又取 $0 < \delta < \delta'$ ，使当 $0 < x < \delta$ 时，有

$$
\left| x ^ {a} \int_ {\delta^ {\prime}} ^ {1} \frac {f (t) - f (0)}{t ^ {a + 1}} d t \right| <   \frac {\varepsilon}{2}.
$$

于是，当 $0 < x < \delta$ 时，就有

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left| x ^ {a} \int_ {x} ^ {1} \frac {f (t) - f (0)}{t ^ {a + 1}} d t \right| = \left| x ^ {a} \int_ {x} ^ {\delta^ {\prime}} \frac {f (t) - f (0)}{t ^ {a + 1}} d t + x ^ {a} \int_ {\delta^ {\prime}} ^ {1} \frac {f (t) - f (0)}{t ^ {a + 1}} d t \right| \\ \leqslant \frac {a \varepsilon}{2} x ^ {a} \int_ {x} ^ {\delta^ {\prime}} \frac {\mathrm {d} t}{t ^ {a + 1}} + \frac {\varepsilon}{2} = \frac {\varepsilon}{2} x ^ {a} \left(\frac {1}{x ^ {a}} - \frac {1}{\delta^ {\prime a}}\right) + \frac {\varepsilon}{2} <   \frac {\varepsilon}{2} + \frac {\varepsilon}{2} = \varepsilon . \\ \end{array}
$$

故 $\lim_{x\to +0}x^{\alpha}\int_{x}^{1}\frac{f(t) - f(0)}{t^{\alpha + 1}}\mathrm{d}x = 0,$

最后得到 $\lim_{x\to +0}x^a\int_x^1\frac{f(t)}{t^{a + 1}}\mathrm{d}t = \lim_{x\to +0}x^a\int_x^1\frac{f(0)}{t^{a + 1}}\mathrm{d}t = \lim_{x\to +0}x^a f(0)\left[-\frac{1}{a} t^{-a}\right]\Bigg{|}_x^1$

$$
= \lim  _ {x \rightarrow + 0} x ^ {a} f (0) \left(\frac {1}{a x ^ {a}} - \frac {1}{a}\right) = \frac {f (0)}{a}.
