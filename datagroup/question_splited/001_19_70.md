19.70 Find the equation of a curve such that $y''$ is always 2 and, at the point (2, 6), the slope of the tangent line is 10.

$y^\prime = \int y''dx = \int 2dx = 2x + C.$ When $x = 2$ $y^\prime = 10;$ so, $10 = 4 + C,\quad C = 6,\quad y^{\prime} = 2x + 6.$ Hence, $y = \int y^{\prime}dx = x^{2} + 6x + C_{1}.$ When $x = 2,\quad y = 6;$ thus, $6 = 16 + C_1,\quad C_1 = -10,\quad y = x^2 +6x - 10.$
