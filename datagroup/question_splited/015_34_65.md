34.107 Find the tangential and normal components of the acceleration vector for the curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (e^t, e^{2t})$ (a motion along the parabola $y = x^2$ ).

$\mathbf{R}^{\prime} = (e^{t},2e^{2t})$ ， $\mathbf{R}'' = (e^t,4e^{2t})$ ： $v = |\mathbf{R}'| = \sqrt{e^{2t} + 4e^{4t}} = e^t\sqrt{1 + 4e^{2t}}.$ Hence,

$$
\frac {d v}{d t} = \frac {4 e ^ {t} \cdot e ^ {2 t}}{\sqrt {1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}}} + e ^ {t} \sqrt {1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}} = \frac {e ^ {t} (8 e ^ {2 t} + 1)}{\sqrt {1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}}} \quad | \mathbb {R} ^ {\prime \prime} | = \sqrt {e ^ {2 t} + 1 6 e ^ {4 t}} = e ^ {t} \sqrt {1 + 1 6 e ^ {2 t}}
$$

Since $\mathbf{R}'' = (dv / dt)\mathbf{T} + (v^2 /\rho)\mathbf{N},$ and $\mathbf{T}$ and $\mathbf{N}$ are perpendicular, the Pythagorean theorem yields $\left|\mathbf{R}''\right|^2 = (dv / dt)^2 +(v^2 /\rho)^2.$ Hence,

$$
e ^ {2 t} \left(1 + 1 6 e ^ {2 t}\right) = \frac {e ^ {2 t} \left(8 e ^ {2 t} + 1\right) ^ {2}}{1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}} + \left(\frac {v ^ {2}}{\rho}\right) ^ {2}
$$

So, $\left(\frac{v^2}{\rho}\right)^2 = e^{2t}(1 + 16e^{2t}) - \frac{e^{2t}(8e^{2t} + 1)^2}{1 + 4e^{2t}} = \frac{e^{2t}}{1 + 4e^{2t}}[(1 + 16e^{2t})(1 + 4e^{2t}) - (64e^{4t} + 16e^{2t} + 1)]$

$$
= \frac {e ^ {2 t}}{1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}} (4 e ^ {2 t}) = \frac {4 e ^ {4 t}}{1 + 4 e ^ {2 t}}
$$

Thus, $v^2 / \rho = \frac{2e^{2t} \sqrt{1 + 4e^{2t}}}{\sqrt{1 + 4e^{2t}}}$ . This is the normal component, and we already found the tangential component as $e'(8e^{2t} + 1) / \sqrt{1 + 4e^{2t}}$ . Note that we avoided a direct computation of $v^2 / \rho$ , which is usually tedious.
