41.55 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x}{x^2 + y^2}$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then

$$
\left| \frac {x}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} \right| = \left| \frac {x}{x ^ {2} + m ^ {2} x ^ {2}} \right| = \frac {1}{| x | (1 + m ^ {2})} \rightarrow + \infty \quad \text {a s} \quad (x, y) \rightarrow (0, 0)
$$

Hence, $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x}{x^2 + y^2}$ does not exist.
