42.67 Let $z = r^2 + s^2 + t^2$ and $t = r su$ . Clarify the two possible values of $\frac{\partial z}{\partial r}$ and find both values.

(1) If $z = f(r, s, t) = r^2 + s^2 + t^2$ , then $\frac{\partial z}{\partial r}$ means $f_r(r, s, t) = 2r$ . (2) If $z = r^2 + s^2 + t^2 = r^2 + s^2 + (rsu)^2 = r^2 + s^2 + r^2s^2u^2 = g(r, s, u)$ , then $\frac{\partial z}{\partial r}$ means $g_r(r, s, u) = 2r + 2rs^2u^2$ . To distinguish the two possible values, one often uses $\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial r}\right)_{s,t}$ for the first value, and $\left(\frac{\partial z}{\partial r}\right)_{s,u}$ for the second value.
