19.97 The equation $xy = c$ represents the family of all equilateral hyperbolas with center at the origin. Find the equation of the family of curves that intersect the curves of the given family at right angles.

For the given equation, $xy' + y = 0$ , $y' = -y/x$ . Hence, for the orthogonal family, $dy/dx = x/y$ , $\int y dy = \int x dx$ , $\frac{1}{2} y^2 = \frac{1}{2} x^2 + C$ , $y^2 - x^2 = C_1$ . This is a family of hyperbolas with axes of symmetry on the $x$ -or $y$ -axis.
