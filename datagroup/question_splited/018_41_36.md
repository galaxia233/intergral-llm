41.36 Describe the level curves of $f(x, y) = y^2 - x^2$ .

See Fig. 41-20. The level curves, $y^{2} - x^{2} = k$ are two sets of rectangular hyperbolas (corresponding to $k > 0$ and $k < 0$ ), plus two straight lines ( $k = 0$ ) to which all the hyperbolas are asymptotic. There is a single level curve through every point $(x, y)$ except $(0, 0)$ , which lies on two.
