42.97 Verify Leibniz's formula (Problem 42.96) for $u = x, \quad v = x^2, \quad \text{and} \quad f(x, y) = x^3y^2 + x^2y^3$ .

\(
\begin{array}{l}
du / dx = 1, dv / dx = 2x, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 3x^2y^2 + 2xy^3, \text{ and } \int_u^v \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} dy = \int_x^{x^2}(3x^2y^2 + 2xy^3)dy = (x^2y^3 + \frac{1}{2} xy^4)\bigg|_x^{x^2} = \\
(x^8 + \frac{1}{2}x^9) - (x^5 + \frac{1}{2}x^5). \quad \text {So,} \quad -f(x,u) \frac{du}{dx} + f(x,v) \frac{dv}{dx} + \int_u^v \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} dy = -(x^3u^2 + x^2u^3) + (x^3v^2 + x^2v^3)2x + \\
x^8 + \frac{1}{2}x^9 - x^5 - \frac{1}{2}x^5 = -2x^5 + (x^7 + x^8)(2x) + x^8 + \frac{1}{2}x^9 - \frac{3}{2}x^5 = -\frac{7}{2}x^5 + 3x^8 + \frac{5}{2}x^9. \quad \text {On the other hand,} \\
\int_u^v f(x,y)dy = \int_x^{x^2}(x^3y^2 + x^2y^3)dy = (\frac{1}{3}x^3y^3 + \frac{1}{4}x^2y^4)\bigg|_x^{x^2} = (\frac{1}{3}x^9 + \frac{1}{4}x^{10}) - (\frac{1}{3}x^6 + \frac{1}{4}x^6) = \frac{1}{3}x^9 + \frac{1}{4}x^{10} - \frac{7}{12}x^6.
\end{array}
\)
