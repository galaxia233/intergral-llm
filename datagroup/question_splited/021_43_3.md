43.3 Find the derivative of $f(x, y) = 2x^{2} - 3xy + 5y^{2}$ at the point (1, 2) in the direction of the unit vector $\mathbf{u}$ making an angle of $45^{\circ}$ with the positive $x$ -axis.

$\mathbf{u} = (\cos 45^{\circ}, \sin 45^{\circ}) = (\sqrt{2}/2, \sqrt{2}/2)$ . $\nabla f = (4x - 3y, -3x + 10y) = (-2, 17)$ at $(1, 2)$ . Hence, the derivative is $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = (-2, 17) \cdot (\sqrt{2}/2, \sqrt{2}/2) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} (15) = \frac{15\sqrt{2}}{2}$ .
