30.22 Find the area of the region in the first quadrant under the curve $y = \frac{1}{x^3 + 27}$ and to the left of the line $x = 3$ . $A = \int_{0}^{3} \frac{dx}{x^3 + 27}$ Note that $x = -3$ is a root of $x^3 + 27$ , and, dividing the latter by $x - 3$ , we obtain $x^2 - 3x + 9$ , which is irreducible. Let $\frac{1}{(x + 3)(x^2 - 3x + 9)} = \frac{A}{x + 3} + \frac{Bx + C}{x^2 - 3x + 9}$ . Then $1 = A(x^2 - 3x + 9) + (x + 3)(Bx + C)$ . Let $x = -3$ . Then $1 = 27A$ , $A = \frac{1}{27}$ . Equate the coefficients of $x^2$ : $0 = A + B$ , $B = -A = -\frac{1}{27}$ . Equate the constant coefficients: $1 = 9A + 3C$ , $C = \frac{6}{27}$ . Thus, $\frac{1}{x^3 + 27} = \frac{1}{27} \frac{1}{x + 3} - \frac{1}{27} \frac{x - 6}{x^2 - 3x + 9}$ . Hence, $\int \frac{dx}{x^3 + 27} = \frac{1}{27} \ln |x + 3| - \frac{1}{27} \int \frac{x - 6}{x^2 - 3x + 9} dx$ . But $\int \frac{x - 6}{x^2 - 3x + 9} dx = \int \frac{(x - 6)dx}{(x - \frac{3}{2})^2 + \frac{27}{4}} = \int \frac{(x - \frac{3}{2})dx}{(x - \frac{3}{2})^2 + \frac{27}{4}} = \frac{1}{2} \ln [(x - \frac{3}{2})^2 + \frac{27}{4}] - \frac{9}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{(3\sqrt{3}/2)} \tan^{-1} \frac{x - \frac{3}{2}}{3\sqrt{3}/2} = \frac{1}{2} \ln (x^2 - 3x + 9) - \sqrt{3} \tan^{-1} \frac{2x - 3}{3\sqrt{3}}$ . Thus, the complete integral is $\left[ \frac{1}{27} \ln |x + 3| - \frac{1}{54} \ln (x^2 - 3x + 9) + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{27} \tan^{-1} \frac{2x - 3}{3\sqrt{3}} \right]_0^3 = \left( \frac{1}{27} \ln 6 - \frac{1}{54} \ln 9 + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{27} \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \right) - \left[ \frac{1}{27} \ln 3 - \frac{1}{54} \ln 9 + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{27} \tan^{-1} \left( -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \right) \right] = \frac{1}{81} (3\ln 2 + \pi\sqrt{3})$ . (Note that $\tan^{-1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\pi}{6}$ .)
