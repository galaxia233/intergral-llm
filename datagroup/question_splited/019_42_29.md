42.30 Find equations of the tangent line at the point $(-2,1,5)$ to the hyperbola that is the intersection of the surface $z = 2x^{2} - 3y^{2}$ and the plane $z = 5$ .

1 Think of $y$ as a function of $x$ and $z$ . Then the slope of the tangent line to the curve is $\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}$ . By implicit differentiation with respect to $x$ , $0 = 4x - 6y(\frac{\partial y}{\partial x})$ . Hence, at $(-2, 1, 5)$ , $0 = -8 - 6(\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}), \frac{\partial y}{\partial x} = -\frac{4}{3}$ . So, a vector in the direction of the tangent line is $(1, -\frac{4}{3}, 0)$ , and parametric equations for the tangent line are $x = -2 + t$ , $y = 1 - \frac{4}{3} t$ , $z = 5$ (or, equivalently, the pair of planes $4x + 3y = -5$ and $z = 5$ ).
