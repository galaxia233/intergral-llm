41.30 Show that the hyperbolic paraboloid $z = y^{2} - x^{2}$ is a ruled surface. (See the definition in Problem 41.29.)

Consider any point $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$ on the surface. The following line $L$ : $x = x_0 + t$ , $y = y_0 + t$ , $z = z_0 + 2(y_0 - x_0)t$ contains the given point (when $t = 0$ ). Note that $\mathcal{L}$ lies on the paraboloid: for $(x, y, z)$ on $\mathcal{L}$ , $y^2 - x^2 = (y_0 + t)^2 - (x_0 + t)^2 = y_0^2 + 2y_0t - x_0^2 - 2x_0t = (y_0^2 - x_0^2) + 2(y_0 - x_0)t = z_0 + 2(y_0 - x_0)t = z$ . (See Fig. 41-15.)
