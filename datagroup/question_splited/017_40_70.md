40.70 Find the points at which the line of Problem 40.69 cuts the coordinate planes.

By Problem 40.69, the parametric equations are $x = 1 + 2t$ , $y = 2 + 6t$ , $z = -5 + t$ . To find the intersection with the $xy$ -plane, set $z = 0$ . Then $t = 5$ , $x = 11$ , $y = 32$ . So, the intersection point is (11, 32, 0). To find the intersection with the $xz$ -plane, set $y = 0$ . Then $t = -\frac{1}{3}$ , $x = \frac{1}{3}$ , $z = -\frac{16}{3}$ . So, the intersection is $\left(\frac{1}{3}, 0, -\frac{16}{3}\right)$ . To find the intersection with the $yz$ -plane, set $x = 0$ . Then $t = -\frac{1}{2}$ , $y = -1$ , $z = -\frac{11}{2}$ . So, the intersection is $(0, -1, -\frac{11}{2})$ .
