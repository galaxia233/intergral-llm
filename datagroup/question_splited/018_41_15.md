41.15 Describe and sketch the graph of $\frac{z^2}{c^2} -\frac{x^2}{a^2} -\frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1,$ where $a,b,c > 0$

See Fig. 41-10. Note that $\frac{z^2}{c^2} = 1 + \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} \geq 1$ . Hence, $|z| \geq c$ . The sections by planes $z = k$ with $|k| > c$ , are ellipses. When $|z| = c$ , we obtain a point $(0,0,\pm c)$ . The sections determined by planes $x = k$ or $y = k$ are hyperbolas. The surface is called a hyperboloid of two sheets.
