42.28 Find the slopes of the tangent lines to the curves cut from the surface $z = 3x^{2} + 4y^{2} - 6$ by planes through the point $(1,1,1)$ and parallel to the $xz$ - and $yz$ -planes.

The plane through $(1,1,1)$ and parallel to the $xz$ -plane is $y = 1$ . The slope of the tangent line to the resulting curve is $\partial z / \partial x = 6x = 6$ . The plane through $(1,1,1)$ and parallel to the $yz$ -plane is $x = 1$ . The slope of the tangent line to the resulting curve is $\partial z / \partial y = 8y = 8$ .
