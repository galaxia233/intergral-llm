21.10 The bounded region between the parabola $y = x^{2} - x - 6$ and the line $y = -4$ .

See Fig. 21-10. First we find the points of intersection: $-4 = x^{2} - x - 6$ , $x^{2} - x - 2 = 0$ , $(x - 2)(x + 1) = 0$ , $x = 2$ or $x = -1$ . Thus, the intersection points are $(2, -4)$ and $(-1, -4)$ . The upper boundary of the region is $y = -4$ , and the lower boundary is the parabola. The area is given by $\int_{-1}^{2}[-4 - (x^{2} - x - 6)] dx = \int_{-1}^{2}(2 - x^{2} + x) dx = (2x - \frac{1}{3}x^{3} + \frac{1}{2}x^{2})]_{-1}^{2} = (4 - \frac{8}{3} + 2) - (-2 + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{2}) = \frac{9}{2}$ .
