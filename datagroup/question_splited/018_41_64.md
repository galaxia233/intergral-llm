41.64 Find the domain of definition of the function $f(x, y) = \frac{x + 2y}{\sqrt{4 - x^2 - y^2}}$ .

The function is defined when the denominator is defined and $\neq 0$ . The latter holds when and only when $4 - x^{2} - y^{2} > 0$ , that is, when $x^{2} + y^{2} < 4$ . So, the domain is the inside of the circle of radius 2 with center at the origin.
