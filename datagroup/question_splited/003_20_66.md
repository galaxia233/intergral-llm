20.66 State the trapezoidal rule for approximation of integrals.

Let $f(x) \geq 0$ be integrable on $[a, b]$ . Divide $[a, b]$ into $n$ equal parts of length $\Delta x = (b - a) / n$ , by means of points $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{n-1}$ . Then

$$
\int_ {a} ^ {b} f (x) d x \approx \frac {\Delta x}{2} \left[ f (a) + f (b) + 2 \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n - 1} f \left(x _ {i}\right) \right]
$$
