34.25 Find the coordinates of the highest point of the curve $x = 96t, y = 96t - 16t^2$ .

We must maximize $y$ . $dy / dt = 96 - 32t$ , $d^2y / dt^2 = -32$ . So, the only critical number is $t = 3$ , and, by the second-derivative test, we have a relative (and, therefore, an absolute) maximum. When $t = 3$ , $x = 288$ , $y = 144$ .
