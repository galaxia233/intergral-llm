19.76 A ball is rolled in a straight line over a level lawn, with an initial velocity of $10 \, \text{ft/s}$ . If, because of friction, the velocity decreases at the rate of $4 \, \text{ft/s}^2$ , how far will the ball roll?

The deceleration $a = -4$ . $v = \int a dt = -4t + v_0$ . When $t = 0$ , $v = 10$ ; hence, $v_0 = 10$ , $v = -4t + 10$ . Then the position $s = \int v dt = -2t^2 + 10t$ [We assume $s = 0$ when $t = 0$ .] The ball stops when $v = 0$ , that is, when $t = 2.5$ . Hence, the distance rolled is $-2(2.5)^2 + 10(2.5) = 12.5$ ft.
