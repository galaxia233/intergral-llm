39.28 Estimate In 1.1 to within three-decimal-place accuracy.

$\ln (1 + x) = x - x^2 / 2 + x^3 / 3 - x^4 / 4 + \dots$ for $|x| < 1$ . Thus, $\ln 1.1 = (0.1) - \frac{1}{2} (0.1)^2 + \frac{1}{3} (0.1)^3 - \frac{1}{4} (0.1)^4 + \dots$ . This is an alternating series. We must find $n$ so that $(0.1)^n / n = 1 / n10^n < 0.0005$ , or $2000 < n10^n$ . Hence, $n \geq 3$ . Therefore, we may use the first two terms: $0.1 - (0.1)^2 / 2 = 0.1 - 0.005 = 0.095$ .
