28.4 $\int \sin^{-1}x dx.$

Let $u = \sin^{-1}x, \quad dv = dx, \quad du = (1 / \sqrt{1 - x^2})dx, \quad v = x.$ Then $\int \sin^{-1}x dx = x\sin^{-1}x - \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} dx = x\sin^{-1}x + \frac{1}{2}\int (1 - x^2)^{-1/2}(-2x)dx = x\sin^{-1}x + \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2(1 - x^2)^{1/2} + C = x\sin^{-1}x + \sqrt{1 - x^2} + C.$
