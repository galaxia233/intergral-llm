45.17 Find a formula for the binormal vector $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{T}\times \mathbf{N}$ in the case of the helix $\mathbf{R}(t) = (3\cos 2t,3\sin 2t,8t)$ .

$\mathbf{R}^{\prime}(t) = (-6\sin 2t,6\cos 2t,8).$ $\left|\mathbf{R}(t)\right| = \sqrt{36 + 64} = 10.$ Hence, $\mathbf{T} = \frac{1}{5} (-3\sin 2t,3\cos 2t,4).$ Therefore, $\mathbf{T}' = \frac{1}{5} (-6\cos 2t, - 6\sin 2t,0) = -\frac{6}{5} (\cos 2t,\sin 2t,0),$ and $|\mathbf{T}'| = \frac{6}{5}.$ Thus, $\mathbf{N} = \frac{\mathbf{T}'}{|\mathbf{T}'|} = -(\cos 2t,\sin 2t,0)$ and $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{T}\times \mathbf{N} = \frac{1}{5} (-3\sin 2t,3\cos 2t,4)\times -(\cos 2t,\sin 2t,0) = -\frac{1}{5} (-4\sin 2t, - 4\cos 2t, - 3) = \frac{1}{5} (4\sin 2t,4\cos 2t,3).$
