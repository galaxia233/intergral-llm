42.100 Assume $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = G(x)$ for all $(x, y)$ . Then prove there are functions $g(x)$ and $h(y)$ such that $f(x, y) = g(x) + h(y)$ .

Let $F(x, y) = f(x, y) - \int_{0}^{x} G(t) \, dt$ . Then $\frac{\partial F}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} - G(x) = 0$ . By Problem 42.98, $F(x, y) = h(y)$ for some $h$ . Thus, $f(x, y) = \int_{0}^{x} G(t) \, dt + h(y)$ . Let $g(x) = \int_{0}^{x} G(t) \, dt$ .
