27.70 If $\cos^{-1}xy = e^{2y}$ , find $y'$ .

Use implicit differentiation. $\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1 - (xy)^2}}\cdot (xy' + y) = e^{2y}\cdot 2y', xy' + y = -2e^{2y}y'\sqrt{1 - x^2y^2},$

$$
y ^ {\prime} \left(x + 2 e ^ {2 y} \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2} y ^ {2}}\right) = - y, \quad y ^ {\prime} = \frac {- y}{x + 2 e ^ {2 y} \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2} y ^ {2}}}.
$$
