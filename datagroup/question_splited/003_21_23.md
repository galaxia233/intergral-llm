21.23 Find the area bounded by the curves $y = 2x^{2} - 2$ and $y = \therefore^2 + x$ .

See Fig. 21-18. $y = 2x^{2} - 2$ is a parabola with vertex at $(0, -2)$ . On the other hand, $y = x^{2} + x = (x + \frac{1}{2})^{2} - \frac{1}{4}$ is a parabola with vertex $(-\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{4})$ . To find the points of intersection, set $2x^{2} - 2 = x^{2} + x$ , $x^{2} - x - 2 = 0$ , $(x - 2)(x + 1) = 0$ , $x = 2$ or $x = -1$ . Thus, the points are $(2,6)$ and $(-1,0)$ . Hence, the area is $\int_{-1}^{2} [(x^{2} + x) - (2x^{2} - 2)] dx = \int_{-1}^{2} (2 + x - x^{2}) dx = (2x + \frac{1}{2} x^{2} - \frac{1}{3} x^{3})]_{-1}^{2} = (4 + 2 - \frac{8}{3}) - (-2 + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3}) = -\frac{9}{2}$ .
