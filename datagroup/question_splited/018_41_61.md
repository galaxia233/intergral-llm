41.61 Is it possible to define $f(x, y) = \frac{xy}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}$ at the origin so that $f(x, y)$ is continuous?

In general, $\left(\frac{xy}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}\right)^2 = \frac{x^2y^2}{x^2 + y^2} \leq y^2 \to 0$ as $(x,y) \to (0,0)$ . Hence, $|xy / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}| \to 0$ . So, if we define $f(0,0) = 0$ , $f(x,y)$ will be continuous at the origin, and, therefore, everywhere.
