44.60 Use a triple integral to find the volume $V$ inside $x^{2} + y^{2} = 4x$ , above $z = 0$ , and below $x^{2} + y^{2} = 4z$ .

$x^{2} + y^{2} = 4x$ is equivalent to $(x - 2)^{2} + y^{2} = 4$ , the cylinder of radius 2 with axis $x = 2$ , $y = 0$ . Hence, $V = \int_{0}^{4}\int_{-\sqrt{4x - x^2}}^{\sqrt{4x - x^2}}\int_{0}^{(x^2 +y^2) / 4}1dzdydx$ . This is difficult to compute; so let us switch to cylindrical coordinates. The circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = 4x$ becomes $r = 4\cos \theta$ , and we get $V = \int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{4\cos \theta}\theta \int_{0}^{r^{2 / 4}}r dz dr d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{4\cos \theta}r z\left]_{0}^{r^{2 / 4}}drd\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{4\cos \theta}\frac{1}{4} r^{3}drd\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi}\frac{1}{16} r^{4}\right]_{0}^{4\cos \theta}d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi}16\cos^{4}\theta d\theta = 32\int_{0}^{\pi /2}\cos^{4}\theta d\theta = 6\pi$ (Problem 44.29).
