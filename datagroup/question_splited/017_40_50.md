40.50 If $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{C}$ and $\mathbf{A} \neq \mathbf{0}$ , does it follow that $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{C}$ ?

No. $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{C}$ is equivalent to $\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{C}) = \mathbf{0}$ . The last equation holds when $\mathbf{A}$ is parallel to $\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{C}$ , and this can happen when $\mathbf{B} \neq \mathbf{C}$ .
