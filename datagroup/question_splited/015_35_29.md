35.29 Show that the point with polar coordinates $(3, 3\pi/2)$ lies on the curve with the polar equation $r^2 = 9\sin \theta$ .

Notice that $r = 3$ , $\theta = 3\pi /2$ do not satisfy the equation $r^2 = 9\sin \theta$ . However, the point with polar coordinates $(3,3\pi /2)$ also has polar coordinates $(-3,\pi /2)$ , and $r = -3$ , $\theta = \pi /2$ satisfy the equation $r^2 = 9\sin \theta$ , since $(-3)^2 = 9\cdot 1$ .
