20.57 If the average value of $f(x) = x^3 + bx - 2$ on $[0, 2]$ is 4, find $b$ .

$$
4 = \frac {1}{2 - 0} \int_ {0} ^ {2} (x ^ {3} + b x - 2) d x = \frac {1}{2} (\frac {1}{4} x ^ {4} + \frac {1}{2} b x ^ {2} - 2 x) ] _ {0} ^ {2} = \frac {1}{2} [ (4 + 2 b - 4) - 0 ] = b. \quad \text {T h u s}, \quad b = 4.
$$
