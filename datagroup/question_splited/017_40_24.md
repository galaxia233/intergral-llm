40.24 Find the locus of all points $(x,y,z)$ that are twice as far from $(3,2,0)$ as from $(3,2,6)$ .

$\sqrt{(x - 3) + (y - 2)^2 + z^2} = 2\sqrt{(x - 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 6)^2}, \quad (x - 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + z^2 = 4[(x - 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 6)^2], \quad 3(x - 3)^2 + 3(y - 2)^2 + 4(z - 6)^2 - z^2 = 0, \quad 3x^2 - 18x + 27 + 3y^2 - 12y + 12 + 3z^2 - 48z + 144 = 0, \quad x^2 - 6x + y^2 - 4y + z^2 - 16z + 61 = 0, \quad (x - 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 8)^2 + 61 = 9 + 4 + 64, \quad (x - 3)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 8)^2 = 16.$ Thus, the locus is the sphere with center $(3,2,8)$ and radius $4$
