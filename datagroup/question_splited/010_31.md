31.16 A spring with a natural length of 10 inches is stretched $\frac{1}{2}$ inch by a 12-pound force. Find the work done in stretching the spring from 10 to 18 inches.   
We use Hooke's law: The spring pulls back with a restoring force of $F = kx$ pounds, where the spring is stretched $x$ inches beyond its natural length, and $k$ is a constant. Then, $12 = \frac{1}{2}k$ , $k = 24$ . $F = 24x$ , and the work $W = \int_{0}^{8} F \, dx = \int_{0}^{8} 24x \, dx = 12x^2 \bigg]_{0}^{8} = 12(64) = 768 \, \text{in} \cdot \text{lb} = 64 \, \text{ft} \cdot \text{lb}$ .
