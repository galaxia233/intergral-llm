21.16 The region in the first quadrant bounded by the curves $y = x^2$ and $y = x^4$ .

See Fig. 21-16. Let us find the points of intersection: $x^4 = x^2$ , $x^4 - x^2 = 0$ , $x^2(x^2 - 1) = 0$ , $x = 0$ or $x = \pm 1$ . So, the intersection points in the first quadrant are $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 1)$ . $y = x^2$ is the upper curve. Hence, the area is $\int_0^1 (x^2 - x^4) dx = \left( \frac{1}{3} x^3 - \frac{1}{5} x^5 \right) \bigg]_0^1 = \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{2}{15}$ .
