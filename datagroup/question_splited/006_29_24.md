29.24 $\int \frac{dx}{(4 - x^2)^{3 / 2}}.$

Since $\sqrt{4 - x^2}$ is present, let $x = 2\sin \theta$ , $dx = 2\cos \theta d\theta$ , $\sqrt{4 - x^2} = 2\cos \theta$ . Then $\int \frac{dx}{(4 - x^2)^{3/2}} = \frac{2\cos \theta d\theta}{8\cos^3 \theta} = \frac {1}{4}\int \sec^2 \theta d\theta = \frac {1}{4}\tan \theta + C = \frac {1}{4}\frac {x}{\sqrt {4 - x^2}} + C.$
