46.27 Explain the method of solving $\frac{dy}{dx} + p(x)y = q(x)$ by means of an integrating factor.

Define the integrating factor $\rho = \exp [\int p(x)dx]$ . Hence, $\frac{d\rho}{dx} = \exp [\int p(x)dx]\cdot p(x) = \rho \cdot p(x)$ . Thus, $\frac{d}{dx} (\rho y) = \rho \frac{dy}{dx} +\frac{d\rho}{dx} y = \rho \frac{dy}{dx} +\rho \cdot p(x)\cdot y$ . If we multiply the original differential equation by $\rho$ , $\rho \frac{dy}{dx} +\rho \cdot p(x)\cdot y = \rho \cdot q(x)$ . Hence, $\frac{d}{dx} (\rho y) = \rho q(x)$ . So $\rho y = \int \rho q(x)dx + C$ , and $y = \frac{1}{\rho} [\int \rho q(x)dx + C]$ .
