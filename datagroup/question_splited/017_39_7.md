39.7 Compute the first three nonzero terms of the Maclaurin series for $e^{\cos x}$ .

Let $f(x) = e^{\cos x}$ . Then, $f'(x) = -e^{\cos x} \sin x$ , $f''(x) = e^{\cos x} (\sin^2 x - \cos x)$ , $f'''(x) = e^{\cos x} (\sin x) (3 \cos x + 1 - \sin^2 x)$ , $f^{(4)}(x) = e^{\cos x} [(-\sin^2 x)(3 + 2 \cos x) + (3 \cos x + 1 - \sin^2 x) (\cos x - \sin^2 x)]$ . Thus, $f(0) = e$ , $f'(0) = 0$ , $f''(0) = -e$ , $f'''(0) = 0$ , $f^{(4)}(0) = 4e$ . Hence, the Maclaurin series is $e(1 - \frac{1}{2} x^2 + \frac{1}{6} x^4 + \dots)$ .
