41.14 Describe and sketch the graph of $\frac{x^2}{a^2} +\frac{y^2}{b^2} -\frac{z^2}{c^2} = 1,$ where $a,b,c > 0$

See Fig. 41-9. Each section $z = k$ parallel to the $xy$ -plane cuts out an ellipse. The ellipses get bigger as $|z|$ increases. (For $z = 0$ , the $xy$ -plane, the section is the ellipse $\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$ .) For sections made by planes $x = k$ , we obtain hyperbolas, and, similarly for sections made by planes $y = k$ . The surface is called a hyperboloid of one sheet.
