43.22 If $z = f(x, y)$ has a relative maximum or minimum at a point $(x_0, y_0)$ , show that $\nabla z = 0$ at $(x_0, y_0)$ .

We wish to show that both partial derivatives of $z$ vanish at $(x_0, y_0)$ . The plane $y = y_0$ intersects the surface $z = f(x, y)$ in a curve $z = f(x, y_0)$ that has a relative maximum (or minimum) at $x = x_0$ . Hence, $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 0$ at $(x_0, y_0)$ . Similarly, the plane $x = x_0$ intersects the surface $z = f(x, y)$ in a curve $z = f(x_0, y)$ that has a relative maximum (or minimum) at $y = y_0$ . Hence, the derivative $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 0$ at $(x_0, y_0)$ . Similar results hold for functions $f$ of more than two variables.
