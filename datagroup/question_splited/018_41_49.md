41.49 Prove that $\lim_{(x,y)\to (1,2)}2x - 3y = -4.$

Let $\varepsilon > 0$ . We must find $\delta > 0$ such that $|(x, y) - (1, 2)| < \delta$ implies $|(2x - 3y) - (-4)| < \varepsilon$ ; that is, $\sqrt{(x - 1)^2 + (y - 2)^2} < \delta$ implies $|2x - 3y + 4| < \varepsilon$ . Let $\delta = \varepsilon / 5$ . Assume $\sqrt{(x - 1)^2 + (y - 2)^2} < \delta$ . Then $|x - 1| < \delta$ and $|y - 2| < \delta$ . So, $|2x - 3y + 4| = |2(x - 1) - 3(y - 2)| \leq 2|x - 1| + 3|y - 2| < 2\delta + 3\delta = 5\delta < \varepsilon$ .
