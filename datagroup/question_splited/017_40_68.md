40.68 If $\mathbf{A} = (2, -3, 1)$ is normal to one plane $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathbf{B} = (-1, 4, -2)$ is normal to another plane $\mathcal{P}_2$ , show that $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathcal{P}_2$ intersect and find a vector parallel to the line $\mathcal{L}$ in which they intersect.

If $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathcal{P}_2$ were parallel, $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ would have to be parallel. But $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ are not parallel (since $\mathbf{A}$ is not a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{B}$ ). Therefore, $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathcal{P}_2$ intersect. Since $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to plane $\mathcal{P}_1$ , $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to the line $\mathcal{L}$ in $\mathcal{P}_1$ . Likewise, $\mathbf{B}$ is perpendicular to $\mathcal{L}$ . Hence, $\mathcal{L}$ is parallel to $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ .
