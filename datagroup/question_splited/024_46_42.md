46.42 Solve the Bernoulli equation $y' - (1/x + 2x^4)y = x^3y^2$ .

Use the method of Problem 46.40. Let $v = 1 / y$ , $v' = -y'/y^2$ . Then, $v' + (1/x + 2x^4)v = -x^3$ . Let $\rho = \exp \left[ \int \left( \frac{1}{x} + 2x \right) dx \right] = e^{ln x + 2x^{5/5}} = xe^{2x^{5/5}}$ . Then, $v = -\frac{1}{x}e^{-2x^{5/5}}(\int x^4e^{2x^{5/5}}dx) = -\frac{e^{-2x^{5/5}}}{x} (\frac{1}{2}e^{2x^{5/5}} + C) = -\left( \frac{1 + Ke^{-2x^{5/5}}}{2x} \right)$ . Therefore, $y = -\frac{2x}{1 + Ke^{-2x^{5/5}}}$ .
