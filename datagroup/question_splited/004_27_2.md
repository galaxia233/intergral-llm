27.2 Show that $D_{x}(\sin^{-1}x) = 1 / \sqrt{1 - x^{2}}.$

Let $y = \sin^{-1} x$ . Then $\sin y = x$ . By implicit differentiation, $\cos y \cdot D_x y = 1$ , $D_x y = 1 / \cos y$ . But $\cos y = \pm \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 y} = \pm \sqrt{1 - x^2}$ . Since, by definition, $-\pi / 2 \leq y \leq \pi / 2$ , $\cos y \geq 0$ , and, therefore, $\cos y = \sqrt{1 - x^2}$ and $D_x y = 1 / \sqrt{1 - x^2}$ .
