44.61 Use a triple integral to find the volume inside the cylinder $r = 4$ , above $z = 0$ , and below $2z = y$ .

The solid is wedge-shaped. The base is the half-disk $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi, 0 \leq r \leq 4$ . The height is $\frac{y}{2} = \frac{r \sin \theta}{2}$ . Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} V = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} \int_ {0} ^ {4} \int_ {0} ^ {(r \sin \theta) / 2} r d z d r d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} \int_ {0} ^ {4} r z \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {(r \sin \theta) / 2} d r d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} \int_ {0} ^ {4} \frac {1}{2} r ^ {2} \sin d r d \theta = \frac {1}{6} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} r ^ {3} \sin \theta ] _ {0} ^ {4} d \theta \\ = \frac {1}{6} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} 6 4 \sin \theta d \theta = \frac {3 2}{3} (- \cos \theta) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {\pi} = - \frac {3 2}{3} (- 1 - 1) = \frac {6 4}{3} \\ \end{array}
$$
