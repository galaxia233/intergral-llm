42.76 Verify Euler's theorem (Problem 42.74) for the function $f(x, y) = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ .

$f(x,y)$ is homogeneous of degree 1, since $f(tx,ty) = \sqrt{(tx)^2 + (ty)^2} = t\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = tf(x,y)$ for $t > 0$ . We must check that $x f_x + y f_y = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ . But, $f_x = x / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ and $f_y = y / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ . Thus, $x f_x + y f_y = (x^2 / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}) + (y^2 / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}) = (x^2 + y^2) / \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ .
