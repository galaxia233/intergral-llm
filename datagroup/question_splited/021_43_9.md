43.9 For the surface of Problem 43.8, find the direction of the level curve through $(1,1)$ and show that it is perpendicular to the direction of steepest grade.

The level curve $z = 2$ is $8 - 4x^{2} - 2y^{2} = 2$ , or $2x^{2} + y^{2} = 3$ . By implicit differentiation, $4x + 2y \frac{dy}{dx} = 0$ , $dy / dx = -2x / y = -2$ . Hence, a tangent vector is $(1, -2, 0)$ . A vector in the direction of steepest grade is $(-8, -4, 80)$ , by Problem 43.8. Because $(-8, -4, 80) \cdot (1, -2, 0) = 0$ , the two directions are perpendicular.
