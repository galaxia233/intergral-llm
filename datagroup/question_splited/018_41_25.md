41.25 Identify the graph of $x^{2} + 4z^{2} = 2y$

This is equivalent to $(x^2 / 4) + z^2 = y / 2$ . This is an elliptic paraboloid, with the $y$ -axis as its axis. For $y = k > 0$ , the cross section is an ellipse $(x^2 / 4) + z^2 = k / 2$ . ( $y = 0$ is the origin, and the cross sections $y = k < 0$ are empty.) The cross section $x = k$ is a parabola $y / 2 = z^2 + (k^2 / 4)$ . The cross section $z = k$ is a parabola $y / 2 = (x^2 / 4) + k^2$ .
