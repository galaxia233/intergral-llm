27.76 See Fig. 27-9. Two ships sail from $A$ at the same time, for 1 hour until it reaches point $B$ , and then sails north. hours.

One sails south at $15\mathrm{mi / h}$ the other sails east at $25\mathrm{mi / h}$ Find the rate of rotation of the line joining them, after 3

Let $\theta$ be the angle between the line joining the ships and the line parallel to $AB$ . The distance $AB$ is 25 miles. Then $\theta = \tan^{-1}\frac{15t + 25(t - 1)}{25} = \tan^{-1}\frac{40t - 25}{25}$ . Hence, $D_{t}\theta = \frac{1}{1 + [(40t - 25) / 25]^{2}}\left(\frac{40}{25}\right)$ . When $t = 3$ , $D_{t}\theta = \frac{40}{25 + (19)^{2}} = \frac{20}{193}$ radian per hour.
