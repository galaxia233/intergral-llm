46.61 Solve $y^{\prime \prime} + y^{\prime} - 12y = xe^{x}$

A general solution of the corresponding homogeneous equation is $C_1 e^{3x} + C_2 e^{-4x}$ , since 3 and -4 are the roots of the auxiliary equation $u^2 + u - 12 = 0$ . Let us try $y = (A + Bx)e^x$ as a particular solution of the given equation. Then $y' = (A + B + Bx)e^x$ , and $y'' = (A + 2B + Bx)e^x$ . Thus, $(A + 2B + Bx)e^x + (A + B + Bx)e^x - 12(A + Bx)e^x = xe^x$ . Hence, $(A + 2B + Bx) + (A + B + Bx) - 12(A + Bx) = x$ . Equating the coefficients of $x$ as well as the constant coefficients, we get $-10B = 1$ and $-10A + 3B = 0$ . Therefore, $B = -\frac{1}{10}$ and $A = -\frac{3}{100}$ . Thus, $y = -\frac{1}{100}(3 + 10x)e^x$ , and the general solution is $-\frac{1}{100}(3 + 10x)e^x + C_1 e^{3x} + C_2 e^{-4x}$ .
