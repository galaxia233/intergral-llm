41.45 Describe the level surfaces of $f(x, y, z) = \frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{9} + z^2$ .

The level surfaces form a family of ellipsoids $\frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{9} + z^2 = k > 0$ , or $\frac{x^2}{(5\sqrt{k})^2} + \frac{y^2}{(3\sqrt{k})^2} + \frac{z^2}{(\sqrt{k})^2} = 1$ , in which the axes along the $x$ -axis, $y$ -axis, and $z$ -axis are in the proportion $5:3:1$ . There is a level surface through every point (except the origin if one does not count a point as a level surface).
