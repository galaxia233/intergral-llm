33.6 Show that the vector $\mathbf{A} = (a, b)$ is perpendicular to the line $ax + by + c = 0$ .

Let $P_{1}(x_{1}, y_{1})$ and $P_{2}(x_{2}, y_{2})$ be two points on the line. Then $ax_{1} + by_{1} + c = 0$ and $ax_{2} + by_{2} + c = 0$ . By subtraction, $a(x_{1} - x_{2}) + b(y_{1} - y_{2}) = 0$ , or $(a, b) \cdot (x_{1} - x_{2}, y_{1} - y_{2}) = 0$ . Thus, $(a, b) \cdot P_{2}P_{1} = 0$ , $(a, b) \bot P_{2}P_{1}$ . (Recall that two nonzero vectors are perpendicular to each other if and only if their dot product is 0.) Hence, $(a, b)$ is perpendicular to the line.
