【2184】从积分的定义出发，求 $\int_0^T (v_0 + gt)\mathrm{d}t$ ，其中 $T, v_0, g$ 为常数.

解 $f(t) = v_{0} + gt$ 在 $[0,T]$ 上为增函数 $(T > 0)$

$$
h = \frac {T}{n}, \quad m _ {i} = v _ {0} + i g h, \quad M _ {i} = v _ {0} + (i + 1) g h \quad (i = 0, 1, 2, \dots , n - 1)
$$

于是， $\underline{S_n} = \sum_{i = 0}^{n - 1}(v_0 + igh)h = n v_0 h + gh^2\sum_{i = 0}^{n - 1}i = v_0 T + \frac{gT^2}{n^2}\cdot \frac{n(n - 1)}{2} = v_0 T + \frac{gT^2}{2} -\frac{gT^2}{2n},$

$$
\overline {{S _ {n}}} = \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {n - 1} [ v _ {0} + (i + 1) g h ] h = v _ {0} T + \frac {g T ^ {2}}{2} + \frac {g T ^ {2}}{2 n}.
$$

因为 $\lim_{n\to \infty}\overline{S_n} = \lim_{n\to \infty}\overline{S_n} = v_0T + \frac{gT^2}{2},$

所以， $\int_0^T (v_0 + gt)\mathrm{d}t = v_0T + \frac{gT^2}{2}.$
