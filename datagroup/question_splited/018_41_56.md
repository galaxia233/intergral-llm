41.56 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x^2y^2}{x^3 + y^3}$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then

$$
\frac {x ^ {2} y ^ {2}}{x ^ {3} + y ^ {3}} = \frac {x ^ {2} \left(m ^ {2} x ^ {2}\right)}{x ^ {3} + m ^ {3} x ^ {3}} = \frac {x m ^ {2}}{1 + m ^ {3}} \rightarrow 0 \quad \text {a s} (x, y) \rightarrow (0, 0)
$$

Now let $y = -xe^{x}$ . Then

$$
\frac {x ^ {2} y ^ {2}}{x ^ {3} + y ^ {3}} = \frac {- x ^ {4} e ^ {2 x}}{x ^ {3} - x ^ {3} e ^ {3 x}} = \frac {x e ^ {2 x}}{e ^ {3 x} - 1}
$$

By L'Hôpital's rule,

$$
\lim  _ {x \rightarrow 0} \frac {x e ^ {2 x}}{e ^ {3 x} - 1} = \lim  _ {x \rightarrow 0} \frac {e ^ {2 x} (2 x + 1)}{3 e ^ {3 x}} = \frac {1}{3}
$$

Hence, the limit does not exist.
