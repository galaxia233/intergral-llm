39.49 Show that $1 = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty}\frac{n}{(n + 1)!}$

1 $e^{x} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n}}{n!}$ . Hence, $e^{x} - 1 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n}}{n!}$ , and, therefore, $\frac{e^{x} - 1}{x} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n-1}}{n!} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n}}{(n+1)!}$ . Now, let $g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{e^{x} - 1}{x} \right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n x^{n-1}}{(n+1)!}$ . Then $g(1) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n}{(n+1)!}$ . On the other hand, $g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{e^{x} - 1}{x} \right) = \frac{x e^{x} - (e^{x} - 1)}{x^{2}}$ , and, thus, $g(1) = \frac{e - (e - 1)}{1} = 1$ .
