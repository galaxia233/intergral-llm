42.77 Verify Euler's theorem (Problem 42.74) for the function $f(x, y, z) = 3xz^2 - 2xyz + y^2z$ .

$f$ is clearly homogeneous of degree 3. Now, $f_{x} = 3z^{2} - 2yz$ , $f_{y} = -2xz + 2zy$ , $f_{z} = 6xz - 2xy + y^{2}$ . Thus, $x f_{x} + y f_{y} + z f_{z} = x(3z^{2} - 2yz) + y(-2xz + 2zy) + z(6xz - 2xy + y^{2}) = 3xz^{2} - 2xyz - 2xyz + 2y^{2}z + 6xz^{2} - 2xyz + y^{2}z = 9xz^{2} - 6xyz + 3y^{2}z = 3f(x,y,z)$ .
