45.32 Show that, for any scalar function $f(x,y,z)$ , $\operatorname{div} \nabla f = f_{xx} + f_{yy} + f_{zz}$ . (The latter sum, called the Laplacian of $f$ , is often notated as $\nabla^2 f$ .)

$$
\nabla f = \left(f _ {x}, f _ {y}, f _ {z}\right). \quad \text {H e n c e}, \quad \operatorname {d i v} \nabla f = \frac {\partial}{\partial x} \left(f _ {x}\right) + \frac {\partial}{\partial y} \left(f _ {y}\right) + \frac {\partial}{\partial z} \left(f _ {z}\right) = f _ {x x} + f _ {y y} + f _ {z z}.
$$
