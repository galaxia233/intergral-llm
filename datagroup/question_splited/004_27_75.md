27.75 Refer to Fig. 27-8. In a circular arena of radius $r$ , there is a light at $L$ . A boy starting from $B$ runs toward the center $O$ at the rate of 10 ft/s. At what rate will his shadow be moving along the side when he is halfway from $B$ to $O$ ?

Let $P$ be the boy's position, $x$ the distance of $P$ from $B$ , $\theta$ the angle $OLP$ , and $s$ the arc intercepted by $\theta$ . We are given that $D_{r}x = 10$ . Then $s = r(2\theta)$ , $\theta = \tan^{-1}(r - x) / r$ . $D_{r}s = 2rD_{t}\theta = 2r \cdot \frac{1}{1 + [(r - x) / r]^{2}}$ . $\left(-\frac{1}{r}\right) \cdot D_{r}x = \frac{-2r^{2}}{r^{2} + (r - x)^{2}} \cdot 10 = \frac{-20r^{2}}{r^{2} + (r - x)^{2}}$ . When $x = \frac{r}{2}$ , $D_{r}s = \frac{-20r^{2}}{r^{2} + r^{2/4}} = -\frac{20}{\frac{s}{4}} = -16$ . Hence, the shadow is moving at the rate of $16\mathrm{ft/s}$ .
