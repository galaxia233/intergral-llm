43.4 Find the derivative of $f(x, y) = x - \sin xy$ at $(1, \pi/2)$ in the direction of $\mathbf{u} = \left(\cos \frac{\pi}{3}, \sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right) = \left(\frac{1}{2}, \sqrt{3}/2\right)$ .

$\nabla f = (1 - y \cos xy, -x \cos xy) = (1, 0)$ . Hence, the derivative is $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = \frac{1}{2}(1, 0) \cdot (1, \sqrt{3}) = \frac{1}{2}$ .
