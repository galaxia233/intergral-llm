45.23 Find the curvature of $\mathbf{R} = (e^{t}\sin t,e^{t}\cos t,e^{t})$ at $t = 0$

$\mathbf{R} = e^t (\sin t,\cos t,1).$ Hence, $\mathbf{R}' = e'(0,\cos t, - \sin t,0) + e'(0,\cos t,1) = e'(0,\cos t + \sin t,\cos t-$ sin $t,1)$ . $\pmb {R}'' = e''(2\cos t, - 2\sin t,1)$ . At $t = 0$ $\mathbf{R}' = (1,1,1)$ and $\mathbf{R}'' = (2,0,1)$ . Hence, $\mathbf{R}'\times \mathbf{R}'' = (1,1, - 2)$ $|\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\times \mathbf{R}^{\prime \prime}| = \sqrt{6},$ and $|\mathbf{R}^{\prime}| = \sqrt{3}$ . By Problem 45.22, $\kappa = \frac{|\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\times\mathbf{R}^{\prime\prime}|}{|\mathbf{R}^{\prime}|^{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{(\sqrt{3})^{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}$
