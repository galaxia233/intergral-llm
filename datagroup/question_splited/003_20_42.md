20.42 Prove that, if $f$ is continuous on $[a, b]$ , $D_x[\int_a^x f(t)dt] = f(x)$ .

Let $g(x) = \int_{a}^{x} f(t) \, dt$ . Then $g(x + \Delta x) - g(x) = \int_{a}^{x + \Delta x} f(t) \, dt - \int_{a}^{x} f(t) \, dt = \int_{x}^{x + \Delta x} f(t) \, dt$ . By the Mean-Value Theorem for integrals, the last integral is $\Delta x \cdot f(x^*)$ for some $x^*$ between $x$ and $x + \Delta x$ . Hence,

$$
\frac {g (x + \Delta x) - g (x)}{\Delta x} = f \left(x ^ {*}\right) \quad \text {a n d} \quad D _ {x} \left[ \int_ {a} ^ {x} f (t) d t \right] = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac {g (x + \Delta x) - g (x)}{\Delta x} = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} f \left(x ^ {*}\right)
$$

But as $\Delta x\to 0$ $x^{*}\rightarrow x$ , and, by the continuity of $f,\lim_{\Delta x\to 0}f(x^{*}) = f(x).$
