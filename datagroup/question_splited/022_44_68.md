44.67 Find the volume of the solid enclosed by the paraboloids $z = x^{2} + y^{2}$ (upward-opening) and $z = 36 - 3x^{2} - 8y^{2}$ (downward-opening).

The projection of the intersection of the surfaces is the ellipse $4x^{2} + 9y^{2} = 36$ . By symmetry, we can calculate the integral with respect to $x$ and $y$ in the first quadrant and then multiply it by 4.

$$
\begin{array}{l} V = 4 \int_ {0} ^ {3} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} / 3} \int_ {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} ^ {3 6 - 3 x ^ {2} - 8 y ^ {2}} d z d y d x = 4 \int_ {0} ^ {3} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} / 3} \left[ (3 6 - 3 x ^ {2} - 8 y ^ {2}) - (x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}) \right] d y d x \\ = 4 \int_ {0} ^ {3} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} / 3} (3 6 - 4 x ^ {2} - 9 y ^ {2}) d y d x = 4 \int_ {0} ^ {3} (3 6 y - 4 x ^ {2} y - 3 y ^ {3}) ] _ {0} ^ {2 \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} / 3} d x \\ = \frac {8}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {3} \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} [ 3 6 - 4 x ^ {2} - 4 (9 - x ^ {2}) ] d x = \frac {3 2}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {3} \sqrt {9 - x ^ {2}} \left[ \frac {2}{3} (9 - x ^ {2}) \right] d x = \frac {6 4}{9} \int_ {0} ^ {3} (9 - x ^ {2}) ^ {3 / 2} d x \\ \end{array}
$$

Let $x = 3 \sin \theta, \quad dx = 3 \cos \theta d\theta.$ Then $V = \frac{64}{9} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} 27 \cos^{3} \theta \cdot 3 \cos \theta d\theta = 576 \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos^{4} \theta d\theta = 108\pi$ (from Problem 44.29).
