Given a relationship $F(x, y, z) = 0$ , where $F$ has nonzero partial derivatives with respect to its arguments, prove the cyclical formula $(\partial x / \partial y)(\partial y / \partial z)(\partial z / \partial x) = -1$ .

1 Holding $z$ constant, differentiate the functional equation on $y$ : $F_{x}x_{y} + F_{y} = 0$ , or $x_{y} = -F_{y} / F_{x}$ . Similarly (or by cyclical permutation of the variables), $y_{z} = -F_{z} / F_{y}$ and $z_{x} = -F_{x} / F_{z}$ . Then, by multiplication, $x_{y}y_{z}z_{x} = -F_{y}F_{z}F_{x} / F_{x}F_{y}F_{z} = -1$ , which is the desired result.
