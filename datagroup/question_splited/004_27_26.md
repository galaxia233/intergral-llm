27.26 Explain the answers to Problems 27.22-27.25.

In each case, since the derivative is 0, the function has to be a constant. Consider, for example, $\sin^{-1}x + \cos^{-1}x$ . When $x > 0$ , $\theta_{1} = \sin^{-1}x$ and $\theta_{2} = \cos^{-1}x$ are acute angles whose sum is $\pi / 2$ [see Fig. 27-5(a)]. For $x < 0$ , $\sin^{-1}(-x) = -\theta_{1}$ and $\cos^{-1}(-x) = \pi - \theta_{2}$ , and, therefore, $\sin^{-1}(-x) + \cos^{-1}(-x) = -\theta_{1} + \pi - \theta_{2} = \pi - (\theta_{1} + \theta_{2}) = \pi - \pi / 2 = \pi / 2$ . [See Fig. 27.5(b).] Hence, in all cases, $\sin^{-1}x + \cos^{-1}x = \pi / 2$ .
