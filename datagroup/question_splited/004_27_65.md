27.65 A person is viewing a painting hung high on a wall. The vertical dimension of the painting is 2 feet and the bottom of the painting is 2 feet above the eye level of the viewer. Find the distance $x$ that the viewer should stand from the wall in order to maximize the angle $\theta$ subtended by the painting.

From Fig. 27-6, $\theta = \tan^{-1}4 / x - \tan^{-1}2 / x$ . So

$$
\begin{array}{l} D _ {x} \theta = \frac {1}{1 + (4 / x) ^ {2}} \cdot \frac {- 4}{x ^ {2}} - \frac {1}{1 + (2 / x) ^ {2}} \cdot \frac {- 2}{x ^ {2}} = \frac {- 4}{x ^ {2} + 1 6} + \frac {2}{x ^ {2} + 4} \\ = \frac {- 4 x ^ {2} - 1 6 + 2 x ^ {2} + 3 2}{(x ^ {2} + 1 6) (x ^ {2} + 4)} = \frac {2 (8 - x ^ {2})}{(x ^ {2} + 1 6) (x ^ {2} + 4)} \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus, the only positive critical number is $x = \sqrt{8} = 2\sqrt{2}$ , which, by the first-derivative test, yields a relative (and, therefore, an absolute) maximum.
