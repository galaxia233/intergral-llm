43.13 If the temperature is given by $f(x, y, z) = 3x^{2} - 5y^{2} + 2z^{2}$ and you are located at $\left(\frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{5}, \frac{1}{2}\right)$ and want to get cool as soon as possible, in which direction should you set out?

$\nabla f = (6x, -10y, 4z) = (2, -2, 2) = 2(1, -1, 1)$ . The direction in which $f$ decreases the most rapidly is that of $-\nabla f$ ; thus, you should move in the direction of the vector $(-1, 1, -1)$ .
