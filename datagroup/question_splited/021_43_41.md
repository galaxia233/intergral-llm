43.41 Show that a rectangular box (with top) of maximum volume $V$ having prescribed surface area $S$ is a cube.

Let $x, y, z$ be the length, width, and height, respectively. $S = 2xy + 2xz + 2yz$ . We must maximize $V = xyz$ . Think of $z$ as a function of $x$ and $y$ ; then so is $V$ . Now,

$$
V _ {x} = y \left(x \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} + z\right) = y z + y x \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} \quad \text {a n d} \quad V _ {y} = x \left(y \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} + z\right) = x z + x y \frac {\partial z}{\partial y}
$$

Differentiate the surface area equation with respect to $x$ and to $y$ :

$$
0 = 2 y + 2 \left(x \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} + z\right) + 2 y \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} \tag {1}
$$

$$
0 = 2 x + 2 \left(y \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} + z\right) + 2 x \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} \tag {2}
$$

From (1), $\partial z / \partial x = -(y + z) / (x + y)$ . From (2), $\partial z / \partial y = -(x + z) / (x + y)$ . Then

$$
V _ {x} = y z - y x \left(\frac {y + z}{x + y}\right) \quad \text {a n d} \quad V _ {y} = x z - x y \left(\frac {x + z}{x + y}\right)
$$

Set $V_{x} = V_{y} = 0$ . Then $z = x[(y + z) / (x + y)]$ and $z = y[(x + z) / (x + y)]$ , $zx + zy = xy + xz$ and $xz + yz = yx + yz$ , $zy = xy$ and $xz = yx$ , $z = x$ and $z = y$ . Thus, $x = y = z$ and the box is a cube.
