44.35 Use polar coordinates to evaluate $I = \int_0^1\int_0^{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}(x^2 +y^2)^{5 / 2}dydx.$

The region of integration is the part of the unit disk in the first quadrant: $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi / 2$ , $0 \leq r \leq 1$ . Hence, $I = \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \int_{0}^{1} r^{5} \cdot r dr d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \int_{0}^{1} r^{6} dr d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \frac{1}{7} r^{7}]_{0}^{1} d\theta = \frac{1}{7} \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} d\theta = \frac{1}{7} (\pi / 2) = \pi / 14$ .
