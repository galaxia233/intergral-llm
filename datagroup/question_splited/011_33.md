33.29 Evaluate $\int_{1}^{3}\frac{dx}{x - 2}.$

There is a discontinuity at $x = 2$ . So, $\int_{1}^{3} \frac{dx}{x - 2} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} \int_{1}^{v} \frac{dx}{x - 2} + \lim_{w \to 2^{+}} \int_{w}^{3} \frac{dx}{x - 2} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} \ln |x - 2|_{1}^{v} + \lim_{w \to 2^{+}} \ln |x - 2|_{w}^{3} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} \ln |v - 2| + \lim_{w \to 2^{+}} -\ln |w - 2|$ . Neither limit exists. Therefore, the integral diverges.
