【2137】 $\int \frac{1 + \sqrt{1 - x^2}}{1 - \sqrt{1 - x^2}}\mathrm{d}x.$

解 $\int \frac{1 + \sqrt{1 - x^2}}{1 - \sqrt{1 - x^2}}\mathrm{d}x = \int \frac{(1 + \sqrt{1 - x^2})(1 + \sqrt{1 - x^2})}{(1 - \sqrt{1 - x^2})(1 + \sqrt{1 - x^2})}\mathrm{d}x$

$$
\begin{array}{l} = \int \frac {2 - x ^ {2} + 2 \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}}}{x ^ {2}} d x = - \frac {2}{x} - x - 2 \int \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}} d \left(\frac {1}{x}\right) \\ = - \frac {2}{x} - x - \frac {2}{x} \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}} - 2 \int \frac {\mathrm {d} x}{\sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}}} = - \frac {2 + x ^ {2}}{x} - \frac {2}{x} \sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}} - 2 \arcsin x + C. \\ \end{array}
