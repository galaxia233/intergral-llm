45.28 For any vector field $\mathbf{F}(x,y,z) = (f(x,y,z),g(x,y,z),h(x,y,z))$ , define curl $\mathbf{F}$ .

$\mathbf{I}$ $\operatorname {curl}\mathbf{F} = \left(\frac{\partial h}{\partial y} -\frac{\partial g}{\partial z},\quad \frac{\partial f}{\partial z} -\frac{\partial h}{\partial x},\quad \frac{\partial g}{\partial x} -\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right),$ or, more vividly,

$$
\operatorname {c u r l} \mathbf {F} = \boldsymbol {\nabla} \times \mathbf {F} = \left| \begin{array}{l l l} \mathbf {i} & \mathbf {j} & \mathbf {k} \\ \frac {\partial}{\partial x} & \frac {\partial}{\partial y} & \frac {\partial}{\partial z} \\ f & g & h \end{array} \right|
$$

where $\nabla = \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial x},\frac{\partial}{\partial y},\frac{\partial}{\partial z}\right).$
