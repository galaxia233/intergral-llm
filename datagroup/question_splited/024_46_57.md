46.57 Apply the theorem of Problem 46.56 to the first-order linear equation $y' + p(x)y = q(x)$ , and thereby retrieve the integrating-factor method (Problem 46.27).

First solve the homogeneous equation, which is separable: $y_{g}^{\prime} + p(x)y_{g} = 0$ , $y_{g}^{\prime} / y_{g} = -p(x)$ , $\ln y_{g} = -\int p(x)dx + C_{1}$ ,

$$
y _ {g} = C \exp \left[ - \int p (x) d x \right] \equiv \frac {C}{\rho}
$$

Now look for a particular solution of the nonhomogeneous equation of the form $y = z / \rho$ ; exploit the fact that $\rho' = \rho p$ .

$$
\frac {1}{\rho} z ^ {\prime} - \frac {\rho^ {\prime}}{\rho^ {2}} z + \frac {p}{\rho} z = q \quad z ^ {\prime} = \rho q \quad z = \int \rho q d x
$$

Thus our general solution is $y_{p} + y_{g} = \rho^{-1}(\int \rho q dx + C)$ , as in Problem 46.27.
