34.34 Find the arc length of the curve $x = \frac{1}{2}\ln (1 + t^2),y = \tan^{-1}t,$ from $t = 0$ to $t = 1$

$dx/dt = t/(1 + t^2), (dx/dt)^2 = t^2/(1 + t^2)^2, dy/dt = 1/(1 + t^2), (dy/dt)^2 = 1/(1 + t^2)^2.$ Hence,

$$
s = \int_ {0} ^ {1} \sqrt {\frac {t ^ {2}}{(1 + t ^ {2}) ^ {2}} + \frac {1}{(1 + t ^ {2}) ^ {2}}} d t = \int_ {0} ^ {1} \frac {1}{\sqrt {1 + t ^ {2}}} d t
$$

The substitution $t = \tan \theta, dt = \sec^2 \theta d\theta$ yields $\int_0^{\pi/4} \sec \theta d\theta = \ln |\sec \theta + \tan \theta|_0^{\pi/4} = \ln |\sqrt{2} + 1| - \ln 1 = \ln (\sqrt{2} + 1)$ .
