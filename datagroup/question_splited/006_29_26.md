29.26 $\int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{16 - 9x^2}}.$

Since $\sqrt{16 - 9x^2}$ is present, let $x = \frac {4}{3}\sin \theta$ , $dx = \frac {4}{3}\cos \theta d\theta$ , and $\sqrt{16 - 9x^2} = \sqrt{16 - 16\sin^2\theta} = 4\cos \theta$ . Then $\int \frac{dx}{x\sqrt{16 - 9x^2}} = \int \frac{\frac {4}{3}\cos\theta d\theta}{\frac {4}{3}\sin\theta \cdot 4\cos\theta} = \frac {1}{4}\int \frac {d \theta}{\sin\theta} = \frac {1}{4}\int \csc \theta d\theta = \frac {1}{4}\ln |\csc \theta -\cot \theta | + C = \frac {1}{4}\ln \left|\frac {4 - \sqrt{16 - 9x^2}}{3x}\right| + C.$
