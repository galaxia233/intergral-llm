33.10 Generalize the method of Problem 33.9 to find a formula for the distance from a point $P(x_{1}, y_{1})$ to the line $ax + by + c = 0$ .

Take the point $A(-c / a, 0)$ on the line. The vector $\mathbf{B} = (a, b)$ is perpendicular to the line. As in Problem 33.9,

$$
d = \frac {\left| \overrightarrow {A P} \cdot \mathbf {B} \right|}{\left| \mathbf {B} \right|} = \frac {\left| \left(x _ {1} + c / a , y _ {1} - 0\right) \cdot (a , b) \right|}{\sqrt {a ^ {2} + b ^ {2}}} = \frac {\left| a x _ {1} + b y _ {1} + c \right|}{\sqrt {a ^ {2} + b ^ {2}}}
$$

This derivation assumes $a \neq 0$ . If $a = 0$ , a similar derivation can be given, taking $A$ to be $(0, -c / b)$ .
