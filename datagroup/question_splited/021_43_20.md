43.20 Prove $\nabla (f^n) = nf^{n - 1}\nabla f$

The proof is by induction on $n$ . The result is clearly true when $n = 1$ . Assume the result true for $n$ . Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla \left(f ^ {n + 1}\right) = \nabla \left(f ^ {n} \cdot f\right) = f ^ {n} \nabla f + \nabla \left(f ^ {n}\right) f \quad (\text {b y}) \\ = f ^ {n} \boldsymbol {\nabla} f + n f ^ {n - 1} \boldsymbol {\nabla} f (f) \quad (\text {b y}) \\ = f ^ {\prime \prime} \nabla f + n f ^ {\prime \prime} \nabla f = (n + 1) f ^ {\prime \prime} \nabla f \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus, the identity also holds for $n + 1$ .
