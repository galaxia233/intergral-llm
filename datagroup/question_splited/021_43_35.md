43.35 Find the maximum value of $xy^{2}z^{3}$ on the part of the plane $x + y + z = 12$ in the first octant.

We must maximize $f(y, z) = (12 - y - z)y^{2}z^{3} = 12y^{2}z^{3} - y^{3}z^{3} - y^{2}z^{4}$ subject to the conditions $y + z < 12$ , $y > 0$ , $z > 0$ . $f_{y} = 24yz^{3} - 3y^{2}z^{3} - 2yz^{4}$ , $f_{z} = 36y^{2}z^{2} - 3y^{3}z^{2} - 4y^{2}z^{3}$ . Set $f_{y} = 0$ , $f_{z} = 0$ . Then $yz^{3}(24 - 3y - 2z) = 0$ and $y^{2}z^{2}(36 - 3y - 4z) = 0$ . Since $y \neq 0$ and $z \neq 0$ , $24 - 3y - 2z = 0$ and $36 - 3y - 4z = 0$ . Subtracting, we get $12 - 2z = 0$ , $z = 6$ . Hence, $y = 4$ and $x = 2$ . That this point yields the absolute maximum can be shown by an argument similar to that in Problem 43.32.
