35.64 Find the area of one petal of the three-leaved rose $r = \sin 3\theta$

By Problem 35.47, one petal is swept out from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = \pi / 3$ . Hence, the area is $\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} \sin^{2} 3\theta d\theta = \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} \frac{1}{2} (1 - \cos 6\theta) d\theta = \frac{1}{4} (\theta - \frac{1}{6} \sin 6\theta) \bigg]_{0}^{\pi / 3} = \frac{1}{4} (\pi / 3) = \pi / 12$ .
