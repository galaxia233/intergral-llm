43.38 Find the point on the surface $z = xy - 1$ that is nearest the origin.

It suffices to minimize $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2}$ for arbitrary points $(x, y, z)$ on the surface. Hence, we must minimize $f(x, y) = x^{2} + y^{2} + (xy - 1)^{2}$ . $f_{x} = 2x + 2(xy - 1)(y)$ , $f_{y} = 2y + 2(xy - 1)(x)$ . Set $f_{x} = 0$ , $f_{y} = 0$ . Then $x + (xy - 1)y = 0$ and $y + (xy - 1)x = 0$ . Hence, $x^{2} + xy(xy - 1) = 0$ and $y^{2} + xy(xy - 1) = 0$ . Therefore, $x^{2} = y^{2}$ , $x = \pm y$ . Substitute in $x + (xy - 1)y = 0$ , getting $x + x^{3} \pm x = 0$ . Hence, $x^{3} = 0$ or $x(x^{2} + 2) = 0$ . In either case, $x = 0$ , $y = 0$ , $z = -1$ . By geometric reasoning, there must be a minimum. Hence, it must be located at $(0, 0, -1)$ .
