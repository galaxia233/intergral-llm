45.38 Let $\mathbf{P} = (x, y, z)$ . For any function $f(u)$ , show that $\operatorname{curl}(f(|\mathbf{P}|)\mathbf{P}) = \mathbf{0}$ .

$\operatorname{curl}(f(|\mathbf{P}|)\mathbf{P}) = \operatorname{curl}(xf(|\mathbf{P}|), yf(|\mathbf{P}|), zf(|\mathbf{P}|)) = \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial y} [zf(|\mathbf{P}|)] - \frac{\partial}{\partial z} [yf(|\mathbf{P}|)], \frac{\partial}{\partial z} [xf(|\mathbf{P}|)] - \frac{\partial}{\partial x} [zf(|\mathbf{P}|)], \frac{\partial}{\partial x} [yf(|\mathbf{P}|)] - \frac{\partial}{\partial y} [xf(|\mathbf{P}|)]\right). \quad \text{Consider the first component:}$

$$
z f ^ {\prime} (| \mathbf {P} |) \frac {\partial}{\partial y} \sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}} - y f ^ {\prime} (| \mathbf {P} |) \frac {\partial}{\partial z} \sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}} = f ^ {\prime} (| \mathbf {P} |) \left(z \frac {y}{\sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}}} - y \frac {z}{\sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}}}\right) = 0.
$$

Similarly, the second and third components also are 0.
