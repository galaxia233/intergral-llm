44.46 Evaluate $I = \int_{\sqrt{2} /2}^{1}\int_{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}^{x}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} dy dx.$

The region of integration (Fig. 44-29) consists of all points in the first quadrant above the circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = 1$ and under the line $y = x$ . Transform to polar coordinates, noting that $x = 1$ is equivalent to $r = \sec \theta$ . $I = \int_{0}^{\pi /4}\int_{1}^{\sec \theta}\frac{1}{r}\cdot rdrd\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi /4}\int_{1}^{\sec \theta}drd\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi /4}(\sec \theta -1)d\theta = (\ln |\sec \theta +\tan \theta | - \theta)\big]_{0}^{\pi /4} = \ln (\sqrt{2} + 1) - \pi /4.$
