31.30 The region bounded by $y = e^{x}, \quad y = e^{-x}, \quad \text{and} \quad x = 1$ (Fig. 31-8).

![](images/a346b3601f431c49055df1ba0bb1265f303966137ee045a4c2750317899cf2c7.jpg)  
Fig. 31-8

The area $A = \int_0^1 (e^x - e^{-x}) dx = (e^x + e^{-x}) \big]_0^1 = e + e^{-1} - 2 = (e - 1)^2 / e$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^1 x(e^x - e^{-x}) dx = \int_0^1 xe^x dx - \int_0^1 xe^{-x} dx = e^x(x - 1) \big]_0^1 + e^{-x}(x + 1) \big]_0^1 = 1 + (2 / e - 1) = 2 / e$ . Hence, $\bar{x} = 2 / (e - 1)^2$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis is $M_x = \int_{1/e}^{1} y(1 + \ln y) dy + \int_{1/e}^{e} y(1 - \ln y) dy = \int_{1/e}^{e} y dy + \int_{1/e}^{1} y \ln y dy - \int_{1/e}^{e} y \ln y dy$ . To compute $\int y \ln y dy$ , we use integration by parts. Let $u = \ln y$ , $du = y dy$ , $du = \frac{1}{y} dy$ , $v = \frac{1}{2} y^2$ . Then $\int y \ln dy = \frac{1}{2} y^2 \ln y - \frac{1}{4} y^2 = \frac{1}{4} y^2 (2 \ln y - 1)$ . Thus, $M_x = \frac{1}{2} y^2 \big]_{1/e}^{e} + \frac{1}{4} y^2 (2 \ln y - 1) \big]_{1/e}^{1} - \frac{1}{4} y^2 (2 \ln y - 1) \big]_1^e = \frac{1}{2} (e^2 - e^{-2}) + (-\frac{1}{4} + \frac{3}{4} e^{-2}) - (-\frac{1}{4} e^2 + \frac{1}{4}) = \frac{1}{4} (3e^2 - 2 + e^{-2})$ . Thus, $\bar{y} = \frac{e(3e^2 - 2 + e^{-2})}{4(e - 1)^2}$ .
