40.46 If $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ , what can be concluded about $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ ?

$|\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}| = |\mathbf{B}| |\mathbf{C}|\sin \theta$ , where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ . So, if $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ , either $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{0}$ or $\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ or $\sin \theta = 0$ . Note that $\sin \theta = 0$ is equivalent to $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ being parallel; in other words, linearly dependent.
