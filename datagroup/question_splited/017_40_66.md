40.66 Show that $\mathbf{K}_i\perp \mathbf{V}_j$ for $i\neq j$ . (For the notation, see Problem 40.65.)

$\mathbf{K}_1 \cdot \mathbf{V}_2 = \frac{\mathbf{V}_2 \times \mathbf{V}_3}{c} \cdot \mathbf{V}_2 = 0$ since $\mathbf{V}_2$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{V}_2 \times \mathbf{V}_3$ . $\mathbf{K}_1 \cdot \mathbf{V}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{V}_2 \times \mathbf{V}_3}{c} \cdot \mathbf{V}_3 = 0$ since $\mathbf{V}_3$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{V}_2 \times \mathbf{V}_3$ . The other cases are handled similarly.
