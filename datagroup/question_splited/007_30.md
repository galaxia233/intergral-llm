30.1 $\int \frac{dx}{x^2 - 9}.$

$\frac{1}{x^2 - 9} = \frac{1}{(x - 3)(x + 3)} = \frac{A}{x - 3} + \frac{B}{x + 3}$ . Clear the denominators by multiplying both sides by $(x - 3)(x + 3)$ : $1 = A(x + 3) + B(x - 3)$ . Let $x = 3$ . Then $1 = 6A$ , $A = \frac{1}{6}$ . Let $x = -3$ . Then $1 = -6B$ , $B = -\frac{1}{6}$ . So $1 / (x^2 - 9) = \frac{1}{6} [1 / (x - 3)] - \frac{1}{6} [1 / (x + 3)]$ . Hence, $\int \frac{dx}{x^2 - 9} = \frac{1}{6} \ln |x - 3| - \frac{1}{6} \ln |x + 3| + C = \frac{1}{6} \ln |(x - 3) / (x + 3)| + C$ .
