31.33 Use Pappus's theorem to find the volume of a torus obtained by revolving a circle of radius $a$ about a line in its plane at a distance $b$ from its center $(b > a)$ .   
Pappus's theorem states that the volume of a solid generated by revolving a region $\mathcal{R}$ about a line $\mathcal{L}$ not passing through the region is equal to the product of the area $A$ of $\mathcal{R}$ and the distance $d$ traveled around the line by its centroid. In this case, $A = \pi a^2$ ; the centroid is the center of the circle (by symmetry), so that $d = 2\pi b$ . Hence, the volume $V = \pi a^2 \cdot 2\pi b = 2\pi^2 a^2 b$ .
