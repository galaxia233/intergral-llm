45.24 Show that the gradient $\nabla f$ of the scalar function $f(x, y, z) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}}$ satisfies $\nabla f = \frac{-\mathbf{P}}{|\mathbf{P}|^3}$ , where $\mathbf{P} = (x, y, z)$ .

$$
\nabla f = \left(f _ {x}, f _ {y}, f _ {z}\right) = \left(- \frac {x}{\left(x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}\right) ^ {3 / 2}}, - \frac {y}{\left(x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}\right) ^ {3 / 2}}, - \frac {z}{\left(x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} + z ^ {2}\right) ^ {3 / 2}}\right) = - \frac {1}{| \mathbf {P} | ^ {3}} \mathbf {P}.
$$
