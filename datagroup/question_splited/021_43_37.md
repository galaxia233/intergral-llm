43.37 The surface area of a rectangular box without a top is to be 108 square feet. Find the greatest possible volume.

Let $x, y, z$ be the length, width, and height. Then the surface area $S = xy + 2xz + 2yz = 108$ . The volume $V = xyz$ . Think of $z$ as a function of $x$ and $y$ ; then $V$ becomes a function of $x$ and $y$ . $V_x = y\left(x\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} + z\right)$ , $V_y = x\left(y\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} + z\right)$ . From $xy + 2xz + 2yz = 108$ by differentiation with respect to $x$ , $y + 2\left(x\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} + z\right) + 2y\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 0$ , and, therefore, $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -(2z + y) / [2(x + y)]$ . Similarly, $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -(2z + x) / [2(x + y)]$ . Set

$V_{x} = 0$ and $V_{y} = 0$ . Since $y \neq 0$ and $x \neq 0$ , $x \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} + z = 0$ and $y \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} + z = 0$ . So, $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -z / x$ and $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -z / y$ . Then, $-z / x = -(2z + y) / [2(x + y)]$ and $-z / y = -(2z + x) / [2(x + y)]$ . Thus, $2xz + 2yz = 2xz + xy$ and $2xz + 2yz = 2yz + xy$ , and, therefore, $z = x / 2$ and $z = y / 2$ . Substitute in $xy + 2xz + 2yz = 108$ . Then $4z^{2} + 4z^{2} + 4z^{2} = 108$ , $12z^{2} = 108$ , $z^{2} = 9$ , $z = 3$ . Hence, $x = 6$ , $y = 6$ . Therefore, the maximum volume is 108 cubic feet. (This can be shown in the usual way to be a relative maximum. An involved argument is necessary to show that it is an absolute maximum.)
