29.13 $\int \sin 2x\cos 2xdx.$

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int \sin 2 x \cos 2 x d x = \frac {1}{2} \int \sin 2 x \cdot 2 \cos 2 x d x = \frac {1}{2} \cdot \frac {1}{2} \sin^ {2} 2 x + C = \frac {1}{4} \sin^ {2} 2 x + C = \frac {1}{4} (2 \sin x \cos x) ^ {2} + C \\ = \sin^ {2} x \cos^ {2} x + C. \\ \end{array}
$$
