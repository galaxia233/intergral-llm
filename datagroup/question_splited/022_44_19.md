44.19 Find the volume $V$ of the tetrahedron bounded by the coordinate planes and the plane $z = 6 - 2x + 3y$ .

As shown in Fig. 44-8, the solid lies above the triangle in the $xy$ -plane bounded by $2x + 3y = 6$ and the $x$ and $y$ axes. $V = \int_0^3 \int_0^{2 - 2x/3} (6 - 2x - 3y) \, dy \, dx = \int_0^3 6y - 2xy - \frac{3}{2} y^2 \big]_0^{2 - 2x/3} \, dx = \int_0^3 (2 - \frac{2}{3} x)(6 - 2x - 3 + x) \, dx = \int_0^3 \frac{2}{3} (3 - x)(3 - x) \, dx = \frac{2}{3} \int_0^3 (3 - x)^2 \, dx = \frac{2}{3} (-\frac{1}{3})(3 - x)^3 \big]_0^3 = -\frac{2}{9} (-3^3) = 6.$ (Check against the formula $V = \frac{1}{6} abc$ .)
