43.24 Using the assumptions and notation of Problem 43.23, give examples to show that case 3, where $\Delta = 0$ , allows no conclusions to be drawn.

Each of the functions $f_{1}(x, y) = x^{4} + y^{4}$ , $f_{2}(x, y) = -(x^{4} + y^{4})$ , and $f_{3}(x, y) = x^{3} - y^{3}$ vanishes at $(0, 0)$ together with its first and second partials; hence $\Delta(0, 0) = 0$ for each function. But, at $(0, 0)$ , $f_{1}$ has a relative minimum, $f_{2}$ has a relative maximum, and $f_{3}$ has neither $[f_{3}(x, 0) = x^{3}$ takes on both positive and negative values in any neighborhood of the origin].
