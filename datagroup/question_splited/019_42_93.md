42.93 If $g(u)$ is continuously differentiable, show that $w = g(x^2 - y^2)$ is a solution of $y\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} + x\frac{\partial w}{\partial y} = 0$ .

$\partial w / \partial x = g'(x^2 - y^2)(2x), \partial w / \partial y = g'(x^2 - y^2)(-2y).$ Hence,

$$
y \frac {\partial w}{\partial x} + x \frac {\partial w}{\partial y} = [ y (2 x) + x (- 2 y) ] g ^ {\prime} (x ^ {2} - y ^ {2}) = 0.
$$
