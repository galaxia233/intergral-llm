20.81 Find a function $f$ such that $\int_0^x f(t)dt = \cos x - x^2$ .

Differentiating both sides of the given equation, we obtain $f(x) = -\sin x - 2x$ . Checking back, $\int_0^x (-\sin t - 2t)dt = (\cos t - t^2)\rfloor_0^x = (\cos x - x^2) - (1 - 0)\neq \cos x - x^2$ . Thus, there is no solution. In order for there to be a solution of $\int_0^x f(t)dt = g(x)$ , we must have $g(0) = 0$ .
