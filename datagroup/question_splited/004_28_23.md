28.23 $\int \frac{x^3}{\sqrt{1 + x^2}} dx.$

$$
\begin{array}{l} \text {A s i m p l e s u b s t i t u t i o n w o r k s : l e t} u = 1 + x ^ {2}, d u = 2 x d x. \int \frac {x ^ {3}}{\sqrt {1 + x ^ {2}}} d x = \frac {1}{2} \int \frac {u - 1}{\sqrt {u}} d u = \frac {1}{2} \int (u ^ {1 / 2} - \\ u ^ {- 1 / 2}) d u = \frac {1}{2} (\frac {2}{3} u ^ {3 / 2} - 2 u ^ {1 / 2}) + C = \frac {1}{3} u ^ {1 / 2} (u - 3) + C = \frac {1}{3} \sqrt {1 + x ^ {2}} (x ^ {2} - 2) + C. \end{array}
