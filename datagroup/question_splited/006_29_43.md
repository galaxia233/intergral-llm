29.43 In a disk of radius $a$ , a chord $b$ units from the center cuts off a region of the disk called a segment. Find a formula for the area of the segment.

From a diagram, the area $A = 2\int_{b}^{a}\sqrt{a^2 - x^2} dx$ . Let $x = a\sin \theta$ , $dx = a\cos \theta d\theta$ . Then, $\int \sqrt{a^2 - x^2} dx = \int a\cos \theta \cdot a\cos \theta d\theta = a^2\int \cos^2\theta d\theta$ . By Problem 29.1, this is $a^2 \cdot \frac {1}{2} (\theta + \sin \theta \cos \theta) = \frac {1}{2} a^2\left(\sin^{-1}\frac {x}{a} + \frac {x}{a}\right)$ . Hence, $A = a^2\left(\sin^{-1}\frac {x}{a} + \frac {x}{a}\frac{\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}}{a}\right)\bigg]_b^a = a^2\left[\left(\frac {\pi}{2} + 0\right) - \left(\sin^{-1}\frac {b}{a} + \frac {b\sqrt{a^2 - b^2}}{a^2}\right)\right] = a^2\left(\frac {\pi}{2} - \frac {b}{a}\right)$ .
