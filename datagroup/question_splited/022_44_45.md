44.44 Use polar coordinates to evaluate $I = \int_0^2\int_0^{\sqrt{2x - x^2}}xdydx.$

$y = \sqrt{2x - x^2}$ is equivalent to $y^{2} = 2x - x^{2},$ $y\geq 0,$ or the upper half of the circle $(x - 1)^2 +y^2 = 1$ (see Fig. 44-28). In polar coordinates, $x^{2} + y^{2} = 2x$ is equivalent to $r^2 = 2r$ cos $\theta ,$ or $r = 2\cos \theta .$ Thus, $I = \int_0^{\pi /2}\int_0^{2\cos \theta}(r\cos \theta)rdrd\theta = \int_0^{\pi /2}\int_0^{2\cos \theta}r^2\cos \theta drd\theta = \int_0^{\pi /2}\frac{1}{3} r^3\cos \theta ]_0^{2\cos \theta}d\theta = \frac{8}{3}\int_0^{\pi /2}\cos^4\theta d\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}$ (by Problem 44.29).
