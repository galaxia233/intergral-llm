40.76 Show that the lines $x = 1 + t$ , $y = 2t$ , $z = 1 + 3t$ and $x = 3s$ , $y = 2s$ , $z = 2 + s$ intersect, and find their point of intersection.

Assume $(x, y, z)$ is a point of intersection. Then $2t = y = 2s$ . So, $t = s$ . From $1 + t = x = 3s$ , we then have $1 + s = 3s$ , $2s = 1$ , $s = \frac{1}{2}$ . So, $s = t = \frac{1}{2}$ . Note that $1 + 3t = 1 + 3\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{5}{2}$ and $2 + s = 2 + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$ . Thus, the equations for $z$ are compatible. The intersection point is $\left(\frac{3}{2}, 1, \frac{5}{2}\right)$ .
