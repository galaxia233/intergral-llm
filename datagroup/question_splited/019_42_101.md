42.101 Assume $\partial f / \partial x = g(y)$ for all $(x,y)$ . Show that $f(x,y) = xg(y) + h(y)$ for some function $h$ .

Let $H(x, y) = f(x, y) - x g(y)$ . Then, $\frac{\partial H}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} - g(y) = 0$ . By Problem 42.98, $H(x, y) = h(y)$ for a suitable function $h$ . Hence, $f(x, y) = x g(y) + h(y)$ .
