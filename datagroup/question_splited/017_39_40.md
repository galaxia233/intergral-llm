39.40 Estimate $\int_0^{1 / 2}\frac{\ln(1 + x)}{x} dx$ to within two-decimal-place accuracy.

For $|x| < 1$ , $\frac{\ln(1 + x)}{x} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n+1} \frac{x^{n-1}}{n}$ . Hence, $\int_{0}^{1/2} \frac{\ln(1 + x)}{x} dx = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n+1} \frac{x^n]_0^{1/2}}{n^2} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2 2^n}$ . This is an alternating series. Therefore, we seek $n$ for which $1/n^2 2^n \leq 0.005$ , $200 \leq n^2 2^n$ , $n \geq 4$ . Hence, we may use $\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{16} + \frac{1}{72} = \frac{65}{144} \approx 0.45$ .
