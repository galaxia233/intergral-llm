44.28 Find $I = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} e^{x^3} dA$ , where $\mathcal{R}$ is the region bounded by $y = x^2, x = 3,$ and $y = 0$ .

Use strips parallel to the $y$ -axis (see Fig. 44-16). $I = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{x^2}e^{x^3}dy dx = \int_{0}^{3}e^{x^3}y]_0^{x^2}dx = \int_{0}^{3}e^{x^3}x^2 dx = \frac{1}{3} e^{x^3}]_0^3 = \frac{1}{3} (e^{27} - e^0) = \frac{1}{3} (e^{27} - 1)$ . Note that the integral with the variables in reverse order would have been impossible to calculate.
