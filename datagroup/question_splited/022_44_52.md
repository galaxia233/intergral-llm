44.51 Use spherical coordinates to find the volume of a right circular cone of height $h$ and radius of base $b$ .

For the orientation shown in Fig. 44-30, the points of the cone satisfy $0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi$ , $0 \leq \phi \leq \tan^{-1}(b / h)$ , $0 \leq \rho \leq h \sec \phi$ . Thus, $V = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\tan^{-1}(b / h)} \int_{0}^{h \sec \phi} \rho^{2} \sin \phi d\rho d\phi d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\tan^{-1}(b / h)} \frac{1}{3} \rho^{3} \sin \phi \big]_{0}^{h \sec \phi} d\phi d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\tan^{-1}(b / h)} \frac{1}{3} h^{3} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \frac{1}{2 \cos^{2}\phi} \bigg]_{0}^{\tan^{-1}(b / h)} d\theta = \frac{1}{3} h^{3} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{s^{2}}{h^{2}} - 1\right) d\theta = \frac{h^{3}}{6h^{2}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} (s^{2} - h^{2}) d\theta = \frac{hb^{2}}{6} \int_{0}^{2\pi} d\theta = \frac{hb^{2}}{6} \cdot 2\pi = \frac{1}{3} \pi b^{2} h.$
