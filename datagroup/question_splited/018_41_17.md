41.17 Describe and sketch the graph of the function $f(x, y) = x^{2} + y^{2}$ .

See Fig. 41-12. This is the graph of $z = x^2 + y^2$ . Note that $z \geq 0$ . When $z = 0$ , $x^2 + y^2 = 0$ , and, therefore, $x = y = 0$ . So, the intersection with the $xy$ -plane is the origin. Sections made by planes $z = k > 0$ are circles with centers on the $z$ -axis. Sections made by planes $x = k$ or $y = k$ are parabolas. The surface is called a circular paraboloid.
