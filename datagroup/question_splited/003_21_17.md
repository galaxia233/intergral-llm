21.17 $y = \frac{x^4}{8} +\frac{1}{4x^2}$ from $x = 1$ to $x = 2$

Recall that the arc length formula is $L = \int_{a}^{b}\sqrt{1 + (y')^2} dx$ . In this case,

$$
y ^ {\prime} = \frac {1}{2} x ^ {3} - \frac {1}{2} \frac {1}{x ^ {3}} = \frac {1}{2} \left(\frac {x ^ {6} - 1}{x ^ {3}}\right) \quad (y ^ {\prime}) ^ {2} = \frac {(x ^ {6} - 1) ^ {2}}{4 x ^ {6}} = \frac {x ^ {1 2} - 2 x ^ {6} + 1}{4 x ^ {6}}
$$

Hence, $1 + (y')^2 = \frac{4x^6 + x^{12} - 2x^6 + 1}{4x^6} = \frac{x^{12} + 2x^6 + 1}{4x^6} = \left(\frac{x^6 + 1}{2x^3}\right)^2$

Thus, $L = \int_{1}^{2}\frac{x^6 + 1}{2x^3} dx = \frac{1}{2}\int_{1}^{2}\left(x^3 +x^{-3}\right)dx = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x^4}{4} -\frac{1}{2} x^{-2}\right)\Bigg{|}_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} [(4 - \frac{1}{8}) - (\frac{1}{4} -\frac{1}{2})] = \frac{33}{16}$
