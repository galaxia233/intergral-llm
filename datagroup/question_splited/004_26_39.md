26.39 An object cools from 120 to $95^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ in half an hour when surrounded by air whose temperature is $70^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ . Use Newton's law of cooling (Problem 26.22) to find its temperature at the end of another half an hour.

Let $y$ be the difference in temperature between the object and the air. By Newton's law, $y = y_0e^{Kt}$ . Since $y_0 = 120 - 70 = 50$ , $y = 50e^{Kt}$ . At $t = \frac{1}{2}$ , $25 = 50e^{K/2}$ , $\frac{1}{2} = e^{K/2} = (e^K)^{1/2}$ , $e^K = \frac{1}{4}$ . When $t = 1$ , $y = 50e^K = 50 \cdot \frac{1}{4} = 12.5$ . Hence, the temperature of the object is $70 + 12.5 = 82.5^\circ \mathrm{F}$ .
