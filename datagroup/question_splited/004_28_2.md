28.2 $\int e^{x}\sin xdx.$

Let $u = \sin x, \quad dv = e^x dx, \quad du = \cos x dx, \quad v = e^x$ . Then

$$
\int e ^ {x} \sin x d x = e ^ {x} \sin x - \int e ^ {x} \cos x d x. \tag {1}
$$

We use integration by parts again for the latter integral: let $u = \cos x$ , $dv = e^x dx$ , $du = -\sin x dx$ , $v = e^x$ . Then $\int e^x \cos x dx = e^x \cos x + \int e^x \sin x dx$ . Substituting in (1), $\int e^x \sin x dx = e^x \sin x - (e^x \cos x + \int e^x \sin x dx) = e^x \sin x - e^x \cos x - \int e^x \sin x dx$ . Thus, $2 \int e^x \sin x dx = e^x (\sin x - \cos x) + C$ , $\int e^x \sin x dx$ from which $\frac{1}{2} e^x (\sin x - \cos x) + C_1$ .
