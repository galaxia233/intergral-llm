46.22 Find the orthogonal trajectories of the family of circles tangent to the $y$ -axis at the origin.

The family consists of all curves $(x - a)^2 + y^2 = a^2$ , with $a \neq 0$ , or

$$
x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} = 2 a x \tag {1}
$$

Differentiate: $2x + 2y \frac{dy}{dx} = 2a, \quad x + y \frac{dy}{dx} = a.$ Substitute this value of $a$ in (1), obtaining $x^2 + y^2 = 2x\left(x + y \frac{dy}{dx}\right)$ . To find the orthogonal trajectories, replace $\frac{dy}{dx}$ by its negative reciprocal, which will be the slope of the orthogonal curves:

$$
x ^ {2} + y ^ {2} = 2 x \left(x - \frac {y}{y ^ {\prime}}\right) \tag {2}
$$

Solve for $y'$ : $\frac{y}{y'} = x - \frac{x^2 + y^2}{2x} = \frac{x^2 - y^2}{2x}$ , $y' = \frac{2xy}{x^2 - y^2}$ . The numerator and denominator on the right are homogeneous. Let $y = vx$ . Then $v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{2x^2v}{x^2 - x^2v^2} = \frac{2v}{1 - v^2}$ , $x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{2v}{1 - v^2} - v = \frac{v + v^3}{1 - v^2}$ . $\int \frac{1 - v^2}{v + v^3} dv = \int \frac{dx}{x}$ . By a partial-fractions decomposition, $\int \frac{dv}{v} - 2\int \frac{v}{1 + v^2} dv = \int \frac{dx}{x}$ , $\ln |v| - \ln (1 + v^2) = \ln |x| + \ln C_1$ , $\frac{v}{1 + v^2} = C_x$ , $\frac{y/x}{1 + y^2/x^2} = C_y$ , $\frac{y}{x^2 + y^2} = C_z$ , $x^2 + y^2 = \frac{1}{C} y$ , $x^2 + y^2 = 2Ky$ , $x^2 + (y - K)^2 = K^2$ . This is the family of all circles tangent to the $x$ -axis at the origin.
