42.110 Find an equation of the tangent plane to the sphere $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = 1$ at the point $\left(\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2},1 / \sqrt{2}\right)$ .

By Problem 42.109, a normal vector to the tangent plane will be $(2x, 2y, 2z) = (1, 1, \sqrt{2})$ . Hence, an equation for the tangent plane is $(x - \frac{1}{2}) + (y - \frac{1}{2}) + \sqrt{2} [z - (1 / \sqrt{2})] = 0$ , or, equivalently, $x + y + \sqrt{2} z = 2$ .
