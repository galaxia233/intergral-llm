34.66 Prove the converse of Problem 34.59.

By Problem 34.57, $\frac{d}{dt} [\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t)] = 2\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}'(t)$ . Hence, $\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}'(t) = 0$ implies $\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t) = c^2$ for some positive constant $c$ . Then $|\mathbf{R}(t)| = \sqrt{\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot\mathbf{R}(t)} = c$ .
