20.50 If $f$ is an even function, show that $\int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx$ .

Let $u = -x, \quad du = -dx$ . Then $\int_{-a}^{0} f(x) dx = -\int_{a}^{0} f(-u) du = -\int_{a}^{0} f(u) du = \int_{0}^{a} f(u) du = \int_{0}^{a} f(x) dx$ . Hence, $\int_{-a}^{a} f(x) dx = \int_{0}^{a} f(x) dx + \int_{0}^{a} f(x) dx = 2\int_{0}^{a} f(x) dx$ .
