31.27 The region bounded by $y = \frac{1}{x}, \quad y = 0, \quad x = 1, \quad x = 2$ (Fig. 31-5).

The area $A = \int_{1}^{2} \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln x \bigg]_{1}^{2} = \ln 2$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_{1}^{2} x \cdot \frac{1}{x} dx = \int_{1}^{2} 1 \, dx = 1$ . Thus, $\bar{x} = \frac{1}{\ln 2}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis is $M_x = \int_{0}^{1/2} y \, dy + \int_{1/2}^{1} y \left( \frac{1}{y} - 1 \right) \, dy = \frac{1}{2} y^2 \Bigg]_0^{1/2} + \left( y - \frac{1}{2} y^2 \right) \Bigg]_{1/2}^{1} = \frac{1}{8} + \left[ \left( 1 - \frac{1}{2} \right) - \left( \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{8} \right) \right] = \frac{1}{4}$ . Thus, $\bar{y} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\ln 2}$ . So the centroid is $\left( \frac{1}{\ln 2}, \frac{1}{4\ln 2} \right)$ .
