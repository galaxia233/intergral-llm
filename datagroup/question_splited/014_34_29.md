34.31 Where does the curve $x = 2t^2 - 5, y = t^3 + t$ have a tangent line that is perpendicular to the line $x + y + 3 = 0$ ?

$dx/dt = 4t, \quad dy/dt = 3t^2 + 1.$ So, $dy/dx = (3t^2 + 1)/4t.$ The slope of the line $x + y + 3 = 0$ is $-1$ , and, therefore, the slope of a line perpendicular to it is 1. Thus, we must have $dy/dx = 1$ , $(3t^2 + 1)/4t = 1$ , $3t^2 + 1 = 4t$ , $3t^2 - 4t + 1 = 0$ , $(3t - 1)(t - 1) = 0$ , $t = \frac{1}{3}$ or $t = 1$ . Hence, the required points are $(- \frac{43}{9}, \frac{10}{27})$ and $(-3, 2)$ .
