46.46 Solve $y^{\prime \prime} - (y^{\prime})^{2} = 0$

Let $z = y'$ . Then $z' - z^2 = 0$ is a first-order equation. $dz / dx = z^2$ . The variables $x$ and $z$ are separable. $\int \frac{dz}{z^2} = \int dx, -1 / z = x + C, z = -1 / (x + C)$ . Hence, $dy / dx = -1 / (x + C), y = -\ln |x + C| + K$ .
