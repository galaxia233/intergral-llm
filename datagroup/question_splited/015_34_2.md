34.44 If $\mathbf{R}(\theta) = (r\cos \theta, r\sin \theta)$ , with fixed $r > 0$ , show that $\mathbf{R}'(\theta) \perp \mathbf{R}(\theta)$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \text {B y P r o b l e m 3 4 . 4 3 ,} \quad \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (\theta) = (- r \sin \theta , r \cos \theta). \quad \text {T h e n} \quad \mathbf {R} (\theta) \cdot \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (\theta) = (r \cos \theta , r \sin \theta) \cdot (- r \sin \theta , r \cos \theta) = \\ - r ^ {2} \cos \theta \sin \theta + r ^ {2} \sin \theta \cos \theta = 0. \end{array}
