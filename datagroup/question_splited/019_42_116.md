42.116 Let normal lines be drawn at all points on the surface $z = ax^2 + by^2$ that are at a given height $h$ above the $xy$ -plane. Find an equation of the curve in which these lines intersect the $xy$ -plane.

The normal vectors are $(2ax, 2by, -1)$ . Hence, the normal line at $(x_0, y_0, h)$ has parametric equations $x = x_0 + 2ax_0t$ , $y = y_0 + 2by_0t$ , $z = h - t$ . This line hits the $xy$ -plane when $z = 0$ , that is, when $t = h$ . Thus, the point of intersection is $x = x_0 + 2ax_0h = x_0(1 + 2ah)$ , $y = y_0 + 2by_0h = y_0(1 + 2bh)$ . Note that $h = ax_0^2 + by_0^2 = \frac{ax^2}{(1 + 2ah)^2} + \frac{by^2}{(1 + 2bh)^2}$ . Hence, the desired equation is $\frac{ax^2}{(1 + 2ah)^2} + \frac{by^2}{(1 + 2bh)^2} = h$ .
