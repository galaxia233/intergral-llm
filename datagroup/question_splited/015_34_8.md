34.50 Find the unit tangent vector for the curve $\mathbf{R}(\theta) = (e^{\theta}, e^{-\theta})$ .

$\mathbf{R}^{\prime}(\theta) = (e^{\theta}, - e^{-\theta})$ $\left|\mathbf{R}'(\theta)\right| = \sqrt{e^{2\theta} + e^{-2\theta}} = \sqrt{e^{4\theta} + 1} /e^{\theta}$ . Hence, the unit tangent vector

$$
\mathbf {T} = \frac {1}{| \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (\theta) |} \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (\theta) = \frac {e ^ {\theta}}{\sqrt {e ^ {4 \theta} + 1}} (e ^ {\theta}, - e ^ {- \theta}) = \frac {1}{\sqrt {e ^ {4 \theta} + 1}} (e ^ {2 \theta}, - 1).
$$
