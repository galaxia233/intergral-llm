20.68 State Simpson's rule for approximation of integrals.

Let $f(x)$ be integrable and nonnegative on $[a, b]$ . Divide $[a, b]$ into $n = 2k$ equal subintervals of length $\Delta x = (b - a) / n$ . Then $\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx \approx \frac{\Delta x}{3} \left[ f(a) + 4f(a + \Delta x) + 2f(a + 2\Delta x) + 4f(a + 3\Delta x) + 2f(a + 4\Delta x) + \dots + 2f(a + (2k - 2)\Delta x) + 4f(a + (2k - 1)\Delta x) + f(b) \right]$ .
