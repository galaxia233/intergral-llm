【1817】 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{(a^2 + x^2)^2}$

解当 $a = 0$ 时，因为 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{(a^2 + x^2)^2} = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^4} = -\frac{1}{3x^3} +C;$

当 $a \neq 0$ 时，因为 $\frac{1}{a} \arctan \frac{x}{a} = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^2 + a^2} = \frac{x}{x^2 + a^2} + 2\int \frac{x^2 + a^2 - a^2}{(x^2 + a^2)^2}\mathrm{d}x,$

故得 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{(x^2 + a^2)^2} = \frac{1}{2a^2}\left(\frac{x}{x^2 + a^2} +\frac{1}{a}\arctan \frac{x}{a}\right) + C.$
