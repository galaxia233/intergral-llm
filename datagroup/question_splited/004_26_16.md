26.16 The half-life of radium is 1690 years. If 10 percent of an original quantity of radium remains, how long ago was the radium created?

Let $y$ be the number of grams of radius $t$ years after the radium was created. Then $y = y_0e^{Kt}$ , where $1690K = -\ln 2$ , by Problem 26.15. If at the present time $t$ , $y = \frac{1}{10} y_0$ , then $\frac{1}{10} y_0 = y_0e^{Kt}$ , $\frac{1}{10} = e^{Kt}$ , $Kt = \ln \frac{1}{10} = -\ln 10$ . Hence, $-(1n2/1690)t = -\ln 10$ , $t = 1690\ln 10/\ln 2 \approx 5614.477$ . Thus, the radium was created about 5614 years ago.
