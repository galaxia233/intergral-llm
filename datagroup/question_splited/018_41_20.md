41.20 Find the points at which the line $\frac{x - 6}{3} = \frac{y + 2}{-6} = \frac{z - 2}{-4}$ intersects the ellipsoid $\frac{x^2}{81} + \frac{y^2}{36} + \frac{z^2}{9} = 1$ .

The line can be written in parametric form as $x = 6 + 3t$ , $y = -2 - 6t$ , $z = 2 + 4t$ . Hence, substituting in the equation of the ellipsoid, we get $\frac{(6 + 3t)^2}{81} + \frac{(2 + 6t)^2}{36} + \frac{(2 + 4t)^2}{9} = 1$ , $4(6 + 3t)^2 + 9(2 + 6t)^2 + 36(2 + 4t)^2 = 324$ , $26(36t^2) + 26(36t) + 4(81) = 324$ , $t^2 + t = 0$ , $t(t + 1) = 0$ , $t = 0$ or $t = -1$ . So, the points are $(6, -2, 2)$ and $(3, 4, -2)$ .
