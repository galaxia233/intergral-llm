46.35 Solve $y' + \frac{2}{x}y = 6x^2$ .

Use an integrating factor. Let $\rho = \exp \left(\int \frac{2}{x} dx\right) = e^{2\ln x} = x^2$ . Hence, $\int \rho q(x) dx = \int x^2 \cdot 6x^2 dx = 6 \int x^4 dx = \frac{6}{5} x^5$ . Then $y = (1 / x^2)(\frac{6}{5} x^5 + C) = \frac{6}{5} x^3 + C / x^2$ .
