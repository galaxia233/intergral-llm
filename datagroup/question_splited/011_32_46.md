32.47 Show that the region in the first quadrant under the curve $y = 1 / (x + 1)^2$ has a finite area but does not have a centroid.

$A = \int_{0}^{\infty}\frac{dx}{(x + 1)^2} = \lim_{v\to +\infty} - \frac{1}{x + 1}\bigg]_0^v = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\left(-\frac{1}{v + 1} +1\right) = 1.$ However,

$$
M _ {y} = \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} \frac {x d x}{(x + 1) ^ {2}} = \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} \left[ \frac {1}{x + 1} - \frac {1}{(x + 1) ^ {2}} \right] d x = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \left(\ln | x + 1 | + \frac {1}{x + 1}\right) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {v} = + \infty
$$

Hence, the $x$ -coordinate of the centroid is infinite.
