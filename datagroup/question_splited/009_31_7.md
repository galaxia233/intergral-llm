31.7 $x^{2 / 3} + y^{2 / 3} = a^{2 / 3}$ in the first quadrant; about the $x$ -axis.

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {2}{3} x ^ {- 1 / 3} + \frac {2}{3} y ^ {- 1 / 3} y ^ {\prime} = 0, \quad y ^ {\prime} = - \frac {y ^ {1 / 3}}{x ^ {1 / 3}}, \quad 1 + (y ^ {\prime}) ^ {2} = 1 + \frac {y ^ {2 / 3}}{x ^ {2 / 3}} = \frac {x ^ {2 / 3} + y ^ {2 / 3}}{x ^ {2 / 3}} = \frac {a ^ {2 / 3}}{x ^ {2 / 3}}. \\ 2 \pi \int_ {0} ^ {a} a ^ {1 / 3} \frac {(a ^ {2 / 3} - x ^ {2 / 3}) ^ {3 / 2}}{x ^ {1 / 3}} d x = 2 \pi a ^ {1 / 3} (- \frac {3}{2}) (\frac {2}{5}) (a ^ {2 / 3} - x ^ {2 / 3}) ^ {5 / 2} ] _ {0} ^ {a} = - \frac {6 \pi}{5} a ^ {1 / 3} (- a ^ {5 / 3}) = \frac {6 \pi}{5} a ^ {6 / 3} = \frac {6 \pi}{5} a ^ {2}. \end{array}
$$
