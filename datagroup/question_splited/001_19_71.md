19.71 Find the equation of a curve such that $y'' = 6x - 8$ and, at the point $(1,0)$ , $y' = 4$ .

$y^\prime = \int y^{\prime \prime}dx = \int (6x - 8)dx = 3x^2 -8x + C.$ When $x = 1$ $y^\prime = 4$ so, $4 = -5 + C,$ $C = 9$ . Thus, $y^\prime = 3x^2 -8x + 9.$ Then $y = \int y^{\prime}dx = x^{3} - 4x^{2} + 9x + C_{1}.$ When $x = 1$ $y = 0$ . Hence, $0 = 6 + C_{1}$ $C_1 = -6$ So, $y = x^3 -4x^2 +9x - 6.$
