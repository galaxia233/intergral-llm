43.6 Find the derivative of $f(x, y, z) = x^{2}y^{2}z$ at (2, 1, 4) in the direction of the vector $(1, 2, 2)$ .

The unit vector in the indicated direction is $\mathbf{u} = \frac{1}{3}(1, 2, 2)$ . $\nabla f = (2xy^2z, 2x^2yz, x^2y^2) = (16, 32, 64) = 16(1, 2, 4)$ . Hence, the derivative is $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = 16(1, 2, 4) \cdot \frac{1}{3}(1, 2, 2) = \frac{16}{3}(13) = \frac{208}{3}$ .
