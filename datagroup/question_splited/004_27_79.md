27.79 A person walking along a straight path at the rate of 6 ft/s is followed by a spotlight that comes from a point 30 feet from the path. How fast is the spotlight turning when the person is 40 feet past the point $A$ on the path nearest the light?

Let $x$ be the distance of the person from $A$ , and let $\theta$ be the angle between the spotlight and the line to $A$ . Then $\theta = \tan^{-1}(x / 30)$ , and $D_x\theta = \{1 / [1 + (x / 30)^2]\} \cdot \frac{1}{30} \cdot D_tx = 180 / (900 + x^2)$ . When $x = 40$ , $D_x\theta = \frac{18}{250} = 0.072$ radian per second.
