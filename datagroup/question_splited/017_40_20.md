40.20 Find the point on the $y$ -axis equidistant from $(2,5,-3)$ and $(-3,6,1)$ .

Let the point be $(0, y, 0)$ . Then $\sqrt{(2 - 0)^2 + (5 - y)^2 + (-3 - 0)^2} = \sqrt{(-3 - 0)^2 + (6 - y)^2 + (1 - 0)^2}$ , $13 + (5 - y)^2 = 10 + (6 - y)^2$ , $y^2 - 10y + 38 = y^2 - 12y + 46$ , $2y = 8$ , $y = 4$ . The desired point is $(0, 4, 0)$ .
