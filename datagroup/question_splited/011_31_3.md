31.26 The region bounded by $y = \sin x, \quad y = 0, \quad \text{from} \quad x = 0 \quad \text{to} \quad x = \pi$ (Fig. 31-4).

By symmetry, $\bar{x} = \frac{\pi}{2}$ . The area $A = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin x \, dx = -\cos x$ , $\int_{0}^{\pi} = -(-1 - 1) = 2$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis $M_x = \int_{0}^{1} y \cdot 2\left(\frac{\pi}{2} - \sin^{-1} y\right) dy = 2\int_{0}^{1} \left(\frac{\pi}{2} y - y \sin^{-1} y\right) dy$ . By Problem 29.40, $\int_{0}^{1} y \sin^{-1} y dy = \frac{1}{4} [(2y^2 - 1) \sin^{-1} y + y \sqrt{1 - y^2}]$ . Thus, $M_x = 2\left\{\frac{\pi}{4} y^2 - \frac{1}{4} ((2y^2 - 1) \sin^{-1} y + y \sqrt{1 - y^2})\right\}\Bigg|_0^1 = 2\left(\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{\pi}{8}\right) = \frac{\pi}{4}$ . Hence, $\bar{y} = \frac{\pi / 4}{2} = \frac{\pi}{8}$ .
