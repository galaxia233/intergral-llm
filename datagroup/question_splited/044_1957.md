【1957】 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{(1 + x^2)\sqrt{1 - x^2}}.$

解设 $x = \sin t$ ，并限制 $-\frac{\pi}{2} < t < \frac{\pi}{2}$ ，则 $\mathrm{d}x = \cos t\mathrm{d}t,\quad \sqrt{1 - x^2} = \cos t.$

代入得 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{(1 + x^2)\sqrt{1 - x^2}} = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}t}{1 + \sin^2 t} = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}t}{2\sin^2 t + \cos^2 t} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \int \frac{\mathrm{d}(\sqrt{2} \tan t)}{(\sqrt{2} \tan t)^2 + 1}$

$$
= \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \arctan (\sqrt {2} \tan t) + C = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \arctan \left(\frac {x \sqrt {2}}{\sqrt {1 - x ^ {2}}}\right) + C.
$$
