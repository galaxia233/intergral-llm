32.30 Evaluate $\int_{1}^{5}\frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}}.$

There is a discontinuity at $x = 2$ . Thus, $\int_{1}^{5} \frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}} = \int_{1}^{2} \frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}} + \int_{2}^{5} \frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} \int_{1}^{v} \frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}} + \lim_{u \to 2^{+}} \int_{u}^{5} \frac{dx}{\sqrt[3]{x - 2}} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} \frac{3}{2}(x - 2)^{2/3}]_{1}^{v} + \lim_{u \to 2^{+}} \frac{3}{2}(x - 2)^{2/3}]_{u}^{5} = \lim_{v \to 2^{-}} [\frac{3}{2}(v - 2)^{2/3} - \frac{3}{2}] + \lim_{u \to 2^{+}} [\frac{3}{2}(3)^{2/3} - \frac{3}{2}(u - 2)^{2/3}] = -\frac{3}{2} + \frac{3}{2}(3)^{2/3} = \frac{3}{2} (\sqrt[3]{9} - 1)$ .
