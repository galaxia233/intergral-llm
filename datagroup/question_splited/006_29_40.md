29.40 Find $\int x\sin^{-1}x dx$

First, use integration by parts. Let $u = \sin^{-1} x$ , $dv = x \, dx$ , $du = \frac {1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx$ , $v = \frac {1}{2} \, x^2$ . Then $\int x \sin^{-1} x \, dx = \frac {1}{2} x^2 \sin^{-1} x - \frac {1}{2} \int \frac {x^2}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx$ . For the latter integral, use a trigonometric substitution. Let $x = \sin \theta$ , $dx = \cos \theta \, d\theta$ . Then $\int \frac {x^2}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx = \int \frac{\sin^2 \theta \cos \theta \, d\theta}{\cos \theta} = \int \sin^2 \theta \, d\theta = \frac {1}{2} (\theta - \sin \theta \cos \theta)$ (by Problem 29.2). Hence, the answer is $\frac {1}{2} x^2 \sin^{-1} x - \frac {1}{4} (\sin^{-1} x - x\sqrt{1 - x^2}) + C = \frac {1}{4} [(2x^2 - 1) \sin^{-1} x + x\sqrt{1 - x^2}] + C$ .
