32.35 Evaluate $\int_{-1}^{8}\frac{1}{x^{1 / 3}} dx.$

There is a discontinuity at $x = 0$ . So, $\int_{-1}^{8} \frac{1}{x^{1/3}} dx = \int_{-1}^{0} \frac{dx}{x^{1/3}} + \int_{0}^{8} \frac{dx}{x^{1/3}}$ . For the first integral, $\int_{-1}^{0} \frac{dx}{x^{1/3}} = \lim_{u \to 0^-} \frac{3}{2} x^{2/3}]_{-1}^u = \lim_{u \to 0^-} \frac{3}{2} (u^{2/3} - 1) = -\frac{3}{2}$ . Also, $\int_{0}^{8} \frac{dx}{x^{1/3}} = \lim_{v \to 0^+} \frac{3}{2} x^{2/3}]_v^8 = \lim_{v \to 0^+} \frac{3}{2} (4 - v^{2/3}) = 6$ .

Thus, the value is $-\frac{3}{2} + 6 = \frac{9}{2}$ .
