19.72 A car is slowing down at the rate of $0.8\mathrm{ft / s^2}$ . How far will the car move before it stops if its speed was initially $15\mathrm{mi / h}$ ?

$a = -0.8$ . Hence, $v = \int a dt = -0.8t + C$ . When $t = 0$ , $v = 22 ft/s$ [15 mi/h = (15·5280)/3600 ft/s = 22 ft/s.] So, $v = -0.8t + 22$ . The car stops when $v = 0$ , that is, when $t = 27.5$ . The position $s = \int v dt = -0.4t^2 + 22t + s_0$ , where $s_0$ is the initial position. Hence, the distance traveled in 27.5 seconds is $-0.4(27.5)^2 + 22(27.5) = 302.5$ ft.
