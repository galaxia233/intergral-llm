【2283】 $I_{n} = \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\tan^{2n}x\mathrm{d}x.$

提示 $I_{n} = \frac{1}{2n - 1} - I_{n - 1}$

解 $I_{n} = \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\tan^{2n - 2}x(\sec^{2}x - 1)\mathrm{d}x = \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\tan^{2n - 2}xd(\tan x) - \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\tan^{2n - 2}x\mathrm{d}x = \frac{1}{2n - 1} -I_{n - 1},$

即 $I_{n} = \frac{1}{2n - 1} - I_{n - 1}$ .

由于 $I_0 = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\mathrm{d}x = \frac{\pi}{4}$ ，于是，利用上述递推公式即可求得

$$
\begin{array}{l} I _ {n} = \frac {1}{2 n - 1} - \left(\frac {1}{2 n - 3} - I _ {n - 2}\right) = \dots = \frac {1}{2 n - 1} - \frac {1}{2 n - 3} + \frac {1}{2 n - 5} - \dots + (- 1) ^ {n} I _ {\circ} \\ = (- 1) ^ {n} \left[ \frac {\pi}{4} - \left(1 - \frac {1}{3} + \frac {1}{5} - \dots + \frac {(- 1) ^ {n - 1}}{2 n - 1}\right) \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$
