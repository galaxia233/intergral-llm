34.76 Let $\mathbf{R}(t) = (10\cos 2\pi t,10\sin 2\pi t)$ . Find the acceleration vector.

Let $\theta = 2\pi t$ . Then $d\theta / dt = 2\pi$ and $\mathbf{R}(t) = 10(\cos \theta, \sin \theta)$ . By the chain rule, $\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{R}'(t) = 10(-\sin \theta, \cos \theta)\frac{d\theta}{dt} = 20\pi (-\sin \theta, \cos \theta)$ . By the chain rule again, the acceleration vector $\mathbf{a}(t) = d\mathbf{v} / dt = 20\pi (-\cos \theta, -\sin \theta)\frac{d\theta}{dt} = -40\pi^2 (\cos \theta, \sin \theta) = -4\pi^2 \mathbf{R}(t)$ . Note that this is a special case of Problem 34.74.
