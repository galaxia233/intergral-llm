42.65 If $u = x^{2} - 2y^{2} + z^{3}$ and $x = \sin t, y = e^{t}, z = 3t,$ find $du / dt$

By the formula of Problem 42.64,

$$
\frac {d u}{d t} = \frac {\partial u}{\partial x} \frac {d x}{d t} + \frac {\partial u}{\partial y} \frac {d y}{d t} + \frac {\partial u}{\partial z} \frac {d z}{d t} = 2 x \cos t - 4 y e ^ {t} + 3 z ^ {2} (3) = 2 \sin t \cos t - 4 e ^ {t} \left(e ^ {t}\right) + 9 (3 t) ^ {2} = \sin 2 t - 4 e ^ {2 t} + 8 1 t ^ {2}.
$$
