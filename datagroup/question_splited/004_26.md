26.1 A quantity $y$ is said to grow or decay exponentially in time if $D_{t}y = Ky$ for some constant $K$ . ( $K$ is called the growth constant or decay constant, depending on whether it is positive or negative.) Show that $y = y_{0}e^{Kt}$ , where $y_{0}$ is the value of $y$ at time $t = 0$ .

\(
D_{T}\left(\frac{y}{e^{Kt}}\right) = \frac{e^{Kt} \cdot D_{t}y - y(Ke^{Kt})}{e^{2Kt}} = \frac{e^{Kt}(D_{t}y - Ky)}{e^{2Kt}} = 0.
\) Hence, $y / e^{Kt}$ is a constant $C$ , $y = Ce^{Kt}$ . When $t = 0$ , $y_0 = Ce^0 = C$ . Thus, $y = y_0e^{Kt}$ .
