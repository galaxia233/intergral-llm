39.10 Write the first three nonzero terms of the Maclaurin series for $\sin^{-1}x$ .

Let $f(x) = \sin^{-1} x$ . $f'(x) = (1 - x^2)^{-1/2}$ , $f''(x) = x(1 - x^2)^{-3/2}$ , $f'''(x) = (1 - x^2)^{-5/2}(2x^2 + 1)$ . $f^{(4)}(x) = 3x(1 - x^2)^{-7/2}(2x^2 + 3)$ , $f^{(5)}(x) = 3(1 - x^2)^{-9/2}(3 + 24x^2 + 8x^4)$ . Thus, $f(0) = 0$ , $f'(0) = 1$ , $f''(0) = 0$ , $f'''(0) = 1$ , $f^{(4)}(0) = 0$ , $f^{(5)}(0) = 9$ . Hence, the Maclaurin series is $x + \frac{1}{6}x^3 + \frac{3}{40}x^5 + \dots$ .
