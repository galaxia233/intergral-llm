42.112 Find an equation of the tangent plane to the ellipsoid $\frac{x^2}{a^2} +\frac{y^2}{b^2} +\frac{z^2}{c^2} = 1$ at a point $(x_0,y_0,z_0)$

By Problem 42.109, a normal vector to the tangent plane is $\left(\frac{2x_0}{a^2},\frac{2y_0}{b^2},\frac{2z_0}{c^2}\right)$ , or, better, the vector $\left(\frac{x_0}{a^2},\frac{y_0}{b^2},\frac{z_0}{c^2}\right)$ . Hence, an equation of the tangent plane is $\frac{x_0}{a^2} (x - x_0) + \frac{y_0}{b^2} (y - y_0) + \frac{z_0}{c^2} (z - z_0) = 0$ , or, equivalently $\frac{x_0}{a^2} x + \frac{y_0}{b^2} y + \frac{z_0}{c^2} z = 1$ .
