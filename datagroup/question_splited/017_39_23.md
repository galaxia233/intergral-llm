39.23 For what range of $x$ can $\cos x$ be replaced by the first three nonzero terms of its Maclaurin series to achieve four decimal place accuracy?

The first three nonzero terms of the Maclaurin series for $\cos x$ are $1 - x^{2} / 2 + x^{4} / 24$ . We must have $|R_{5}(x)| < 0.00005$ . Since $|f^{(5)}(c)| = |-\sin c| \leq 1$ , $|R_{5}(x)| = \frac{|f^{(5)}(c)|}{5!} |x|^{5} \leq |x|^{5} / 120$ . Therefore we require $|x|^{5} / 120 < 0.00005$ , $|x|^{5} < 0.006$ , $|x| < \sqrt[3]{0.006} \approx 0.3594$ .
