20.41 If, in a period of time $T$ , an object moves along the $x$ -axis from $x_{1}$ to $x_{2}$ , find a formula for its average velocity.

Let the initial and final time be $t_1$ and $t_2$ , with $T = t_2 - t_1$ . The average velocity is

$$
\frac {1}{t _ {2} - t _ {1}} \int_ {t _ {1}} ^ {t _ {2}} v d t = \frac {1}{T} x \Big ] _ {t _ {1}} ^ {t _ {2}} = \frac {1}{T} [ x (t _ {2}) - x (t _ {1}) ] = \frac {1}{T} (x _ {2} - x _ {1})
$$

Thus, as usual, the average velocity is the distance (more precisely, the displacement) divided by the time.
