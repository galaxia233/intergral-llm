29.19 $\int \frac{\sqrt{x^2 - 1}}{x} dx.$

Since $\sqrt{x^2 - 1}$ is in the integrand, we let $x = \sec \theta$ , $dx = \sec \theta \tan \theta d\theta$ . Then (Fig. 29-1), $\sqrt{x^2 - 1} = \tan \theta$ . Hence, $\int \frac{\sqrt{x^2 - 1}}{x} dx = \int \frac{\tan \theta}{\sec \theta} \sec \theta \tan \theta d\theta = \int \tan^2 \theta d\theta = \int (\sec^2 \theta - 1) d\theta = \tan \theta - \theta + C = \sqrt{x^2 - 1} - \sec^{-1} x + C.$
