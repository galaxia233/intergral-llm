26.17 If radioactive carbon-14 has a half-life of 5750 years, what will remain of 1 gram after 3000 years?

The amount of carbon is $y = y_0 e^{Kt}$ . We know that $5750K = -\ln 2$ . Since $y_0 = 1$ and $t = 3000$ , $y = e^{3000K} = e^{-\frac{4900}{3750} \ln 2} \approx e^{-0.3616} \approx 0.69657$ (from a table for $e^{-x}$ ). Thus, about 0.7 gram will remain.
