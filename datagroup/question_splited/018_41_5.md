41.5 Write an equation for the surface obtained by rotating a curve $f(y, z) = 0$ (in the $yz$ -plane) about the $z$ -axis.

This is a generalization of Problem 41.4. A point $(0, y^*, z^*)$ on the curve yields points $(x, y, z)$ , where $z = z^*$ and $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = y^*$ . Hence, the point $(x, y, z)$ satisfies the equation $f(\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}, z) = 0$ .
