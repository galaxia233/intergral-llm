41.6 Write an equation for the surface obtained by rotating the curve $\frac{y^2}{4} +\frac{z^2}{25} = 1$ (in the $yz$ -plane) about the $z$ -axis.

By Problem 41.5, the equation is obtained by replacing $y$ by $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ in the original equation. So, we get

$$
\frac {(\sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}) ^ {2}}{4} + \frac {z ^ {2}}{2 5} = 1 \quad \frac {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}{4} + \frac {z ^ {2}}{2 5} = 1 \quad \frac {x ^ {2}}{4} + \frac {y ^ {2}}{4} + \frac {z ^ {2}}{2 5} = 1
$$

an ellipsoid.
