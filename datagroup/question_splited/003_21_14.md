21.14 The region bounded by the parabola $x = y^2 + 2$ and the line $y = x - 8$ .

See Fig. 21-14. Let us find the points of intersection: $y + 8 = y^2 + 2$ , $y^2 - y - 6 = 0$ , $(y - 3)(y + 2) = 0$ , $y = 3$ or $y = -2$ . So, the points of intersection are (11, 3) and (6, -2). It is more convenient to integrate with respect to $y$ . The area is $\int_{-2}^{3} [(y + 8) - (y^2 + 2)] dy = \int_{-2}^{3} (y + 6 - y^2) dy = \left( \frac{1}{2} y^2 + 6y - \frac{1}{2} y^3 \right) \bigg]_{-2}^{3} = \left( \frac{9}{2} + 18 - 9 \right) - (2 - 12 + \frac{8}{3}) = \frac{125}{6}$ .
