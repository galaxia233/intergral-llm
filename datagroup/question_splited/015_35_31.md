35.31 Sketch the graph of $r = 1 + 2\cos \theta$

See Fig. 35-4. As $\theta$ goes from 0 to $\pi /2$ , $r$ decreases from 3 to 1. As $\theta$ increases further to $2\pi /3$ , $r$ decreases to 0. As $\theta$ goes on to $\pi$ , $r$ decreases to $-1$ , and then, as $\theta$ moves up to $4\pi /3$ , $r$ goes back up to 0. As $\theta$ moves on to $3\pi /2$ , $r$ goes up to 1, and, finally, as $\theta$ increases to $2\pi$ , $r$ grows to 3. This kind of graph is called a limacon.

$$
\begin{array}{c c c c c c c c} \theta & 0 & \pi / 2 & 2 \pi / 3 & \pi & 4 \pi / 3 & 3 \pi / 2 & 2 \pi \\ \hline r & 3 & 1 & 0 & - 1 & 0 & 1 & 3 \end{array}
$$
