46.60 Solve $y^{\prime \prime} + 2y^{\prime} + 2y = e^{3x}$

The general solution of the corresponding homogeneous equation was found in Problem 46.53: $y_{g} = (C_{1}\cos x + C_{2}\sin x)e^{-x}$ . Let us try $y = Ae^{3x}$ as a particular solution of the given equation. Then, $y' = 3Ae^{3x}$ and $y'' = 9Ae^{3x}$ . So $9Ae^{3x} + 6Ae^{3x} + 2Ae^{3x} = e^{3x}$ . Hence, $15A = 1$ , $A = \frac{1}{15}$ . Thus, a particular solution is $y = \frac{1}{15} e^{3x}$ , and the general solution is $\frac{1}{15} e^{3x} + (C_{1}\cos x + C_{2}\sin x)e^{-x}$ .
