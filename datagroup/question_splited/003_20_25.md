20.25 $\int_{-1}^{2}\sqrt{x + 2} x^2 dx.$

Let $u = x + 2$ , $x = u - 2$ , $du = dx$ . When $x = -1$ , $u = 1$ , and, when $x = 2$ , $u = 4$ . Then, by change of variables, $\int_{-1}^{2} \sqrt{x + 2} x^2 dx = \int_{1}^{4} \sqrt{u} (u - 2)^2 du = \int_{1}^{4} \sqrt{u} (u^2 - 4u + 4) du = \int_{1}^{4} (u^{5/2} - 4u^{3/2} + 4u^{1/2}) du = [ \frac{2}{7} u^{7/2} - 4( \frac{2}{5}) u^{5/2} + 4( \frac{2}{3}) u^{3/2} ]_1^4 = 2u^{3/2} ( \frac{1}{7} u^2 - \frac{4}{5} u + \frac{4}{3}) ]_1^4 = 2[8( \frac{16}{7} - \frac{16}{5} + \frac{4}{3}) - ( \frac{1}{7} - \frac{4}{5} + \frac{4}{3}) ] = 2( \frac{127}{7} - \frac{124}{5} + \frac{28}{3}) = \frac{562}{105}$ .
