42.90 If a point is moving on the curve of intersection of $x^{2} + 3xy + 3y^{2} = z^{2}$ and the plane $x - 2y + 4 = 0$ , how fast is it moving when $x = 2$ , if $x$ is increasing at the rate of 3 units per second?

$$
\begin{array}{l} \begin{array}{l} \text {F r o m} x - 2 y + 4 = 0, \frac {d x}{d t} - 2 \frac {d y}{d t} = 0. \text {S i n c e} d x / d t = 3, d y / d t = \frac {3}{2}. \text {F r o m} x ^ {2} + 3 x y + 3 y ^ {2} = z ^ {2}, \text {b y} \\ \text {t h e c h a i n r u l e ,} (2 x + 3 y) (d x / d t) + (3 x + 6 y) (d y / d t) = 2 z (d z / d t). \text {H e n c e ,} \end{array} \\ (2 x + 3 y) (3) + (3 x + 6 y) \left(\frac {3}{2}\right) = 2 z \frac {d z}{d t} \tag {*} \\ \end{array}
$$

When $x = 2$ , the original equations become $3y^{2} + 6y + 4 = z^{2}$ and $-2y + 6 = 0$ , yielding $y = 3$ , $z = \pm 7$ . Thus, by (*), $39 + 36 = 2z(dz/dt)$ , $dz/dt = \pm \frac{75}{14}$ . Hence, the speed

$$
\frac {d s}{d t} = \sqrt {\left(\frac {d x}{d t}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\frac {d y}{d t}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\frac {d z}{d t}\right) ^ {2}} = \sqrt {9 + \frac {9}{4} + 9 (\frac {2 5}{1 4}) ^ {2}} = 3 \sqrt {1 + \frac {1}{4} + \frac {6 2 5}{1 9 6}} = 3 \sqrt {\frac {8 7 0}{1 9 6}} = \frac {3}{1 4} \sqrt {8 7 0} \approx 6. 3 \text {u n i t s p e r s e c o n d}
$$
