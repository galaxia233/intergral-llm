21.11 The bounded region between the curve $y = \sqrt{x}$ and $y = x^3$ .

See Fig. 21-11. First we find the points of intersection: $\sqrt{x} = x^3$ , $x = x^6$ , $x(x^5 - 1) = 0$ , $x = 0$ or $x = 1$ . Thus, the points of intersection are $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 1)$ . The upper curve is $y = \sqrt{x}$ and the lower curve is $y = x^3$ . Hence, the area is $\int_{0}^{1} (\sqrt{x} - x^3) dx = \left( \frac{2}{3} x^{3/2} - \frac{1}{4} x^4 \right) \bigg]_{0}^{1} = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{12}$ .
