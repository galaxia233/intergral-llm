27.14 Let $\theta = \cos^{-1}\frac{1}{3}$ . Find $\sin \theta$ .

Since $\frac{1}{3} > 0$ , $\theta$ is in $(0, \pi/2)$ . $\cos \theta = \frac{1}{3}$ , and $\sin \theta = \pm \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{8}{9}} = \pm 2\sqrt{2}/3$ . Since $\theta$ is in the first quadrant, the + sign applies and $\sin \theta = 2\sqrt{2}/3$ .
