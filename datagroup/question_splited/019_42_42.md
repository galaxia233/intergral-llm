42.43 Give an example to show that the equation $f_{xy} = f_{yx}$ is not always valid.

$$
\text {L e t} \quad f (x, y) = \left\{ \begin{array}{c l} x y \left(\frac {x ^ {2} - y ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}\right) & \text {i f} (x, y) \neq (0, 0) \\ 0 & \text {i f} (x, y) = (0, 0) \end{array} \right.
$$

$$
f _ {x} (0, y) = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac {f (\Delta x , y) - f (0 , y)}{\Delta x} = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} y \frac {(\Delta x) ^ {2} - y ^ {2}}{(\Delta x) ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} = - y
$$

$$
f _ {y} (x, 0) = \lim  _ {\Delta y \rightarrow 0} \frac {f (x , \Delta y) - f (x , 0)}{\Delta y} = \lim  _ {\Delta y \rightarrow 0} x \frac {x ^ {2} - (\Delta y) ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + (\Delta y) ^ {2}} = x
$$

$$
\text {C o n s e c u t y l y}, \quad f _ {x y} (0, 0) = \lim  _ {\Delta y \rightarrow 0} \frac {f _ {x} (0 , \Delta y) - f _ {x} (0 , 0)}{\Delta y} = \lim  _ {\Delta y \rightarrow 0} \frac {- \Delta y - 0}{\Delta y} = - 1
$$

$$
f _ {y x} (0, 0) = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac {f _ {y} (\Delta x , 0) - f _ {y} (0 , 0)}{\Delta x} = \lim  _ {\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac {\Delta x - 0}{\Delta x} = 1
$$

Thus $f_{xy}(0,0) \neq f_{yx}(0,0)$ . (The conditions of Problem 42.34 are not met by this function.)
