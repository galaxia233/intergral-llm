43.1 Given a function $f(x, y)$ and a unit vector $\mathbf{u}$ , define the derivative of $f$ in the direction $\mathbf{u}$ , at a point $(x_0, y_0)$ , and state its connection with the gradient $\nabla f = (f_x, f_y)$ .

The ray in the direction $\mathbf{u}$ starting at the point $(x_0, y_0)$ is $(x_0, y_0) + t\mathbf{u} (t \geq 0)$ . The (directional) derivative, at $(x_0, y_0)$ , of the function $f$ in the direction $\mathbf{u}$ is the rate of change, at $(x_0, y_0)$ , of $f$ along that ray, that is, $\frac{d}{dt} f((x_0, y_0) + t\mathbf{u})$ , evaluated at $t = 0$ . It is equal to $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u}$ , the scalar projection of the gradient on $\mathbf{u}$ . (Similar definitions and results apply for functions $f$ of three or more variables.)
