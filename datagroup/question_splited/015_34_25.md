34.67 Give an example to show that if $\mathbf{R}(t)$ is a unit vector, then $\mathbf{R}'(t)$ need not be a unit vector (nor even a vector of constant length).

Consider $\mathbf{R}(t) = (\cos t^2, \sin t^2)$ . Then

$\left|\mathbf{R}(t)\right| = 1$ . However, $\mathbf{R}'(t) = (-2t\sin t^2,2t\cos t^2)$ and $\left|\mathbf{R}'(t)\right| = \sqrt{4t^2\sin^2t^2 + 4t^2\cos^2t^2} = 2|t|$
