40.32 Assume the direction angles of a vector $\mathbf{A}$ are equal. What are these angles?

We have $\alpha = \beta = \gamma$ . By Problem 40.29, $\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma = 1$ . So, $3\cos^2\alpha = 1$ , $\cos \alpha = \pm 1/\sqrt{3}$ . Therefore, either $\alpha = \beta = \gamma = \cos^{-1}(1/\sqrt{3})$ or $\alpha = \beta = \gamma = \pi - \cos^{-1}(1/\sqrt{3})$ .
