46.56 Given a linear differential equation of $n$ th order, with coefficients $a_{j} = a_{j}(x)$ :

$$
y ^ {(n)} + a _ {n - 1} y ^ {(n - 1)} + \dots + a _ {1} y ^ {\prime} + a _ {0} y = f (x) \tag {1}
$$

If $y_{p}$ is a particular solution of (1) and if $y_{g}$ is a general solution of the corresponding homogeneous equation

$$
y ^ {(n)} + a _ {n - 1} y ^ {(n - 1)} + \dots + a _ {1} y ^ {\prime} + a _ {0} y = 0 \tag {2}
$$

prove that $y_{p} + y_{g}$ is a general solution of (1).

Clearly, $y_{p} + y_{g}$ is a solution of (1), since $(y_{p} + y_{g})^{(k)} = y_{p}^{(k)} + y_{g}^{(k)}$ . Conversely, $y$ is a solution of (1), it is obvious, by the same reasoning, that $y - y_{p}$ is a solution of (2). Hence, $y - y_{p}$ has the form $y_{g}$ . So $y = y_{p} + y_{g}$ .
