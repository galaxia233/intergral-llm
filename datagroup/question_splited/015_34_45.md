34.87 At a given point on a curve, does the unit tangent vector depend on the direction in which the curve is being swept out?

We let $\mathbf{S}(t) = \mathbf{R}(-t)$ . Then $\mathbf{S}(t)$ traces the same curve as $\mathbf{R}(t)$ , but in the reverse direction. $\mathbf{S}'(t) = -\mathbf{R}'(-t)$ and $|\mathbf{S}'(t)| = |\mathbf{R}'(-t)|$ . Let $\mathbf{T}^*(t)$ be the unit tangent vector for the curve $\mathbf{S}(t)$ . Then $\mathbf{T}^*(t) = \mathbf{S}'(t) / |\mathbf{S}'(t)| = -\mathbf{R}'(-t) / |\mathbf{R}'(-t)| = -\mathbf{T}(-t)$ . Hence, at each point, the direction of the unit tangent vector has been reversed.
