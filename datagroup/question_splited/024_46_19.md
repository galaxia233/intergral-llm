46.19 Solve $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{2x^2 + y^2}{2xy + 3y^2}$ .

The variables are not separable. But the numerator and denominator are homogeneous in $x$ and $y$ of degree 2. Let $y = vx$ . Then $\frac{dy}{dx} = v + x\frac{dv}{dx}$ . Therefore, $v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = -\frac{2x^2 + x^2v^2}{2x(vx) + 3(vx)^2} = -\frac{2 + v^2}{2v + 3v^2}$ . Hence, $x\frac{dv}{dx} = -\left(\frac{2 + v^2}{2v + 3v^2} + v\right) = -\frac{2 + 3v^2 + 3v^3}{2v + 3v^2}$ . The variables $v$ and $x$ are separable. $\int \frac{(2v + 3v^2)dv}{2 + 3v^2 + 3v^3} = -\int \frac{dx}{x}, \quad \frac{1}{3}\ln |2 + 3v^2 + 3v^3| = -\ln |x| + \ln C_1, \quad |2 + 3v^2 + 3v^3|^{1/3} = C_1 / |x|, \quad |2 + 3v^2 + 3v^3| = C_2 / |x|^3, \quad |2 + 3(y/x)^2 + 3(y/x)^3| = C_2 / |x|^3, \quad |2x^3 + 3xy^2 + 3y^3| = C_2, \quad 2x^3 + 3xy^2 + 3y^3 = C.$
