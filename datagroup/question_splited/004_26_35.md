26.35 Solve Problem 26.34 with the modification that instead of pure water, brine containing $\frac{1}{10}$ pound per gallon is run into the tank at 20 gallons per minute, the mixture being drained off at the same rate.

As before, the tank is losing salt at the rate of $0.05y$ pounds per minute. However, it is also gaining salt at the rate of $20\left(\frac{1}{10}\right) = 2$ pounds per minute. Hence, $\frac{dy}{dt} = 2 - 0.05y$ , $\int \frac{1}{2 - 0.05y} dy = \int dt$ , $-20\ln |2 - 0.05y| = t + C$ . When $t = 0$ , $-20\ln |2 - 5| = C$ , $C = -20\ln 3$ . Hence, $-20\ln |2 - 0.05y| = t - 20\ln 3$ , $\ln |2 - 0.05y| = -0.05t + \ln 3$ , $\ln \left|\frac{2 - 0.05y}{3}\right| = -0.05t$ , $\left|\frac{2 - 0.05y}{3}\right| = e^{-0.05t}$ . Note that $y$ is always $>40$ , since $y(0) = 100$ and $y = 40$ is impossible. $[\ln |2 - 0.05(40)| = \ln 0$ is undefined.) Hence, $0.05y > 0.05(40) = 2$ , and $|2 - 0.05y| = 0.05y - 2$ . Thus, $(0.05y - 2)/3 = e^{-0.05t}$ . When $t = 30$ , $(0.05y - 2)/3 = e^{-1.5} \approx 0.2231$ , $0.05y \approx 2.6693$ , $y \approx 53.386$ pounds.
