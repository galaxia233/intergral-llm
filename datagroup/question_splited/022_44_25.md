44.25 Express the integral $I = \int_0^4\int_{x / 2}^2 f(x,y)dydx$ as an integral with the order of integration reversed.

For $0 \leq x \leq 4$ , the region of integration runs from $x/2$ to 2. Hence, the region of integration is the triangle indicated in Fig. 44-13. So, if we use strips parallel to the $x$ -axis, $I = \int_{0}^{2} \int_{0}^{2y} f(x, y) \, dx \, dy$ .
