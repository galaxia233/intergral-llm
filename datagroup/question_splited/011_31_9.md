31.32 The region between $y = x^2$ and $x = y^2$ (Fig. 31-10).   
The area $A = \int_{0}^{1} (\sqrt{x} - x^2) \, dx = \left( \frac{2}{3} x^{3/2} - \frac{1}{3} x^3 \right) \bigg]_{0}^{1} = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{3}$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_{0}^{1} x(\sqrt{x} - x^2) \, dx = \left( \frac{2}{3} x^{5/2} - \frac{1}{4} x^4 \right) \bigg]_{0}^{1} = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{20}$ . Hence, $\tilde{x} = \frac{\frac{3}{20}}{\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{9}{20}$ . By symmetry about the line $y = x$ , $\tilde{y} = \frac{9}{20}$ .
