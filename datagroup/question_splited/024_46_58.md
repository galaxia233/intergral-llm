46.58 Find a general solution of

$$
y ^ {\prime \prime} + 5 y ^ {\prime} + 4 y = 1 + x + x ^ {2} \tag {1}
$$

A general solution of $y'' + 5y' + 4y = 0$ is obtained from the auxiliary equation $u^2 + 5u + 4 = (u + 4)(u + 1) = 0$ . The roots are -1 and -4. Hence, the general solution is $C_1e^{-x} + C_2e^{-4x}$ . We guess that a particular solution of (1) is of the form $y = Ax^2 + Bx + C$ . Then, $y' = 2Ax + B$ , and $y'' = 2A$ . Therefore, $1 + x + x^2 = y'' + 5y' + 4y = 4Ax^2 + (10A + 4B)x + (2A + 5B + 4C)$ . Thus, $4A = 1$ , $A = \frac{1}{4}$ . Also, $1 = 10A + 4B$ , $1 = 10(\frac{1}{4}) + 4B$ , $B = -\frac{3}{8}$ . Finally, $1 = 2A + 5B + 4C$ , $C = \frac{1}{4}(1 - 2A - 5B) = \frac{1}{4}(1 - \frac{1}{2} + \frac{15}{8}) = \frac{19}{32}$ . So a particular solution is $\frac{1}{4}x^2 - \frac{3}{8}x + \frac{19}{32} = \frac{1}{32}(8x^2 - 12x + 19)$ . The general solution is $\frac{1}{32}(8x^2 - 12x + 19) + C_1e^{-x} + C_2e^{-4x}$ .
