43.7 Find the derivative of $f(x, y, z) = x^3 + y^3z$ at $(-1, 2, 1)$ in the direction toward $(0, 3, 3)$ .

A vector in the indicated direction is $(0,3,3) - (-1,2,1) = (1,1,2)$ . The unit vector in that direction is $\mathbf{u} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} (1,1,2)$ . $\nabla f = (3x^2, 3y^2z, y^3) = (3,12,8)$ . Hence, is $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = (3,12,8) \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} (1,2,2) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} (31)$ .
