20.59 If $g$ is continuous, which of the following integrals are equal?

(a) $\int_{a}^{b} g(x) dx$ (b) $\int_{a + 1}^{b + 1} g(x - 1) dx$ (c) $\int_{0}^{b - a} g(x + a) dx$

Let $u = x - 1$ , $du = dx$ . Then $\int_{a + 1}^{b + 1} g(x - 1) \, dx = \int_{a}^{b} g(u) \, du = \int_{a}^{b} g(x) \, dx$ . Let $v = x + a$ , $dv = dx$ . Then $\int_{0}^{b - a} g(x + a) \, dx = \int_{a}^{b} g(v) \, dv = \int_{a}^{b} g(x) \, dx$ . Thus, all three integrals are equal to each other.
