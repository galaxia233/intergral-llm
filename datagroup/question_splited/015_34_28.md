34.70 As a converse to Problem 34.69, show that if $\mathbf{R}'(u) = \mathbf{B} \neq \mathbf{0}$ for all $u$ , then $\mathbf{R}(u) = \mathbf{A} + u\mathbf{B}$ , a straight line.

Let $\mathbf{R}(u) = (f(u), g(u))$ and $\mathbf{B} = (b_1, b_2)$ . Then $f'(u) = b_1$ and $g'(u) = b_2$ . Hence, $f(u) = b_1 u + a_1$ and $g(u) = b_2 u + a_2$ . So, $\mathbf{R}(u) = (b_1 u + a_1, b_2 u + a_2) = \mathbf{A} + u \mathbf{B}$ .
