42.103 Find a general solution of $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y} = 0$ .

Let $L(x, y) = \partial f / \partial y$ . Then $\partial L / \partial x = 0$ . By Problem 42.98, $L(x, y) = g(y)$ for some $g$ . So $\partial f / \partial y = g(y)$ . By an analogue of Problem 42.100, there are functions $A(x)$ and $B(y)$ such that $f(x, y) = A(x) + B(y)$ . Conversely, any such function $f(x, y) = A(x) + B(y)$ , where $A$ and $B$ are twice differentiable, satisfies $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y} = 0$ .
