20.5 Show that $\int_0^b x dx = \frac{b^2}{2}$ by the direct definition of the integral.

1. Divide the interval $[0, b]$ into $n$ equal subintervals of length $b / n$ , by the points $0 = x_0 < b / n < 2b / n < \dots < nb / n = x_n = b$ . In the $i$ th subinterval choose $x_i^*$ to be the right-hand endpoint $ib / n$ . Then an approximating sum is $\sum_{j=1}^{n} \frac{ib}{n} \cdot \frac{b}{n} = \frac{b^2}{n^2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{b^2}{n^2} \frac{n(n+1)}{2} = \frac{b^2}{2} \left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right)$ . As $n \to +\infty$ , the approximating sum approaches $b^2 / 2$ , which is, therefore, the value of the integral.
