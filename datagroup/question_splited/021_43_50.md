43.50 For all positive $x, y, z$ such that $xyz^2 = 2500$ , what is the smallest value of $x + y + z$ ?

This problem is the dual of Problem 43.34; so we already know the solution: $\min (x + y + z) = 20$ . Let us verify this solution by the gradient method. We want to minimize $F(x, y) = x + y + (50 / \sqrt{xy})$ over $x > 0$ , $y > 0$ . Setting $F_x = 1 - (25 / \sqrt{x^3y}) = 0$ , $F_y = 1 - (25 / \sqrt{xy^3}) = 0$ , and solving, we obtain $x = y = 5$ . Hence, $z = \frac{50}{5} = 10$ , and $x + y + z = 20$ .
