41.31 Describe the graph of the function $f(x, y) = \sqrt{a^2 - x^2 - y^2}$ , where $a > 0$ .

This is the graph of $z = \sqrt{a^2 - x^2 - y^2}$ , which, for $z \geq 0$ , is equivalent to $z^2 = a^2 - x^2 - y^2$ , $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = a^2$ , the equation of the sphere with center at the origin and radius $a$ . Hence, the graph is the upper half of that sphere (including the circle $x^2 + y^2 = a^2$ in the $xy$ -plane).
