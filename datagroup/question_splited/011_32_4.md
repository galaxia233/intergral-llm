32.4 For $p > 1$ , is $\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{\ln x}{x^{p}} dx$ convergent?

First we evaluate $\int \left[\left(\ln x\right) / x^{p}\right]dx$ by integration by parts. Let $u = \ln x, d v = (1 / x^{p}) dx, d u = (1 / x) dx,$ $v = \frac{1}{1 - p} \frac{1}{x^{p-1}}$ . Thus, $\int \frac{\ln x}{x^p} dx = \frac{1}{1 - p} \frac{\ln x}{x^{p-1}} - \int \frac{1}{1 - p} \frac{1}{x^p} dx = \frac{1}{1 - p} \frac{\ln x}{x^{p-1}} - \frac{1}{(1 - p)^2} \frac{1}{x^{p-1}}$ . Hence,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {1} ^ {\infty} \frac {\ln x}{x ^ {p}} d x = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \int_ {1} ^ {v} \frac {\ln x}{x ^ {p}} d x = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \left(\frac {1}{1 - p} \frac {\ln x}{x ^ {p - 1}} - \frac {1}{(1 - p) ^ {2}} \frac {1}{x ^ {p - 1}}\right) \Bigg ] _ {1} ^ {v} \\ = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \left[ \frac {1}{1 - p} \frac {\ln v}{v ^ {p - 1}} - \frac {1}{(1 - p) ^ {2}} \frac {1}{v ^ {p - 1}} \right] - \left[ - \frac {1}{(1 - p) ^ {2}} \right] = \frac {1}{(1 - p) ^ {2}} \\ \end{array}
$$

[In the last step, we used L'Hôpital's rule to evaluate $\lim_{v\to +\infty}\frac{\ln v}{v^{p - 1}} = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\frac{1 / v}{(p - 1)v^{p - 2}} = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\frac{1}{p - 1} \times \frac{1}{v^{p - 1}} = 0.$ ] Thus, the integral converges for all $p > 1$ .
