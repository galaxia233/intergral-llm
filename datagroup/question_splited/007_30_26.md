30.26 Find $\int \frac{x^2 + 3}{(x - 1)^3(x + 1)} dx.$

$\frac{x^2 + 3}{(x - 1)^3(x + 1)} = \frac{A}{x + 1} + \frac{B}{x - 1} + \frac{C}{(x - 1)^2} + \frac{D}{(x - 1)^3}$ . Then $x^2 + 3 = A(x - 1)^3 + B(x + 1)(x - 1)^2 + C(x + 1)(x - 1) + D(x + 1)$ . Let $x = -1$ . Then $4 = -8A$ , $A = -\frac{1}{2}$ . Let $x = 1$ . Then $4 = 2D$ , $D = 2$ . Equate coefficients of $x^3$ : $0 = A + B$ , $B = -A = \frac{1}{2}$ . Equate constant coefficients: $3 = -A + B - C + D$ , $C = -A + B + D - 3 = 0$ . Thus, $\frac{x^2 + 3}{(x - 1)^3(x + 1)} = -\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{x + 1} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{x - 1} + \frac{2}{(x - 1)^3}$ , and $\int \frac{(x^2 + 3)dx}{(x - 1)^3(x + 1)} = -\frac{1}{2}\ln |x + 1| + \frac{1}{2}\ln |x - 1| - \frac{1}{(x - 1)^2} + C_1 = \frac{1}{2}\ln \left|\frac{x - 1}{x + 1}\right| - \frac{1}{(x - 1)^2} + C_1.$
