34.106 Assume that, for a curve $\mathbf{R}(t)$ , $v = ds / dt > 0$ . Then the acceleration vector $\mathbf{a}$ can be represented as the following linear combination of the perpendicular vectors $\mathbf{T}$ and $\mathbf{N}$ : $\mathbf{a} = (d^2 s / dt^2)\mathbf{T} + (v^2 /\rho)\mathbf{N}$ . The coefficients of $\mathbf{T}$ and $\mathbf{N}$ are called, respectively, the tangential and normal components of the acceleration vector. (Since the normal component $v^{2} / \rho$ is positive, the acceleration vector points "inside" the curve, just as $\mathbf{N}$ does.)

By definition of $\mathbf{T}$ , $\mathbf{T} = \mathbf{R}'(t) / v$ . So, $\mathbf{R}'(t) = v\mathbf{T}$ . By the product rule, $\mathbf{R}''(t) = v\mathbf{T}' + (dv/dt)\mathbf{T}$ . By Problem 34.105, $\mathbf{T}' = (v/\rho)\mathbf{N}$ . Hence, $\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{R}''(t) = (d^2 s/dt^2)\mathbf{T} + (v^2/\rho)\mathbf{N}$ .
