20.56 Given $H(x) = \int_{1}^{x}\frac{1}{1 + t^2} dt,$ find $H(1)$ and $H^{\prime}(1)$ , and show that $H(4) - H(2) <   \frac{3}{5}.$

H(1)=1 1 H'(x) = 1 1+ x2; so, H'(1) = ½. By the Mean-Value Theorem, H(4)-H(2) = (4-2)·H'(c) for some c in (2,4). Now, H'(c) = 1 1+c² < 1 1+2² = 1/5. Hence, H(4)-H(2)<².
