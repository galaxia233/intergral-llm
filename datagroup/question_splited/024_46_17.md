46.17 Solve $y' = (x + y)^2$ . (As usual, $y' \equiv \frac{dy}{dx}$ .)

The variables do not separate. Try the substitution $z = x + y$ . Then, $z' = 1 + y'$ . So $z' - 1 = z^2$ , $\frac{dz}{dx} = z^2 + 1$ . Now, the variables $x$ and $z$ are separable. $\int \frac{dz}{z^2 + 1} = \int dx$ , $\tan^{-1} z = x + C$ , $z = \tan (x + C)$ , $x + y = \tan (x + C)$ , $y = \tan (x + C) - x$ .
