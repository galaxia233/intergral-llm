41.7 Write an equation of the surface obtained by rotating the hyperbola $\frac{x^2}{9} - \frac{y^2}{4} = 1$ (in the $xy$ -plane) about the $x$ -axis.

By analogy with Problem 41.5, we replace $y$ by $\sqrt{y^2 + z^2}$ , obtaining $\frac{x^2}{9} - \frac{y^2 + z^2}{4} = 1$ , $\frac{x^2}{9} - \frac{y^2}{4} - \frac{z^2}{4} = 1$ . (This surface is called a hyperboloid of two sheets.)
