44.12 Evaluate $I = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} y^2 \, dA$ , where $\mathcal{R}$ is the region bounded by $y = 2x$ , $y = 5x$ , and $x = 2$ .

The lines $y = 2x$ and $y = 5x$ intersect at the origin. For $0 < x \leq 1$ , the region runs from $y = 2x$ up to $y = 5x$ (Fig. 44-3). Hence, $I = \int_{0}^{1} \int_{2x}^{5x} y^{2} dy dx = \int_{0}^{1} \frac{1}{3} y^{3} \bigg]_{2x}^{5x} dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} (125x^{3} - 8x^{3}) dx = \left( \frac {1}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {1} (125x ^ {3} - 8x ^ {3}) dx \right)^{2} dx = \frac {1}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {1} (125x ^ {3} - 8x ^ {3}) dx = \frac {1}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {1} (125x ^ {3} - 8x ^ {3}) dx = \frac {1}{3} \int_ {0} ^ {1} (125x ^ {3} - 8x ^ {3}) dx = 0$ .
