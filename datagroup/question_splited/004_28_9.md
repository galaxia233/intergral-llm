28.9 $\int e^{ax}\cos bxdx.$

Let $u = \cos bx$ , $dv = e^{ax} dx$ , $du = -b \sin bx$ , $v = (1/a)e^{ax}$ . Then $\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx = (1/a)e^{ax} \cos bx + (b/a)\int e^{ax} \sin bx \, dx$ . Apply integration by parts to the latter: $u = \sin bx$ , $dv = e^{ax} \, dx$ , $du = b \cos bx$ , $v = (1/a)e^{ax}$ . So $\int e^{ax} \sin bx \, dx = (1/a)e^{ax} \sin bx - (b/a)\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx$ . Hence, by substitution, $\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx = (1/a)e^{ax} \cos bx + (b/a)[(1/a)e^{ax} \sin bx - (b/a)\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx] = (1/a)e^{ax} \cos bx + (b/a)^2e^{ax} \sin bx - (b^2/a^2)\int e^{x} \cos bx \, dx$ . Thus, $(1 + b^2/a^2)\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx = (e^{ax}/a^2)(a \cos bx + b \sin bx) + C$ , $\int e^{ax} \cos bx \, dx = [e^{ax}/(a^2 + b^2)](a \cos bx + b \sin bx) + C_1$ .
