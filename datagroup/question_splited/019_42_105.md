42.105 Show that the tangent plane to a surface $z = f(x, y)$ at a point $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$ has a normal vector $(f_x(x_0, y_0), f_y(x_0, y_0), -1)$ .

One vector in the tangent plane at $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$ is $(1, 0, f_x)$ , and another is $(0, 1, f_y)$ . Hence, a normal vector is $(0, 1, f_y) \times (1, 0, f_x) = (f_x, f_y, -1)$ .
