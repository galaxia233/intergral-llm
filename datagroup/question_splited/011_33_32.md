33.31 Show that, if $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{C}$ and $\mathbf{A} \neq \mathbf{0}$ , we cannot conclude that $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{C}$ .

If $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{C}$ , then $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{C}) = 0$ . So, $\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{C}$ can be any vector perpendicular to $\mathbf{A}$ ; $\mathbf{B} - \mathbf{C}$ need not be $\mathbf{0}$ .
