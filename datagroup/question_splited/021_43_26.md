43.26 Find the relative maxima and minima of $f(x, y) = x^3 + y^3 - 3xy$ .

$f_{x} = 3x^{2} - 3y, f_{y} = 3y^{2} - 3x.$ Setting $f_{x} = 0, f_{y} = 0,$ we have $x^{2} = y$ and $y^{2} = x$ . So, $y^{4} = y$ and, therefore, either $y = 0$ or $y = 1$ . So, the critical points are $(0,0)$ and $(1,1)$ . $f_{xy} = -3, f_{xx} = 6x$ , $f_{yy} = 6y$ . At $(0,0)$ , $\Delta = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2 = -9 < 0$ . Therefore, by Problem 43.23, there is neither a relative maximum nor a relative minimum at $(0,0)$ . At $(1,1)$ , $\Delta = 36 - 9 > 0$ . Also, $f_{xx} + f_{yy} = 12 > 0$ . Hence, by Problem 43.23, there is a relative minimum at $(1,1)$ .
