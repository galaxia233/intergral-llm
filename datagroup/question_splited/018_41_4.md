41.4 Write the equation for the surface obtained by revolving the curve $z = y^2$ (in the $yz$ -plane) about the $z$ -axis.

When the point $(0, y^{*}, z^{*})$ on $z = y^{2}$ is rotated about the $z$ -axis (see Fig. 41-4), consider any resulting point $(x, y, z)$ . Clearly, $z = z^{*}$ and $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = y^{*}$ . Hence, $x^2 + y^2 = (y^*)^2 = z^* = z$ . So, the resulting points satisfy the equation $z = x^2 + y^2$ .
