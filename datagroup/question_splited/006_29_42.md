29.42 Assume that an object $A$ , starting at $(a,0)$ , is connected by a string of constant length $a$ to an object $B$ , starting at $(0,0)$ . As $B$ moves up the $y$ -axis, $A$ traces out a curve called a tractrix. Find its equation.

Let $A$ be at $(x,y)$ . The string must be tangent to the curve. From Fig. 29-11, the slope of the tangent line is $y' = -\frac{\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}}{x}$ . So, by Problem 29.41, $y = -\int \frac{\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}}{x} dx = -a \ln \left|\frac{a - \sqrt{a^2 - x^2}}{x}\right| - \sqrt{a^2 - x^2} + C$ . Since $y = 0$ when $x = a$ , $C = 0$ .
