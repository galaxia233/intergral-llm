32.58 Calculate $L\{e^t\}$

$$
\begin{array}{l} L \left\{e ^ {t} \right\} = \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} e ^ {- s t} e ^ {t} d t = \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} e ^ {(1 - s) t} d t = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \int_ {0} ^ {v} e ^ {(1 - s) t} d t = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \frac {1}{1 - s} e ^ {(1 - s) t} \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {v} \\ = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \frac {1}{1 - s} e ^ {(1 - s) v} - \frac {1}{1 - s} = \frac {1}{s - 1} \\ \end{array}
$$

The last limit is valid when $s > 1$ . Thus, $L\{e^t\} = 1 / (s - 1)$ (defined for $s > 1$ ).
