27.71 Sketch the graph of $y = \tan^{-1}x - \ln \sqrt{1 + x^2}$

See Fig. 27-7.

![](images/019ab3707b57d57c3a64e3156015c502227209eb8ba4664a25a7b3bbb8289b1f.jpg)  
Fig. 27-7

$$
y = \tan^ {- 1} x - \frac {1}{2} \ln (1 + x ^ {2})
$$

$$
y ^ {\prime} = \frac {1}{1 + x ^ {2}} - \frac {1}{2} \frac {1}{1 + x ^ {2}} \cdot 2 x = \frac {1 - x}{1 + x ^ {2}}
$$

$$
y ^ {\prime \prime} = \frac {(1 + x ^ {2}) (- 1) - (1 - x) (2 x)}{(1 + x ^ {2}) ^ {2}} = \frac {- 1 - x ^ {2} - 2 x + 2 x ^ {2}}{(1 + x ^ {2}) ^ {2}} = \frac {x ^ {2} - 2 x - 1}{(1 + x ^ {2}) ^ {2}}
$$

For the only critical number, $x = 1$ , $y'' = -\frac{1}{2} < 0$ , and, therefore, there is a relative maximum at $x = 1$ , $y = \pi / 4 - \frac{1}{2} \ln 2 \approx 0.4$ . Note that $y(0) = 0$ . Also, as $x \to \pm \infty$ , $y \to -\infty$ . Setting $y'' = 0$ , we find two inflection points at $x = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}$ .
