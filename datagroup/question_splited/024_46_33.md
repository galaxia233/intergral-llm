46.33 Find a curve that passes through $(0,2)$ and has at each point $(x,y)$ a tangent line with slope $y - 2e^{-x}$ .

$\frac{dy}{dx} = y - 2e^{-x}$ . $\frac{dy}{dx} - y = -2e^{-x}$ . Let $\rho = \exp \left[\int (-1) dx\right] = e^{-x}$ . Then $\int \rho q(x) dx = -2\int e^{-2x} dx = e^{-2x}$ . Therefore, $y = e^{x}(e^{-2x} + C) = e^{-x} + Ce^{x}$ . Since the curve passes through $(0, 2)$ , $2 = 1 + C$ , $C = 1$ . Hence, $y = e^{-x} + e^{x} = 2 \cosh x$ .
