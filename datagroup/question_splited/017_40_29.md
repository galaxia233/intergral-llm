40.29 Let vector $\mathbf{A} = (a, b, c)$ have direction cosines $\cos \alpha, \cos \beta, \cos \gamma$ . Show that $\cos^2 \alpha + \cos^2 \beta + \cos^2 \gamma = 1$ .

$\cos^2 \alpha + \cos^2 \beta + \cos^2 \gamma = \frac{a^2}{|\mathbf{A}|^2} + \frac{b^2}{|\mathbf{A}|^2} + \frac{c^2}{|\mathbf{A}|^2} = \frac{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}{|\mathbf{A}|^2} = \frac{|\mathbf{A}|^2}{|\mathbf{A}|^2} = 1.$
