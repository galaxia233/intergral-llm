40.69 Find the vector representation, the parametric equations, and the rectangular equations for the line through the points $P(1, -2, 5)$ and $Q(3, 4, 6)$ .

If $R$ is any point on the line, then $\overrightarrow{PR} = t\overrightarrow{PQ}$ for some scalar $t$ (see Fig. 40-6). Hence, $\overrightarrow{OR} = \overrightarrow{OP} + \overrightarrow{PR} = OP + t\overrightarrow{PQ}$ . Thus, $(1,2,-5) + t(2,6,1)$ is a vector function that generates the line. A parametric form is $x = 1 + 2t$ , $y = 2 + 6t$ , $z = -5 + t$ . If we eliminate $t$ from these equations, we obtain the rectangular equations $\frac{x - 1}{2} = \frac{y - 2}{6} = \frac{z + 5}{1}$ .
