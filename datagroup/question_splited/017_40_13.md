40.13 Show that the three points $P(1,2,3)$ , $Q(4, -5,2)$ , and $R(0,0,0)$ are the vertices of a right triangle.

$\overline{PR} = \sqrt{1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{14}, \overline{QR} = \sqrt{4^2 + (-5)^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{45}, \overline{PQ} = \sqrt{(1 - 4)^2 + [2 - (-5)]^2 + (3 - 2)^2} = \sqrt{59}$ . Thus, $\overline{PQ}^2 = \overline{PR}^2 + \overline{QR}^2$ . Hence, by the converse of the Pythagorean theorem, $\triangle PQR$ is a right triangle with right angle at $R$ .
