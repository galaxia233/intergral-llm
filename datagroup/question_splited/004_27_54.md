27.54 $\int \frac{x^3dx}{x^2 - 2x + 4}.$

Dividing $x^3$ by $x^2 - 2x + 4$ , we obtain $x + 2 - \frac{8}{x^2 - 2x + 4}$ . Hence,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int \frac {x ^ {3} d x}{x ^ {2} - 2 x + 4} = \int \left(x + 2 - \frac {8}{x ^ {2} - 2 x + 4}\right) d x = \frac {1}{2} x ^ {2} + 2 x - 8 \int \frac {1}{(x - 1) ^ {2} + 3} d x \\ = \frac {1}{2} x ^ {2} + 2 x - 8 \cdot \frac {1}{\sqrt {3}} \tan^ {- 1} \frac {x - 1}{\sqrt {3}} + C = \frac {1}{2} x ^ {2} + 2 x - \frac {8}{\sqrt {3}} \tan^ {- 1} \frac {x - 1}{\sqrt {3}} + C. \\ \end{array}
$$
