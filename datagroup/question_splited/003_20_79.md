20.79 Find the distance traveled by an object moving along a line with velocity $v = \sin \pi t$ from $t = \frac{1}{2}$ to $t = 2$ .

The distance is $s = \int_{1/2}^{2} |v| dt$ . Observe that $\sin \pi t$ is positive for $\frac{1}{2} < t < 1$ and negative for $1 < t < 2$ . Hence,

$$
\begin{array}{l} s = \int_ {1 / 2} ^ {1} \sin \pi t d t + \int_ {1} ^ {2} (- \sin \pi t) d t = - \left. \frac {1}{\pi} \cos \pi t \right] _ {1 / 2} ^ {1} + \frac {1}{\pi} \cos \pi t \Bigg ] _ {1} ^ {2} \\ = - \frac {1}{\pi} (- 1 - 0) + \frac {1}{\pi} [ 1 - (- 1) ] = \frac {1}{\pi} + \frac {2}{\pi} = \frac {3}{\pi} \\ \end{array}
$$
