34.20 Find the angle at which the cycloid $x = a\theta -a$ sin $\theta ,y = a - a$ cos $\theta$ meets the $x$ -axis at the origin.

$dx / d\theta = a - a\cos \theta, dy / d\theta = a\sin \theta.$ Hence, $dy / dx = \sin \theta /(1 - \cos \theta) = \cot \theta /2,\lim_{\theta \to 0^{+}}\cot (\theta /2) = +\infty .$ Therefore, the cycloid comes in vertically at the origin.
