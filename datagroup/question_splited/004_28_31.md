28.31 Use the solution to Problem 28.30 to establish the following bounds on $e$ : $2.5 \leq e \leq 2.823$ .

In Problem 28.30, it was shown that $\int_{1}^{e}\left(\frac{\ln x}{x}\right)^{2}dx = 2 - 5 / e$ . By Problem 24.59, $1 / e$ is the maximum value of $\ln x / x$ . Hence, $\int_{1}^{e}\left(\frac{\ln x}{x}\right)^{2}dx\leq \frac{e - 1}{e^{2}}$ . {In general, $\int_{a}^{b}f(x)dx\leq M(b - a)$ if $M$ is an upper bound of $f(x)$ on $[a,b]$ .} Thus, $0\leq 2 - 5 / e\leq (e - 1) / e^2$ . The left-hand inequality gives $e\geq 2.5$ . The right-hand inequality gives $2e^{2} - 5e\leq e - 1$ , $2e^{2} - 6e + 1\leq 0$ . Since the roots of $2x^{2} - 6x + 1 = 0$ are $(3\pm \sqrt{7}) / 2$ , $e\leq (3 + \sqrt{7}) / 2 < 2.823$ (since $\sqrt{7} < 2.646$ ).
