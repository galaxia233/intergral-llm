26.37 The number of bacteria in a culture doubles every hour. How long does it take for a thousand bacteria to produce a billion?

$y = y_0e^{Kt}$ . We are told that $y = 2y_0$ when $t = 1$ . Hence, $2y_0 = y_0e^K$ , $2 = e^K$ . If we start with 1000, we obtain a billion when $10^9 = 10^3 e^{Kt}$ , $10^6 = (e^K)^t = 2^t$ , $\ln (10^6) = t\ln 2$ , $6\ln 10 = t\ln 2$ , $t = (6\ln 10) / \ln 2 \approx 6(2.3026) / 0.6931 \approx 19.9$ hours.
