27.78 A billboard, 54 feet wide, is perpendicular to a straight road and is 18 feet from the nearest point $A$ on the road. As a motorist approaches the billboard along the road, at what point does she see the billboard in the widest angle?   
In Fig. 27-10, let $x$ be the distance of the motorist from $A$ , and let $\theta$ be her angle of vision of the billboard. Then $\theta = \cot^{-1}(x / 72) - \cot^{-1}(x / 18)$ . Then $D_x\theta = -1 / [1 + (x / 72)^2] \cdot \frac{1}{72} + 1 / [1 + (x / 18)^2] \cdot \frac{1}{18} = 18 / [(18)^2 + x^2] - 72 / [(72)^2 + x^2]$ . Setting $D_x\theta = 0$ , we obtain $(72)^2 + x^2 = 4(18)^2 + 4x^2$ , $x^2 = 81 \cdot 16$ , $x = 36$ . The first-derivative test will verify that this yields a maximum value of $\theta$ .
