34.84 Compute $\mathbf{N}(t)$ for the curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (2\cos t^2, 2\sin t^2)$ .

$\mathbf{R}^{\prime}(t) = (-4t\sin t^{2},4t\cos t^{2}) = 4t(-\sin t^{2},\cos t^{2}).$ So, $|\mathbf{R}'(t)| = 4|t|$ . Thus, $\mathbf{T}(t) = \frac{t}{|t|} (-\sin t^2,\cos t^2)$ . Hence, $\mathbf{T}'(t) = \frac{t}{|t|} (-2t\cos t^2, - 2t\sin t^2) = -\frac{2t^2}{|t|} (\cos t^2,\sin t^2)$ . So, $|\mathbf{T}'(t)| = 2|t|$ , and, therefore, $\mathbf{N}(t) = \mathbf{T}'(t) / |\mathbf{T}'(t)| = -(\cos t^2,\sin t^2)$ . Again, $\mathbf{N}(t)$ has the same direction as the acceleration vector, that is, it points toward the center of the circle traced out by $\mathbf{R}(t)$ .
