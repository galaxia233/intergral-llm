40.51 If $\mathbf{A},\mathbf{B},\mathbf{C}$ are mutually perpendicular, show that $\mathbf{A}\times (\mathbf{B}\times \mathbf{C}) = \mathbf{0}$

$\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}$ is a vector perpendicular to the plane of $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ . By hypothesis, $\mathbf{A}$ is also such a vector and, therefore, $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}$ are parallel. Hence, $\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \mathbf{0}$ .
