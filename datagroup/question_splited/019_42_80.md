42.80 Show that any function $f(x, y)$ that is homogeneous of degree $n$ is separable in polar coordinates and has the form $f(x, y) = r^n \Theta(\theta)$ .

1 Choose new variables $u = \ln x, v = y / x,$ and write

$$
\frac {f (x , y)}{(\sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}) ^ {n}} \equiv \phi (u, v)
$$

Replacing $x$ and $y$ by $tx$ and $ty$ ( $t > 0$ ), we have

$$
\phi (u + \ln t, v) = \frac {f (t x , t y)}{[ \sqrt {(t x) ^ {2} + (t y) ^ {2}} ] ^ {n}} = \frac {t ^ {n} f (x , y)}{t ^ {n} (\sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}) ^ {n}} = \phi (u, v)
$$

Because $\ln t$ assumes all real values, the above equation can hold only if $\phi$ is independent of $u$ ; i.e., $\phi(u, v) = \psi(v)$ . Hence, $f(x, y) = (\sqrt{x^2 + y^2})^n \psi(y/x) = r^n \psi(\tan \theta) = r^n \Theta(\theta)$ .
