44.82 Find the center of mass of the first-quadrant part of the disk of radius $a$ with center at the origin, if the density function is $y$ .

The mass $M = \int_{0}^{a}\int_{0}^{\sqrt{a^{2} - y^{2}}}y  dx  dy = \int_{0}^{a}y\sqrt{a^{2} - y^{2}}  dy = \left. - \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{2}{3} (a^{2} - y^{2})^{3 / 2}\right]_{0}^{a} = -\frac{1}{3} (-a^{3}) = \frac{1}{3} a^{3}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis is

$$
\begin{array}{l} M _ { x } = \iint_ {\mathcal {R}} y \cdot y d A = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \int_ {0} ^ {a} y ^ {2} \cdot r d r d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \int_ {0} ^ {a} r ^ {3} \sin \theta d r d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {1}{4} a ^ {4} \sin^ {2} \theta d \theta \\ = \frac {1}{4} a ^ {4} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {1 - \cos 2 \theta}{2} d \theta = \frac {1}{8} a ^ {4} \left(\theta - \frac {1}{2} \sin 2 \theta\right) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} = \frac {a ^ {4}}{8} \left(\frac {\pi}{2}\right) = \frac {\pi a ^ {4}}{1 6} \\ \end{array}
$$

Hence, $\bar{y} = \frac{M_x}{M} = \frac{\pi a^4 / 16}{a^3 / 3} = \frac{3\pi a}{16}$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^a \int_0^{\sqrt{a^2 - y^2}} xy \, dx \, dy = \int_0^a \left[\frac{1}{2} y x^2\right]_0^{y^2} dy = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^a y (a^2 - y^2) \, dy = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{a^2}{2} y^2 - \frac{y^4}{4}\right) \int_0^a = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{a^4}{2} - \frac{a^4}{4}\right) = \frac{a^4}{8}$ . Therefore, $\bar{x} = \frac{M_y}{M} = \frac{a^4 / 8}{a^3 / 3} = \frac{3a}{8}$ . Hence, the center of mass is $\left(\frac{3a}{8}, \frac{3\pi a}{16}\right)$ .
