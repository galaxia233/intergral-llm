【2369】 $\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sin^{p}x\cos^{q}x}.$

解 先考虑积分 $\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sin^{p}x\cos^{q}x}$ 对于任何 $q$ 值，由于

$$
\lim  _ {x \rightarrow + 0} x ^ {p} \frac {1}{\sin^ {p} x \cos^ {q} x} = \lim  _ {x \rightarrow + 0} \left(\frac {x}{\sin x}\right) ^ {p} \lim  _ {x \rightarrow + 0} \left(\frac {1}{\cos^ {q} x}\right) = 1,
$$

故积分 $\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sin^{p}x\cos^{q}x}$ 仅当 $p < 1$ （ $q$ 为任意值）时收敛.

再考虑积分 $\int_{\frac{\pi}{4}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sin^{p} x \cos^{q} x}$ . 对于任何 $p$ 值，由于

$$
\lim  _ {x \rightarrow \frac {\pi}{2} - 0} \left(\frac {\pi}{2} - x\right) ^ {q} \frac {1}{\sin^ {p} x \cos^ {q} x} = \lim  _ {x \rightarrow \frac {\pi}{2} - 0} \left(\frac {\frac {\pi}{2} - x}{\cos x}\right) ^ {q} \lim  _ {x \rightarrow \frac {\pi}{2} - 0} \left(\frac {1}{\sin^ {p} x}\right) = \lim  _ {t \rightarrow + 0} \left(\frac {t}{\sin t}\right) ^ {q} = 1,
$$

故积分 $\int_{\frac{x}{4}}^{\frac{x}{2}} \frac{dx}{\sin^{p} x \cos^{q} x}$ 仅当 $q < 1$ （ $p$ 为任意值）时收敛.

于是，当 $p < 1$ 且 $q < 1$ 时，积分 $\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sin^{p}x\cos^{q}x}$ 收敛.
