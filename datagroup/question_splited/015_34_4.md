34.46 Show that, if $\mathbf{R}(t)$ traces out a curve and the parameter $t$ represents time, then $\mathbf{R}(t)$ is the velocity vector, that is, its direction is the direction of motion and its length is the speed.

By Problem 34.45, we already know that $\mathbf{R}'(t)$ has the direction of the tangent vector along the curve. By Problem 34.43, $\mathbf{R}'(t) = (dx/dt, dy/dt)$ , since $\mathbf{R}(t) = (x(t), y(t))$ . Hence, $|\mathbf{R}'(t)| = \sqrt{(dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2} = ds/dt$ , where $s$ is the arc length along the curve (measured from some fixed point on the curve). But $ds/dt$ is the speed. [The "speed" is how fast the end of the position vector $\mathbf{R}(t)$ is moving, which is the rate of change of its position $s$ along the curve.]
