41.26 Show that, if a curve $\mathcal{C}$ in the $xy$ -plane has the equation $f(x,y) = 0$ , then an equation of the cylinder generated by a line intersecting $\mathcal{C}$ and moving parallel to the vector $\mathbf{A} = (a,b,1)$ is $f(x - az,y - bz) = 0$ .

The line $\mathcal{L}$ through a point $(x_0, y_0, 0)$ on $\mathcal{C}$ and parallel to $\mathbf{A}$ has parametric equations $x = x_0 + at$ , $y = y_0 + bt$ , $z = t$ . Then, $x_0 = x - az$ and $y_0 = y - bz$ . Hence, $f(x - az, y - bz) = 0$ . Conversely, if $f(x - az, y - bz) = 0$ , then, setting $x_0 = x - az$ , $y_0 = y - bz$ , we see that $(x_0, y_0, 0)$ is on $\mathcal{C}$ and $(x, y, z)$ is on the corresponding line $\mathcal{L}$ .
