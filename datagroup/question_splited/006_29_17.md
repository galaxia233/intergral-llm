29.17 Calculate $\int_0^\pi \sin nx\sin kx dx$ when $n$ and $k$ are distinct positive integers.

$\sin nx \sin kx = \frac{1}{2} [\cos (n - k)x - \cos (n + k)x]$ . So $\int_0^\pi \sin nx \sin kx dx = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\pi [\cos (n - k)x - \cos (n + k)x] dx$ $= \frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{\sin(n - k)x}{n - k} - \frac{\sin(n + k)x}{n + k}\right]_0^\pi = \frac{1}{2} [(0 - 0) - (0 - 0)] = 0.$
