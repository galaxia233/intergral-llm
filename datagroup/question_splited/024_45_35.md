45.35 For a scalar field $f$ and a vector field $\mathbf{F}$ , prove the product rule $\operatorname{div}(f\mathbf{F}) = f\operatorname{div}\mathbf{F} + \nabla f\cdot \mathbf{F}$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \text {L e t} \quad \mathbf {F} = (\phi (x, y, z), \psi (x, y, z), \eta (x, y, z)). \text {T h e n} \operatorname {d i v} (f \mathbf {F}) = \operatorname {d i v} (f \phi , f \psi , f \eta) = f \phi_ {x} + f _ {x} \phi + f \psi_ {y} + f _ {y} \psi + \\ f \eta_ {z} + f _ {z} \eta = f (\phi_ {x} + \psi_ {y} + \eta_ {z}) + (f _ {x}, f _ {y}, f _ {z}) \cdot (\phi , \psi , \eta) = f \operatorname {d i v} \mathbf {F} + \nabla f \cdot \mathbf {F}. \end{array}
$$
