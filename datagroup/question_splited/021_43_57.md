43.57 With reference to Problem 43.56, interpret the condition $\nabla f(x_0,y_0) = \lambda \nabla g(x_0,y_0)$ in terms of the directional derivative.

Figure 43-5 shows a portion of the curve $g(x, y) = 0$ , together with its field of tangent vectors $\pmb{v}$ . If $(x_0, y_0)$ is to be an extreme point for $f$ along the curve, the derivative of $f$ in the direction of $\pmb{v}$ must vanish at $(x_0, y_0)$ . Thus, $\pmb{\nabla}f$ must be perpendicular to $\pmb{v}$ , and therefore parallel to the curve normal, at $(x_0, y_0)$ . But, by Problem 43.14, the curve normal can be taken to be $\pmb{\nabla}g$ ; so $\pmb{\nabla}f = \lambda \pmb{\nabla}g$ at $(x_0, y_0)$ .
