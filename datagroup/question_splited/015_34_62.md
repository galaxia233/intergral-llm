34.104 For what value of $x$ is the radius of curvature of $y = e^{x}$ smallest?

$y^\prime = y'' = e^x$ . By Problem 34.97, $\kappa = e^{x} / (1 + e^{2x})^{3 / 2}$ , and the radius of curvature $\rho$ is $(1 + e^{2x})^{3 / 2} / e^x$ Then

$$
\frac {d \rho}{d x} = \frac {e ^ {x} \cdot \frac {3}{2} (1 + e ^ {2 x}) ^ {1 / 2} (2 e ^ {2 x}) - e ^ {x} (1 + e ^ {2 x}) ^ {3 / 2}}{e ^ {2 x}} = \frac {(1 + e ^ {2 x}) ^ {1 / 2} [ 3 e ^ {2 x} - (1 + e ^ {2 x}) ]}{e ^ {x}} = \frac {(1 + e ^ {2 x}) ^ {1 / 2} (2 e ^ {2 x} - 1)}{e ^ {x}}
$$

Setting $d\rho /dx = 0$ , we find $2e^{2x} = 1$ , $2x = \ln \frac{1}{2} = -\ln 2$ , $x = -(\ln 2) / 2$ . The first-derivative test shows that this yields a relative (and, therefore, an absolute) minimum.
