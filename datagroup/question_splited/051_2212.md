【2212】 $\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^2 - 2x\cos\alpha + 1}\quad (0 <   \alpha <  \pi).$

解 $\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^2 - 2x\cos\alpha + 1} = \frac{1}{\sin\alpha}\arctan \frac{x - \cos\alpha}{\sin\alpha}\Bigg|_{-1}^{1}$

$$
= \frac {1}{\sin \alpha} \left[ \arctan \left(\tan \frac {\alpha}{2}\right) + \arctan \left(\cot \frac {\alpha}{2}\right) \right]
$$

\(
= \frac{1}{\sin \alpha} \left\{\frac{\alpha}{2} + \arctan \left[\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\alpha}{2}\right)\right]\right\} = \frac{\pi}{2 \sin \alpha}
\)
