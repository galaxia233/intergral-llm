31.25 The region bounded by the semicircle $y = \sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$ , and $y = 0$ .

By symmetry, $\bar{x} = 0$ . (In general, the centroid lies on any line of symmetry of the region.) The area $A = \frac{\pi a^2}{2}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis $M_x = 2\int_0^a y\sqrt{a^2 - y^2} dy = -\frac{2}{3}(a^2 - y^2)^{3/2}\bigg]_0^a = \frac{2}{3}(a^2)^{3/2} = \frac{2}{3}a^3$ . Thus, $\bar{y} = \left(\frac{2}{3}a^3\right) / (\pi a^2 / 2) = \frac{4a}{3\pi}$ . Thus, the centroid is $\left(0, \frac{4a}{3\pi}\right)$ .
