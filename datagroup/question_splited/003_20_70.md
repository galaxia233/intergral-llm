20.70 Use the trapezoidal rule with $n = 10$ to approximate $\int_0^1 x^2 dx$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {0} ^ {1} x ^ {2} d x \approx \frac {1}{2 0} \left(0 ^ {2} + 1 ^ {2} + 2 \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {9} \frac {i ^ {2}}{1 0 0}\right) = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(1 + \frac {1}{5 0} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {9} i ^ {2}\right) \\ = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(1 + \frac {1}{5 0} \cdot \frac {9 \cdot 1 0 \cdot 1 9}{6}\right) \quad [ b y P r o b l e m 2 0. 6 6 ] \\ = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(\frac {2 0 1}{3 0}\right) = 0. 3 3 5 \\ \end{array}
$$

which is close to the exact value $\frac{1}{3}$ .
