41.16 Describe and sketch the graph of $\frac{x^2}{a^2} +\frac{y^2}{b^2} = \frac{z^2}{c^2}$ where $a,b,c > 0$

See Fig. 41-11. This is an elliptic cone. The horizontal cross sections $z = c \neq 0$ are ellipses. The horizontal cross section $z = 0$ is a point, the origin. The surface intersects the $xz$ -plane $(y = 0)$ in a pair of lines, $z = \pm \frac{c}{a} x$ , and intersects the $yz$ -plane $(x = 0)$ in a pair of lines $z = \pm \frac{c}{b} y$ . The other cross sections, determined by $x = k$ or $y = k$ , are hyperbolas.
