19.75 What constant deceleration is needed to slow a particle from a velocity of 45 ft/s to a dead stop in 15 ft?

$\begin{array}{l}v = \int a  dt = at + v_{0}.\\ s = \int v  dt = \frac{1}{2} at^{2} + 45t. \end{array}$ [We let $s = 0$ at $t = 0.$ ] Then $15 = \frac{1}{2} a\left(-\frac{45}{a}\right)^{2} + 45\left(-\frac{45}{a}\right) = \frac{(45)^{2}}{2a} -$ $\frac{(45)^2}{a} = -\frac{(45)^2}{2a^{\cdot}}$ . Hence, $a = -\frac{135}{2} = -67.5\mathrm{ft / s^2}$
