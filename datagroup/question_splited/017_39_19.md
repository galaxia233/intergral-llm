39.19 Find the interval on which $\sin x$ may be represented by its Maclaurin series.

Let $f(x) = \sin x$ . Note that $f^{(n)}(x)$ is either $\pm \sin x$ or $\pm \cos x$ , and, therefore, $|R_{n}(x)| = \left|\frac{f^{(n)}(c)}{n!} x^{n}\right| \leq \frac{|x|^{n}}{n!} \to 0$ (by Problem 36.13). Hence, $\sin x$ is equal to its Maclaurin series for all $x$ . (See Problem 39.2.)
