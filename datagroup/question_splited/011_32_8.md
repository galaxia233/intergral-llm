32.8 Is $\int_{e}^{\infty}\frac{dx}{(\ln x)^p}$ convergent when $p\geq 1?$

By successive applications of L'Hôpital's rule, we see that $\lim_{x\to +\infty}(\ln x)^{p} / x = 0$ . Hence, $(\ln x)^{p} / x < 1$ for sufficiently large $x$ . Thus, for some $x_0$ , if $x\geq x_0$ , $(\ln x)^p < x$ , $1 / (\ln x)^p > 1 / x$ . So, $\int_{x_0}^v\frac{dx}{(\ln x)^p} > \int_{x_0}^v\frac{dx}{x}\rightarrow +\infty$ . Hence, the integral must be divergent for arbitrary $p\geq 1$ .
