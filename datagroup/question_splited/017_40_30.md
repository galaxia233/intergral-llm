40.30 Given vectors $\mathbf{A} = (3,2, - 1)$ $\mathbf{B} = (5,3,0)$ and $\mathbf{C} = (-2,4,1)$ calculate $\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}$ A-C,and $\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{A}$

Vector addition and subtraction and multiplication by a scalar are all done componentwise. Thus, $\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} = (3 + 5, 2 + 3, -1 + 0) = (8, 5, -1)$ , $\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{C} = (3 - (-2), 2 - 4, -1 - 1) = (5, -2, -2)$ , and $\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{A} = (\frac{1}{2}(3), \frac{1}{2}(2), \frac{1}{2}(-1)) = (\frac{3}{2}, 1, -\frac{1}{2})$ .
