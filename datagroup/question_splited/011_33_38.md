33.37 Prove that, if $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to both $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ , then $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to any vector of the form $u\mathbf{B} + v\mathbf{C}$ .

A $\cdot$ B $= 0$ and $\mathbf{A}\cdot \mathbf{C} = 0$ . Hence, $\mathbf{A}\cdot (u\mathbf{B} + v\mathbf{C}) = u(\mathbf{A}\cdot \mathbf{B}) + v(\mathbf{A}\cdot \mathbf{C}) = u\cdot 0 + v\cdot 0 = 0.$
