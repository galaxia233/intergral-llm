44.43 Use polar coordinates to evaluate $I = \int_0^{1 / \sqrt{2}}\int_x^{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} dydx.$

The region of integration is indicated in Fig. 44-27. $I = \int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2} \int_0^1 r \cdot r \, dr \, d\theta = (\int_0^1 r^2 \, dr)(\int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2} d\theta) = \left( \frac{1}{3} \right) \left( \frac{\pi}{4} \right) = \frac{\pi}{12}$ .
