20.38 Evaluate $\int_{1 / 2}^{3}\sqrt{2x + 1} x^{2}dx.$

Let $u = 2x + 1$ , $x = (u - 1) / 2$ , $du = 2dx$ . Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {1 / 2} ^ {3} \sqrt {2 x + 1} x ^ {2} d x = \frac {1}{2} \int_ {2} ^ {7} \sqrt {u} \left(\frac {u - 1}{2}\right) ^ {2} d u = \frac {1}{8} \int_ {2} ^ {7} \sqrt {u} \left(u ^ {2} - 2 u + 1\right) d u = \frac {1}{8} \int_ {2} ^ {7} \left(u ^ {5 / 2} - 2 u ^ {3 / 2} + u ^ {1 / 2}\right) d u \\ = \frac {1}{8} \left[ \frac {2}{7} u ^ {7 / 2} - 2 \left(\frac {2}{3}\right) u ^ {5 / 2} + \frac {2}{3} u ^ {3 / 2} \right] \Bigg ] _ {2} ^ {7} = \frac {1}{4} u ^ {3 / 2} \left(\frac {1}{7} u ^ {2} - \frac {2}{3} u + \frac {1}{3}\right) \Bigg ] _ {2} ^ {7} \\ = \frac {1}{4} \left[ 7 \sqrt {7} \left(7 - \frac {1 4}{5} + \frac {1}{3}\right) - 2 \sqrt {2} \left(\frac {4}{7} - \frac {4}{5} + \frac {1}{3}\right) \right] = \frac {1}{2 1 0} (1 6 6 6 \sqrt {7} - 1 1 \sqrt {2}). \\ \end{array}
$$
