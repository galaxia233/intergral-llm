44.64 Evaluate $I = \iiint_{\mathcal{R}} x \, dV$ , where $\mathcal{R}$ is the tetrahedron bounded by the coordinate planes and the plane $x + 2y + z = 4$ (see Fig. 44-36).

$y$ can be integrated from 0 to 2. In the base triangle, for a given $y$ , $x$ runs from 0 to $4 - 2y$ . For given $x$ and $y$ , $z$ varies from 0 to $4 - x - 2y$ . Hence, $I = \int_0^2 \int_0^{4 - 2y} \int_0^{4 - x - 2y} x \, dz \, dx \, dy = \int_0^2 \int_0^{4 - 2y} xz \int_0^{4 - x - 2y} dx \, dy = \int_0^2 \int_0^{4 - 2y} x(4 - x - 2y) \, dx \, dy = \int_0^2 \int_0^{4 - 2y} (4x - x^2 - 2xy) \, dx \, dy = \int_0^2 (2x^2 - \frac{1}{3} x^3 - yx^2) \int_0^{4 - 2y} dy = \int_0^2 (4 - 2y)^2 [2 - \frac{1}{3}(4 - 2y) + y] \, dy = \int_0^2 (4 - 2y)^2 (\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} y) \, dy = \frac{4}{3} \int_0^2 (2 - y)^3 dy = \frac{4}{3} \cdot (-\frac{1}{4})(2 - y)^4]_0^2 = -\frac{1}{3} (-16) = \frac{16}{3}$ .
