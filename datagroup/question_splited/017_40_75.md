40.75 Show that the lines $\mathcal{L}_1$ : $x = x_0 + at$ , $y = y_0 + bt$ , $z = z_0 + ct$ and $\mathcal{L}_2$ : $x = x_0 + At$ , $y = y_0 + Bt$ , $z = z_0 + Ct$ are perpendicular if and only if $aA + bB + cC = 0$ .

$\mathcal{L}_1$ is parallel to the vector $(a, b, c)$ . $\mathcal{L}_2$ is parallel to the vector $(A, B, C)$ . $\mathcal{L}_1$ and $\mathcal{L}_2$ have a common point $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$ . Hence, $\mathcal{L}_1$ is perpendicular to $\mathcal{L}_2$ if and only if $(a, b, c) \perp (A, B, C)$ , which is equivalent to $(a, b, c) \cdot (A, B, C) = 0$ , or $aA + bB + cC = 0$ .
