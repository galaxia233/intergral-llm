43.53 A closed rectangular box costs $A$ cents per square foot for the top and bottom and $B$ cents per square foot for the sides. If the volume $V$ is fixed, what should the dimensions be to minimize the cost?

Let $l, w, h$ be the length, width, and height, respectively. The cost $C = 2Alw + 2B(lh + wh)$ . $lwh = V$ (constant). Hence, $C = 2Alw + 2B\left(\frac{V}{w} + \frac{V}{l}\right)$ . Then $\partial C / \partial l = 2Aw - (2VB / l^2)$ , $\partial C / \partial w = 2Al - (2VB / w^2)$ . Set $\partial C / \partial l = \partial C / \partial w = 0$ . Then $Awl^2 = VB$ , $Aw^2 l = VB$ . So, $Awl^2 = Aw^2 l$ . Hence, $l = w$ . Therefore, $Al^3 = VB$ , $l = 2\sqrt[3]{VB / A}$ , $w = 2\sqrt[3]{VB / A}$ , $h = 8 / lw = 2(\sqrt[3]{A / VB})^2$ .
