41.50 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^2}$ if it exists.

Note that, if $y = mx$ and $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ , then $\frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^2} = \frac{2xm^2x^2}{x^2 + m^2x^2} = \frac{2m^2}{1 + m^2} x \to 0$ . However, this is not enough to ensure that $2xy^2 / (x^2 + y^2) \to 0$ no matter how $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ . Take any $\varepsilon > 0$ . Note that $|(x, y) - (0, 0)| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ . Observe also that $|x| \leq \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ and $y^2 \leq x^2 + y^2$ . So,

$$
\left| \frac {2 x y ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} \right| \leq \frac {2 \sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} \left(x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}\right)}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} = 2 \sqrt {x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}}
$$

Hence, if we choose $\delta = \varepsilon / 2$ and if $\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} < \delta$ , then $|2xy^2/(x^2 + y^2)| \leq 2\sqrt{x^2 + y^2} < 2\delta = \varepsilon$ . Thus, $\lim_{(x, y) \to (0, 0)} \frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^2} = 0$ .
