45.48 Verify Green's theorem (Problem 45.47) when $\mathbf{F} = (3x,2y)$ and $\mathcal{R}$ is the disk of radius 1 with center at the origin.

$\operatorname{div} \mathbf{F} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (3x) + \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (2y) = 3 + 2 = 5.$ Therefore, $\iint_{\mathbb{C}} \operatorname{div} \mathbf{F} \, dA = 5 \iint_{\mathbb{C}} dA = 5$ (area of $\mathcal{R}$ ) $= 5\pi$ . $\mathcal{C}$ is the circle $x = \cos t, y = \sin t, 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$ . Then, $\int_{\mathcal{C}}^{\mathcal{R}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} \, ds = \int_{\mathcal{C}} (3x, 2y) \cdot \left(\frac{dy}{ds}, -\frac{dx}{ds}\right) \, ds = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left(3x \frac{dy}{dt} - 2y \frac{dx}{dt}\right) \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} [3 \cos t \cdot \cos t - 2 \sin t (-\sin t)] \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} (3 \cos^2 t + 2 \sin^2 t) \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} (2 + \cos^2 t) \, dt = \int_{0}^{2\pi} (2 + \frac{1 + \cos 2t}{2}) \, dt = \left(\frac{5}{2} t + \frac{\sin 2t}{4}\right) \Bigg]_{0}^{2\pi} = \frac{5}{2} \cdot 2\pi = 5\pi.$
