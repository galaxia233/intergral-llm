41.54 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x^2}{x^2 + y^2}$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then $\frac{x^2}{x^2 + y^2} = \frac{x^2}{x^2 + m^2 x^2} = \frac{1}{1 + m^2}$ . Hence, as $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ along the line $y = mx$ , $x^2 / (x^2 + y^2) \to 1 / (1 + m^2)$ . Therefore, $\lim_{(x, y) \to (0, 0)} \frac{x^2}{x^2 + y^2}$ does not exist, since $1 / (1 + m^2)$ is a nonconstant function of $m$ .
