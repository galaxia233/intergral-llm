31.22 A particle carrying a positive electrical charge $+q$ is released at a distance $d$ from an immovable positive charge $+Q$ . How much work is done on the particle as its distance increases to $2d$ ?

By Coulomb's law, the repulsive force on the particle when at distance $d + x$ is $kQq / (d + x)^2$ , where $k$ is a universal constant. Then the work is $W = \int_{0}^{d}\frac{kQq}{(d + x)^{2}} dx = kQq\left(\frac{-1}{d + x}\right)\int_{0}^{d} = kQq\left(-\frac{1}{2d} +\frac{1}{d}\right) = \frac{kQq}{2d}$ .
