32.51 Evaluate $\int_0^{\pi /2}\frac{dx}{1 - \sin x}.$

$$
\begin{array}{l} I \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {d x}{1 - \sin x} = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {1}{1 - \sin x} \cdot \frac {1 + \sin x}{1 + \sin x} d x = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {1 + \sin x}{\cos^ {2} x} d x = \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} (\sec^ {2} x + \tan x \sec x) d x \\ = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow \pi / 2 ^ {-}} (\tan x + \sec x) ] _ {0} ^ {v} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow \pi / 2 ^ {-}} [ (\tan v + \sec v) - 1 ] = + \infty \\ \end{array}
$$

(The same result is obtained from Problem 29.45.)
