43.54 Find the absolute maximum and minimum of $f(x, y) = 4x^{2} + 2xy - 3y^{2}$ on the unit square $0 \leq x \leq 1$ , $0 \leq y \leq 1$ .

$f_{x} = 8x + 2y, f_{y} = 2x - 6y.$ Set $f_{x} = f_{y} = 0$ . Then $4x + y = 0$ $x - 3y = 0$ Solving, we get $x = y = 0$ Note that $f(0,0) = 0$ . Let us look at the boundary of the square (Fig. 43-3). (1) On the segment $L_{1}$ $x = 0,\quad 0\leq y\leq 1$ . Then $f(0,y) = -3y^{2}$ The maximum is 0, and the minimum is $-3$ at $(0,1)$ . (2) On the segment $L_{2},\quad x = 1,\quad 0\leq y\leq 1.$ $f(1,y) = 4 + 2y - 3y^2.$ $\frac{df}{dy}\left(1,y\right) = 2 - 6y$ Thus, $\frac{1}{3}$ is a critical number. We must evaluate $f(1,y)$ for $y = 0,\quad y = \frac{1}{3}$ , and $y = 1.$ $f(1,0) = 4,\quad f(1,\frac{1}{3}) = \frac{13}{3},\quad f(1,1) = 3.$ (3) On the segment $L_{3},\quad y = 0,\quad 0\leq x\leq 1.$ $f(x,0) = 4x^{2}$ . Hence, the maximum is 4 at $(1,0)$ , and the minimum is O at $(0,0)$ . (4) On the line segment $L_{4},\quad y = 1,\quad 0\leq x\leq 1$ . Then $f(x,1) = 4x^{2} + 2x - 3.$ $\frac{df}{dx}\left(x,1\right) = 8x + 2$ . The critical number is $-\frac{1}{4}$ , which does not lie in the interval $0\leq x\leq 1$ . Thus, we need only look at $f(x,1)$ when $x = 0$ and $x = 1$ . $f(0,1) = -3$ and $f(1,1) = 3$ . Therefore, the absolute maximum is $\frac{13}{3}$ at $(1,\frac{1}{3})$ , and the absolute minimum is $-3$ at $(0,1)$
