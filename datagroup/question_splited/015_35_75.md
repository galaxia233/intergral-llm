35.75 Find the area between the inner and outer ovals of $r^2 = 1 + \sin \theta$ in the first quadrant (see Fig. 35-18).

The area inside the outer oval is

$$
A _ {o} = \frac {1}{2} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} (1 + \sin \theta) d \theta = \frac {1}{2} (\theta - \cos \theta) ] _ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} = \frac {1}{2} \left[ \frac {\pi}{2} - (- 1) \right] = \frac {\pi + 2}{4}
$$

The area inside the inner oval is the same as

$$
A _ {i} = \frac {1}{2} \int_ {\pi} ^ {3 \pi / 2} (1 + \sin \theta) d \theta = \frac {1}{2} (\theta - \cos \theta) ] _ {\pi} ^ {3 \pi / 2} = \frac {1}{2} \left[ \frac {3 \pi}{2} - (\pi + 1) \right] = \frac {\pi - 2}{4}
$$

Hence $A_{o} - A_{i} = \frac{\pi + 2}{4} -\frac{\pi - 2}{4} = 1$
