【2147】+ $\int \frac{\sin 4x}{\sin^8 x + \cos^8 x} \, \mathrm{d}x.$

解 $\sin^8 x + \cos^8 x = (\sin^4 x + \cos^4 x)^2 - 2\sin^4 x\cos^4 x = \left[(\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x)^2 - 2\sin^2 x\cos^2 x\right]^2 - \frac{1}{8}\sin^4 2x$

$$
\begin{array}{l} = \left(1 - \frac {1}{2} \sin^ {2} 2 x\right) ^ {2} - \frac {1}{8} \sin^ {4} 2 x = \frac {1}{8} (\sin^ {4} 2 x - 8 \sin^ {2} 2 x + 8) \\ = \frac {1}{8} (\sin^ {2} 2 x - 4 - 2 \sqrt {2}) (\sin^ {2} 2 x - 4 + 2 \sqrt {2}) = \frac {1}{3 2} (\cos 4 x + 7 + 4 \sqrt {2}) (\cos 4 x + 7 - 4 \sqrt {2}). \\ \end{array}
$$

于是， $\int \frac{\sin 4x}{\sin^8 x + \cos^8 x} \mathrm{d}x = 32 \cdot \frac{1}{8\sqrt{2}} \left[ \int \frac{\sin 4x}{\cos 4x + 7 - 4\sqrt{2}} \mathrm{d}x - \int \frac{\sin 4x}{\cos 4x + 7 + 4\sqrt{2}} \mathrm{d}x \right]$

$$
= - \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \int \frac {\mathrm {d} (\cos 4 x + 7 - 4 \sqrt {2})}{\cos 4 x + 7 - 4 \sqrt {2}} + \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \int \frac {\mathrm {d} (\cos 4 x + 7 + 4 \sqrt {2})}{\cos 4 x + 7 + 4 \sqrt {2}} = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \ln \frac {\cos 4 x + 7 + 4 \sqrt {2}}{\cos 4 x + 7 - 4 \sqrt {2}} + C.
$$
