41.52 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x^2 - y^2}{x^2 + y^2},$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then

$$
\frac {x ^ {2} - y ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} = \frac {x ^ {2} - m ^ {2} x ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + m ^ {2} x ^ {2}} = \frac {1 - m ^ {2}}{1 + m ^ {2}}
$$

Since $(1 - m^2) / (1 + m^2)$ depends on $m$ , $(x^{2} - y^{2}) / (x^{2} + y^{2})$ approaches different numbers as $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ along different lines. Hence, $\lim_{(x, y) \to (0, 0)} \frac{x^2 - y^2}{x^2 + y^2}$ does not exist.
