32.12 Evaluate $\int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{e^x + e^{-x}}.$

1 1 e x +e -x = e. Let u = e, du = e dx. Then $\int \frac{e^x dx}{e^{2x} + 1} = \int \frac{du}{u^2 + 1} = \tan^{-1}u = \tan^{-1}e^x.$ Hence,

$$
\int_ {0} ^ {\infty} \frac {d x}{e ^ {x} + e ^ {- x}} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \int_ {0} ^ {v} \frac {e ^ {x} d x}{e ^ {2 x} + 1} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} (\tan^ {- 1} e ^ {v} - \tan^ {- 1} 1) = \frac {\pi}{2} - \frac {\pi}{4} = \frac {\pi}{4}
$$
