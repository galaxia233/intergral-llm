42.86 The altitude $h$ of a right circular cone is decreasing at the rate of $3\mathrm{mm} / \mathrm{s}$ , while the radius $r$ of the base is increasing at the rate of $2\mathrm{mm} / \mathrm{s}$ . How fast is the volume $V$ changing when the altitude and radius are $100\mathrm{mm}$ and $50\mathrm{mm}$ , respectively?

$$
\begin{array}{l} I _ {V} = \frac {1}{3} \pi r ^ {2} h. \quad \text {S o}, \quad \frac {d V}{d t} = \frac {\partial V}{\partial r} \frac {d r}{d t} + \frac {\partial V}{\partial h} \frac {d h}{d t} = \left(\frac {2}{3} \pi r h\right) (2) + \left(\frac {1}{3} \pi r ^ {2}\right) (- 3) = \frac {4}{3} \pi r h - \pi r ^ {2} = \pi \left[ \frac {4}{3} (5 0 0 0) - 2 5 0 0 \right] = \\ \frac {1 2 , 5 0 0 \pi}{3} \mathrm {m m} ^ {3} / \mathrm {s}. \end{array}
