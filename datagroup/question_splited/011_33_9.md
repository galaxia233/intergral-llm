33.8 Use vector methods to find an equation of the line $N$ through the points $P_{1}(1,4)$ and $P_{2}(3, - 2)$

1. Let $P(x, y)$ be any point on $N$ . Then $\overrightarrow{P_1P} = (x - 1, y - 4)$ and $\overrightarrow{P_1P_2} = (3 - 1, -2 - 4) = (2, -6)$ . Clearly, (6, 2) is perpendicular to $\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}$ , and, therefore, to $\overrightarrow{P_1P}$ . Thus, $0 = (6, 2) \cdot (x - 1, y - 4) = 6(x - 1) + 2(y - 4) = 6x + 2y - 14$ . Hence, $3x + y - 7 = 0$ is an equation of $N$ .
