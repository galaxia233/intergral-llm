26.31 A doomsday equation is an equation of the form $\frac{dP}{dt} = KP^{1.01}$ , with $K > 0$ . Solve this equation and show that $\lim_{t \to t_1} P = +\infty$ for some $t_1$ .

$\int \frac{dP}{P^{1.01}} = \int Kdt = Kt + C.$ But, $\int \frac{dP}{P^{1.01}} = -\frac{100}{P^{0.01}}$ Setting $t = 0$ we find that $C = -100 / P_0^{0.01}$ . Now, $P^{0.01} = -100 / (Kt + C)$ . As $t\to -C / K$ from below, $P\rightarrow +\infty$
