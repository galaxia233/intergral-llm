35.80 Find the length of $r = e^{\theta /2}$ from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = 2\pi$

dr/dθ=1e. Then

$$
L = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \sqrt {e ^ {\theta} + \frac {1}{4} e ^ {\theta}} d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \frac {\sqrt {5}}{2} e ^ {θ / 2} d \theta = \frac {\sqrt {5}}{2} (2 e ^ {\theta / 2}) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {2 \pi} = \sqrt {5} (e ^ {\pi} - 1)
$$
