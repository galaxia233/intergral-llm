46.48 Describe the solutions of a homogeneous linear differential equation of second order with constant coefficients:

$$
y ^ {\prime \prime} + b y ^ {\prime} + c y = 0 \tag {1}
$$

Consider the auxiliary quadratic equation

$$
u ^ {2} + b u + c = 0 \tag {2}
$$

There are three cases. Case 1. $b^{2} - 4c > 0$ . Hence, (2) has two distinct real roots $r_{1}$ and $r_{2}$ . Then, the general solution of (1) is $C_{1}e^{r_{1}x} + C_{2}e^{r_{2}x}$ . Case 2. $b^{2} - 4c = 0$ . Hence, (2) has a double root $r$ . Then the general solution of (1) is $(C_{1} + C_{2}x)e^{rx}$ , or $C_{1}e^{rx} + C_{2}xe^{rx}$ . Case 3. $b^{2} - 4c < 0$ . Hence, (2) has two distinct conjugate complex roots $r_{1} = p + iq$ and $r_{2} = p - iq$ . Then the general solution of (1) is $(C_{1}\cos qx + C_{2}\sin qx)e^{px}$ .
