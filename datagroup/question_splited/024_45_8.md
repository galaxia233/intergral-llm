45.8 If $\mathbf{G}(t) = (e^t,\cos t,t)$ , find $\frac{d}{dt}[(\sin t)\mathbf{G}(t)]$ .

By the formula of Problem 45.7, $\frac{d}{dt}[(\sin t)\mathbf{G}(t)] = (\sin t)\mathbf{G}'(t) + \left(\frac{d}{dt}\sin t\right)\mathbf{G}(t) = (\sin t)(e', -\sin t, 1) + (\cos t)(e', \cos t, t) = (e'(\sin t + \cos t), \cos^2 t - \sin^2 t, \sin t + t\cos t)$ .
