43.68 Prove Cauchy's inequality: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i \leq \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i^2 \right)^{1/2} \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} b_i^2 \right)^{1/2}$ .

Let $A = \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i^2\right)^{1/2}$ and $B = \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} b_i^2\right)^{1/2}$ . Let $x_i = a_i / A$ and $y_i = b_i / B$ . (If $A = 0$ or $B = 0$ , the desired result is obvious.) Then $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i^2 = 1$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i^2 = 1$ . By Problem 43.66, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i y_i \leq 1$ . Hence, $\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{a_i}{A} \frac{b_i}{B} \leq 1$ , $\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i \leq AB$ .
