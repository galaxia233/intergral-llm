44.63 Find the volume within the cylinder $r = 4 \cos \theta$ bounded above by the sphere $r^2 + z^2 = 16$ and below by the plane $z = 0$ .

Integrate first with respect to $z$ from $z = 0$ to $z = \sqrt{16 - r^2}$ , then with respect to $r$ from $r = 0$ to $r = 4\cos \theta$ , and then with respect to $\theta$ from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = \pi$ . (See Fig. 44-35.) $V = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{4\cos \theta} \int_0^{\sqrt{16 - r^2}} r \, dr \, d\theta = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{4\cos \theta} r \, dz \, dr \, d\theta = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{4\cos \theta} r \sqrt{16 - r^2} \, dr \, d\theta = \int_0^\pi [-\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{2}(16 - r^2)^{3/2}] \int_0^{4\cos \theta} d\theta = -\frac{1}{3} \int_0^\pi (64\sin^3 \theta - 64) \, d\theta = -\frac{64}{3} \int_0^\pi (\sin \theta - \cos^2 \theta \sin \theta - 1) \, d\theta = -\frac{64}{3} (-\cos \theta + \frac{1}{3}\cos^3 \theta - 64) \int_0^\pi [(1 - \frac{1}{3} - \pi) - (-1 + \frac{1}{3})] = -\frac{64}{3} (\frac{4}{3} - \pi) = \frac{64}{9} (3\pi - 4)$ .
