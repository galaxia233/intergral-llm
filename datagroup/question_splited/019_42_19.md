42.19 If $z = e^{x / y} \sin (x / y) + e^{y / x} \cos (y / x)$ , show that $x \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} + y \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 0$ .

It is easy to prove a more general result. Let $z = f(x / y)$ , where $f$ is an arbitrary differentiable function. Then

$$
x \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} = x f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right) \frac {1}{y} = \frac {x}{y} f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right) \quad \text {a n d} \quad y \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} = y f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right) \left(- \frac {x}{y ^ {2}}\right) = - \frac {x}{y} f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right)
$$

by addition, $x\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} +y\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 0.$
