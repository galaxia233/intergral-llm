30.13 $\int \frac{x^2 dx}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 4x + 5)}$ .

$x^{2} + 4x + 5$ is irreducible, since its discriminant $b^{2} - 4ac = -4 < 0$ . Thus, $\frac{x^2}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 4x + 5)} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{Bx + C}{x^2 + 4x + 5}$ , or $x^{2} = A(x^{2} + 4x + 5) + (Bx + C)(x - 1)$ . Let $x = 1$ . Then $1 = 10A$ , $A = \frac{1}{10}$ . Equate coefficients of $x^{2}$ : $1 = A + B$ , $B = 1 - A = \frac{9}{10}$ . Equate constant coefficients: $0 = 5A - C$ , $C = 5A = \frac{5}{10}$ . So $\frac{x^2}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 4x + 5)} = \frac{1}{10}\frac{1}{x - 1} + \frac{\frac{9}{10}x + \frac{5}{10}}{x^2 + 4x + 5} = \frac{1}{10}\frac{1}{x - 1} + \frac{1}{10}\frac{9x + 5}{x^2 + 4x + 5}$ . Hence, $\int \frac{x^2 dx}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 4x + 5)} = \frac{1}{10}\ln |x - 1| + \frac{1}{10}\int \frac{9x + 5}{x^2 + 4x + 5} dx$ . To compute the latter integral, complete the square: $x^{2} + 4x + 5 = (x + 2)^{2} + 1$ . Let $x + 2 = \tan \theta$ , $dx = \sec^2 \theta d\theta$ , $9x + 5 = 9\tan \theta - 13$ . Thus (Fig. 30-1), $\int \frac{9x + 5}{x^2 + 4x + 5} dx = \int \frac{(9\tan\theta - 13)\sec^2\theta d\theta}{\sec^2\theta} = \int (9\tan\theta - 13)d\theta = 9\ln |\sec\theta| - 13\theta = 9\ln |\sqrt{x^2 + 4x + 5}| - 13\tan^{-1}(x + 2) = \frac{9}{2}\ln (x^2 + 4x + 5) - 13\tan^{-1}(x + 2)$ . Hence, the complete answer is $\frac{1}{10}\ln |x - 1| + \frac{9}{20}\ln (x^2 + 4x + 5) - \frac{13}{10}\tan^{-1}(x + 2) + C_1$ .
