44.24 Express the integral $I = \int_0^1\int_0^{\sqrt{y}}f(x,y)dx dy$ as an integral with the order of integration reversed.

In the region of integration, the $x$ -values for $0 \leq y \leq 1$ range from 0 to $\sqrt{y}$ . Hence, the bounding curve is $x = \sqrt{y}$ , or $y = x^2$ . Thus (see Fig. 44-12), $I = \int_0^1 \int_{x^2}^1 f(x, y) \, dy \, dx$ .
