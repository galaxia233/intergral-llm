【1841】 $\int \frac{x\mathrm{d}x}{x^2 - 2x\cos\alpha + 1}$

解 $\int \frac{x\mathrm{d}x}{x^2 - 2x\cos\alpha + 1} = \int \frac{x - \cos\alpha + \cos\alpha}{(x - \cos\alpha)^2 + \sin^2\alpha}\mathrm{d}x$

$$
\begin{array}{l} = \frac {1}{2} \int \frac {\mathrm {d} [ (x - \cos \alpha) ^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \alpha ]}{(x - \cos \alpha) ^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \alpha} + \cos \alpha \int \frac {\mathrm {d} (x - \cos \alpha)}{(x - \cos \alpha) ^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \alpha} \\ = \frac {1}{2} \ln (x ^ {2} - 2 x \cos \alpha + 1) + \cot \alpha \cdot \arctan \left(\frac {x - \cos \alpha}{\sin \alpha}\right) + C \quad (a \neq k \pi ; k = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \dots) \\ \end{array}
$$
