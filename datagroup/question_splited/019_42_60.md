42.61 If $f(x, y) = \int_0^1 \cos (x + 2y + t) \, dt$ , find $f_x$ and $f_y$ .

$$
f _ {x} = \int_ {0} ^ {1} \frac {\partial}{\partial x} \cos (x + 2 y + t) d t = - \int_ {0} ^ {1} \sin (x + 2 y + t) d t = \cos (x + 2 y + t) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {1} = \cos (x + 2 y + 1) - \cos (x + 2 y).
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l} f _ {y} = \int_ {0} ^ {1} \frac {\partial}{\partial y} \cos (x + 2 y + t) d t = - \int_ {0} ^ {1} 2 \sin (x + 2 y + t) d t = 2 \cos (x + 2 y + t) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {1} \\ = 2 [ \cos (x + 2 y + 1) - \cos (x + 2 y) ] \\ \end{array}
$$
