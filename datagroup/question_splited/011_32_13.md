32.13 Evaluate $\int_0^\infty e^{-x}\cos xdx$

By Problem 28.9, $\int e^{-x} \cos x \, dx = \frac{1}{2} e^{-x} (\sin x - \cos x)$ . Hence, $\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-x} \cos x \, dx = \lim_{v \to +\infty} [ \frac{1}{2} e^{-x} (\sin x - \cos x) ]_0^v = \lim_{v \to +\infty} \frac{1}{2} [ e^{-v} (\cos v - \sin v) - (-1) ] = \frac{1}{2}$ , since $\lim_{v \to +\infty} \frac{\cos v}{e^v} = \lim_{v \to +\infty} \frac{\sin v}{e^v} = 0$ . (|\cos v| $\leq 1$ and $|\sin v| \leq 1$ .)
