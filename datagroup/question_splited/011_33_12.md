33.11 If $\mathbf{A}$ , $\mathbf{B}$ , $\mathbf{C}$ , $\mathbf{D}$ are consecutive sides of an oriented quadrilateral $PQRS$ (Fig. 33-2), show that $\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C} + \mathbf{D} = \mathbf{0}$ . [0 is (0,0), the zero vector.]

$\overrightarrow{PR} = \overrightarrow{PQ} + \overrightarrow{QR} = \mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}$ . $\overrightarrow{PR} = \overrightarrow{PS} + \overrightarrow{SR} = -\mathbf{D} - \mathbf{C}$ . Hence, $\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} = -\mathbf{D} - \mathbf{C}, \quad \mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C} + \mathbf{D} = 0$ .
