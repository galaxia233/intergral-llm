27.19 $\sin (\cos^{-1}\frac{1}{5} -\tan^{-1}2)$

Let $\theta_{1} = \cos^{-1}\frac{1}{5}$ and $\theta_{2} = \tan^{-1}2$ . Then $\theta_{1}$ and $\theta_{2}$ are in the first quadrant. $\sin (\cos^{-1}\frac{1}{5} - \tan^{-1}2) = \sin (\theta_{1} - \theta_{2}) = \sin \theta_{1}\cos \theta_{2} - \cos \theta_{1}\sin \theta_{2} = (2\sqrt{6} / 5)(1 / \sqrt{5}) - \frac{1}{5} (2 / \sqrt{5}) = (2\sqrt{6} - 2) / 5\sqrt{5}$ .
