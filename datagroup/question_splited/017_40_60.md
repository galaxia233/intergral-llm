40.60 Show that $\mathbf{C} \times (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})$ is a linear combination of $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ ; namely, $\mathbf{C} \times (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) = (\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{B})\mathbf{A} - (\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{A})\mathbf{B}$ .

$\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = (a_{2}b_{3} - a_{3}b_{2}, a_{3}b_{1} - a_{1}b_{3}, a_{1}b_{2} - a_{2}b_{1})$ . So, $\mathbf{C} \times (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) = (c_{2}(a_{1}b_{2} - a_{2}b_{1}) - c_{3}(a_{3}b_{1} - a_{1}b_{3}), c_{3}(a_{2}b_{3} - a_{3}b_{2}) - c_{1}(a_{1}b_{2} - a_{2}b_{1}), c_{1}(a_{3}b_{1} - a_{1}b_{3}) - c_{2}(a_{2}b_{3} - a_{3}b_{2})) = (c_{1}b_{1} + c_{2}b_{2} + c_{3}b_{3})(a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3}) - (a_{1}c_{1} + a_{2}c_{2} + a_{3}c_{3})(b_{1}, b_{2}, b_{3}) = (\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{B})\mathbf{A} - (\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{A})\mathbf{B}$ .
