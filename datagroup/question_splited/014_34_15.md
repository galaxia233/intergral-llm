34.17 Find $dy / dx$ and $d^2 y / dx^2$ for the circle $x = r\cos \theta, y = r\sin \theta.$

Recall that $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy / d\theta}{dx / d\theta}$ . Since $dx / d\theta = -r\sin \theta$ and $dy / d\theta = r\cos \theta$ , we have $dy / dx = r\cos \theta / (-r\sin \theta) = -\cot \theta = -x / y$ . Remember also that $d^2 y / dx^2 = \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right) = \frac{d}{d\theta}\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right) / \left(\frac{dx}{d\theta}\right)$ . Hence,

$$
\frac {d ^ {2} y}{d x ^ {2}} = \frac {\frac {d}{d \theta} (- \cot \theta)}{- r \sin \theta} = - \frac {\csc^ {2} \theta}{r \sin \theta} = - \frac {1}{r} \csc^ {3} \theta = - \frac {r ^ {2}}{y ^ {3}}
$$
