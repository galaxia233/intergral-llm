26.3 A certain chemical decomposes exponentially. Assume that 200 grams becomes 50 grams in 1 hour. How much will remain after 3 hours?

Let $y$ be the number of grams present at time $t$ . Then $y = y_0 e^{Kt}$ . The given information tells us that $50 = 200 e^K$ , $e^K = \frac{1}{4}$ , $K = \ln \frac{1}{4} = -\ln 4$ . When $t = 3$ , $y = 200 e^{3K} = 200(e^K)^3 = 200(\frac{1}{4})^3 = \frac{200}{64} = 3.125$ grams.
