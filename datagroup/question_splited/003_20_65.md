20.65 Prove the formula $1^{3} + 2^{3} + \dots + n^{3} = \left[\frac{n(n + 1)}{2}\right]^{2}$ .

For $n = 1$ , both sides are 1. Assume the formula true for a given $n$ , and add $(n + 1)^3$ to both sides:

$$
\begin{array}{l} 1 ^ {3} + 2 ^ {3} + \dots + n ^ {3} + (n + 1) ^ {3} = \left[ \frac {n (n + 1)}{2} \right] ^ {2} + (n + 1) ^ {3} = (n + 1) ^ {2} \left[ \frac {n ^ {2}}{4} + (n + 1) \right] = \frac {(n + 1) ^ {2}}{4} (n ^ {2} + 4 n + 4) \\ = \frac {(n + 1) ^ {2}}{4} (n + 2) ^ {2} = \left[ \frac {(n + 1) (n + 2)}{2} \right] ^ {2} \\ \end{array}
$$

which is the case of the formula for $n + 1$ . Hence, the formula has been proved by induction.
