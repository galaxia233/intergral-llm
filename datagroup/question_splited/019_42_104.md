42.104 Find a general solution of $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y} = 1$ .

Note that $\frac{\partial^2(xy)}{\partial x\partial y} = 1$ . Let $C(x,y) = f(x,y) - xy$ . Then $\frac{\partial^2C}{\partial x\partial y} = 1 - 1 = 0$ . By Problem 42.103, $C(x,y) = A(x) + B(y)$ for suitable $A(x)$ and $B(y)$ . Then, $f(x,y) = A(x) + B(y) + xy$ . This is the general solution of $\frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x\partial y} = 1$ .
