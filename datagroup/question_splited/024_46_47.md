46.47 Solve $y^{\prime \prime} + a^{2} / y^{3} = 0$

Let $z = y'$ . Then $y'' = \frac{dz}{dx} = \frac{dz}{dy} \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dz}{dy} \cdot z$ . Hence, $z\frac{dz}{dy} + \frac{a^2}{y^3} = 0$ , $z\frac{dz}{dy} = -\frac{a^2}{y^3}$ . $y$ and $z$ are separable. $\int z dz = -a^2\int \frac{dy}{y^3}, \quad \frac{1}{2}z^2 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{a^2}{y^2} + C_1, \quad z^2 = \frac{a^2}{y^2} + C = \frac{a^2 + Cy^2}{y^2}, \quad \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2 + Cy^2}}{y},$ $\int \frac{y dy}{\sqrt{a^2 + Cy^2}} = \int dx, \quad \frac{1}{C}\sqrt{a^2 + Cy^2} = x + K, \quad a^2 + Cy^2 = C^2(x + K)^2.$
