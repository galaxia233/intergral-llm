40.41 Verify that $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ is perpendicular to both $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ , when $\mathbf{A} = (1, 3, -1)$ and $\mathbf{B} = (2, 0, 1)$ .

$\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = (3(1) - (-1)0, (-1)2 - 1(1), 1(0) - 3(2)) = (3, -3, -6), \quad \mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) = (1, 3, -1) \cdot (3, -3, -6) = 3 - 9 + 6 = 0, \quad \mathbf{B} \cdot (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) = (2, 0, 1) \cdot (3, -3, -6) = 6 - 6 = 0.$ Therefore, $\mathbf{A} \perp (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})$ and $\mathbf{B} \perp (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})$ .
