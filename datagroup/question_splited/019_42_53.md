42.54 If the Cauchy-Riemann equations $f_{x} = g_{y}$ and $g_{x} = -f_{y}$ hold, prove that, under suitable assumptions, $f$ and $g$ satisfy Laplace's equation (see Problem 42.52).

Since $f_x = g_y$ , we have $f_{xx} = g_{yx}$ . Since $g_x = -f_y$ , $g_{xy} = -f_{yy}$ . Now, assuming that the second mixed partial derivatives exist and are continuous, we have $g_{yx} = g_{xy}$ , and, therefore, $f_{xx} = -f_{yy}$ . Hence, $f_{xx} + f_{yy} = 0$ . Likewise, $g_{xx} = -f_{yx} = -f_{xy} = -g_{yy}$ , and, therefore, $g_{xx} + g_{yy} = 0$ .
