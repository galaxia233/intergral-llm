39.4 Calculate the Taylor series for $1 / x$ about 1.

Let $f(x) = \frac{1}{x}$ . Then, $f'(x) = -\frac{1}{x^2}$ , $f''(x) = \frac{2}{x^3}$ , $f'''(x) = -\frac{2 \cdot 3}{x^4}$ , $f^{(4)}(x) = \frac{2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4}{x^5}$ , and, in general, $f_{\infty}^{(n)}(x) = (-1)^n \frac{n!}{x^{n+1}}$ . So $f^{(n)}(1) = (-1)^n n!$ . Thus, the Taylor series is $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(1)}{n!} (x-1)^n = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n (x-1)^n = 1 - (x-1) + (x-1)^2 - (x-1)^3 + \cdots$ .
