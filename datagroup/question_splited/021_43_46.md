43.46 Find the shortest distance between the line $x = 2 + 4s$ , $y = 4 + s$ , $z = 4 + 5s$ and the line $x = 1 - 2t$ , $y = -3 + 3t$ , $z = 2 + t$ .

It suffices to minimize the square of the distance: $f(s, t) = (1 + 4s + 2t)^2 + (7 + s - 3t)^2 + (2 + 5s - t)^2$ . $f_s = 2(1 + 4s + 2t)(4) + 2(7 + s - 3t) + 2(2 + 5s - t)(5) = 42 + 84s$ . $f_t = 2(1 + 4s + 2t)(2) + 2(7 + s - 3t)(-3) + 2(2 + 5s - t)(-1) = -42 + 28t$ . Set $f_s = f_t = 0$ . Then $s = -\frac{1}{2}$ , $t = \frac{3}{2}$ . Hence, $f(s, t) = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12$ . Therefore, the distance between the lines is $\sqrt{12} = 2\sqrt{3}$ .
