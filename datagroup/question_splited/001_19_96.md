19.96 Find the escape velocity for an object shot vertically upward from the surface of the Earth. (Let $-g$ be the acceleration due to the Earth's gravity at the surface of the Earth; $g \approx 32\mathrm{ft/s}^2$ .)

By Problem 19.95, $-g = a = -GM / R^2$ , $g = GM / R^2$ , $GM = gR^2$ . Hence, $2GM / R = 2gR$ . Therefore, the escape velocity is $\sqrt{2gR}$ . Now, $g \approx 32\mathrm{ft/s}^2 = \frac{32}{5280}\mathrm{mi/s}^2$ . If we approximate the radius of the Earth by 4000 miles, then the escape velocity is about $\sqrt{2 \cdot \frac{32}{5280} \cdot 4000} \approx 7\mathrm{mi/s}$ .
