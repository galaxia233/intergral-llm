30.19 $\int \frac{x - 1}{x^3 + 2x^2 - x - 2} dx.$

$x = 1$ is a root of the denominator. When the latter is divided by $x - 1$ , we obtain $x^{2} + 3x + 2 = (x + 2)(x + 1)$ . Since $x - 1$ cancels out, we are left with $\int \frac{dx}{(x + 2)(x + 1)}$ . Now, $\frac{1}{(x + 2)(x + 1)} = \frac{A}{x + 2} + \frac{B}{x + 1}$ . Then $1 = A(x + 1) + B(x + 2)$ . Let $x = -1$ . Then $1 = B$ . Let $x = -2$ . Then $1 = -A$ , $A = -1$ . Thus, $\frac{1}{(x + 2)(x + 1)} = -\frac{1}{x + 2} + \frac{1}{x + 1}$ , and $\int \frac{dx}{(x + 2)(x + 1)} = -\ln |x + 2| + \ln |x + 1| + C_1 = \ln \left|\frac{x + 1}{x + 2}\right| + C_1$ .
