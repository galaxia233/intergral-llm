41.88 Find a set of spherical coordinates for the point whose rectangular coordinates are $(1,1,\sqrt{6})$ .

$\rho^2 = 1^2 + 1^2 + (\sqrt{6})^2 = 8.$ Hence, $\rho = 2\sqrt{2}$ . $\tan \phi = \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2} / \sqrt{6} = \sqrt{2} / \sqrt{6} = 1 / \sqrt{3}$ . Therefore, $\phi = \pi / 6$ . $\tan \theta = \frac{1}{1} = 1$ . Hence, $\theta = \pi / 4$ . So, the spherical coordinates are $(2\sqrt{2}, \pi / 6, \pi / 4)$ .
