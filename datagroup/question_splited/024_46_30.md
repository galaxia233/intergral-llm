46.30 Solve $x\frac{dy}{dx} +y = x.$

Divide by $x$ : $\frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x}y = 1$ . Use an integrating factor: $\rho = \exp \left(\int \frac{1}{x} dx\right) = e^{\ln x} = x$ . Then $\int \rho q(x) dx = \int x dx = \frac{1}{2}x^2$ , and $y = (1/x)(\frac{1}{2}x^2 + C) = \frac{1}{2}x + C/x$ . Another method: $d(xy) = d(x^2/2)$ .
