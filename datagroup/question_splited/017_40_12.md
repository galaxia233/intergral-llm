40.12 Find an equation of the sphere with the points $P(7, -1, -2)$ and $Q(3, 4, 6)$ as the ends of a diameter.

The center is the midpoint of the segment $PQ$ , namely, $(5, \frac{3}{2}, 4)$ . The length of the diameter is $\sqrt{(7 - 3)^2 + (-1 - 4)^2 + (2 - 6)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 25 + 16} = \sqrt{57}$ . So, the radius is $\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{57}$ . Thus, an equation of the sphere is $(x - 5)^2 + (y - \frac{3}{2})^2 + (z - 4)^2 = \frac{57}{4}$ .
