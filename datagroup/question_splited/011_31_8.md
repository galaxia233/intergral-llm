31.31 The region under $y = 4 - x^2$ in the first quadrant (Fig. 31-9).

The area $A = \int_0^2 (4 - x^2) dx = (4x - \frac{1}{3} x^3) \bigg]_0^2 = \frac{16}{3}$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^2 x(4 - x^2) dx = 2x^2 - \frac{1}{4} x^4 \bigg]_0^2 = 8 - 4 = 4$ . Thus, $\bar{x} = \frac{4}{\frac{16}{3}} = \frac{3}{4}$ . The moment about the $x$ -axis is $M_x = \int_0^4 y \sqrt{4 - y} dy$ . Let $u = 4 - y$ , $du = -dy$ . Then $M_x = -\int_4^0 (4 - u) \sqrt{u} du = \int_0^4 (4 \sqrt{u} - u^{3/2}) du = (\frac{8}{3} u^{3/2} - \frac{2}{5} u^{5/2}) \bigg]_0^4 = \frac{64}{3} - \frac{64}{5} = \frac{128}{15}$ . Thus, $\bar{y} = \frac{\frac{128}{15}}{\frac{16}{3}} = \frac{8}{5}$ .
