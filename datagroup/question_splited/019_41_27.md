41.93 Find a spherical equation for the surface whose rectangular equation is $x^{2} + y^{2} = 4$ .

Of course, the surface is the right circular cylinder with radius 2 and the $z$ -axis as axis of symmetry. Since $x^{2} + y^{2} = r^{2} = \rho^{2}\sin^{2}\phi$ , we have $\rho^2\sin^2\phi = 4$ . This can be reduced to $\rho \sin \phi = 2$ . (The other possibility $\rho \sin \phi = -2$ yields no additional points.)
