20.67 Use the trapezoidal rule with $n = 10$ to approximate $\int_0^1 x^3 dx$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {0} ^ {1} x ^ {3} d x \approx \frac {1}{2 0} \left[ 0 ^ {3} + 1 ^ {3} + 2 \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {9} \left(\frac {i}{1 0}\right) ^ {3} \right] = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(1 + 2 \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {9} \frac {i ^ {3}}{1 0 0 0}\right) \\ = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(1 + \frac {1}{5 0 0} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {9} i ^ {3}\right) = \frac {1}{2 0} \left[ 1 + \frac {1}{5 0 0} \left(\frac {9 \cdot 1 0}{2}\right) ^ {2} \right] \quad [ b y \\ = \frac {1}{2 0} \left(1 + \frac {8 1}{2 0}\right) = 0. 0 5 (5. 0 5) = 0. 2 5 2 5 \\ \end{array}
$$

The actual value is $\left[\frac{1}{4} x^4\right]_0^1 = \frac{1}{4}$ , by the fundamental theorem of calculus.
