30.12 $\int \frac{dx}{x(x^2 + 5)}$ .

$\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 5)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{Bx + C}{x^2 + 5}$ . So $1 = A(x^2 + 5) + Bx^2 + Cx$ . Let $x = 0$ . Then $1 = 5A$ , $A = \frac{1}{5}$ . Equate coefficients of $x^2$ : $0 = A + B$ , $B = -A = -\frac{1}{5}$ . Equate coefficients of $x$ : $0 = C$ . Hence, $\frac{1}{x(x^2 + 5)} = \frac{1}{5}\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{5}\frac{x}{x^2 + 5}$ , and $\int \frac{dx}{x(x^2 + 5)} = \frac{1}{5}\left(\ln |x| - \frac{1}{2}\ln |x^2 + 5|\right) + C_1 = \frac{1}{5}\ln \left|\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 + 5}}\right| + C_1$ .
