20.58 Find $\lim_{h\to 0}\left(\frac{1}{h}\int_{2}^{2 + h}\sqrt{t^2 + 2} dt\right)$

Let $g(x) = \int_{0}^{x}\sqrt{t^2 + 2} dt$ . Then

$$
\frac {1}{h} \int_ {2} ^ {2 + h} \sqrt {t ^ {2} + 2} d t = \frac {g (2 + h) - g (2)}{h}
$$

Therefore, the desired limit is

$$
\lim  _ {h \rightarrow 0} \frac {g (2 + h) - g (2)}{h} = g ^ {\prime} (2) = \sqrt {2 ^ {2} + 2} = \sqrt {6}
$$
