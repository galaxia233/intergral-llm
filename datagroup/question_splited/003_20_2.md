20.2 Calculate $\int_0^1 5x^2 dx$ by the direct definition of the integral.

Divide [0, 1] into $n$ equal subintervals, each of length $\Delta_{i}x = 1 / n$ . In the $i$ th subinterval, choose $x_{i}^{*}$ to be the right endpoint $i / n$ . Then the approximating sum is

$$
\sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} f \left(x _ {i} ^ {*}\right) \Delta_ {i} x = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} 5 \left(\frac {i}{n}\right) ^ {2} \frac {1}{n} = \frac {5}{n ^ {3}} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} i ^ {2} = \frac {5}{n ^ {3}} \frac {n (n + 1) (2 n + 1)}{6} = \frac {5}{6} \left(\frac {n + 1}{n}\right) \left(\frac {2 n + 1}{n}\right) = \frac {5}{6} \left(1 + \frac {1}{n}\right) \left(2 + \frac {1}{n}\right)
$$

As we make the subdivision finer by letting $n \to +\infty$ , the approximating sum approaches $\frac{5}{8} \cdot 1 \cdot 2 = \frac{5}{3}$ , which is the value of the integral.
