44.13 Evaluate $I = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} (x - y) \, dA$ , where $\mathcal{R}$ is the region above the $x$ -axis bounded by $y^2 = 3x$ and $y^2 = 4 - x$ (see Fig. 44-4).

It is convenient to evaluate $I$ by means of strips parallel to the $x$ -axis. $I = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \int_{y^{-3}}^{4 - y^2} (x - y) \, dx \, dy = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \left( \frac{1}{2} x^2 - yx \right) \int_{y^2 / 3}^{4 - y^2} dy = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \left[ \frac{1}{2} (4 - y^2)^2 - y(4 - y^2) \right] - \left[ \frac{1}{2} (y^2 / 3)^2 - y^3 / 3 \right] dy = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \left( 8 - 4y^2 + \frac {1}{2} y^4 - 4y + y^3 - \frac {1}{18} y^4 - \frac {1}{3} y^3 \right) dy = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} \left( 8 - 4y - 4y^2 + \frac {2}{3} y^3 + \frac {4}{9} y^4 \right) dy = 8y - 2y^2 - \frac {4}{3} y^3 + \frac {1}{6} y^4 + \frac {4}{45} y^5 \int_{0}^{\sqrt{3}} = 8\sqrt{3} - 6 - 4\sqrt{3} + \frac {3}{2} + \frac {4}{5}\sqrt{3} = \frac {24}{5}\sqrt{3} - \frac {9}{2}$ .
