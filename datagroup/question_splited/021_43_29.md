43.29 Find all relative maxima and minima of $f(x, y) = 2x^{2} + y^{2} + 6xy + 10x - 6y + 5$ .

$f_{x} = 4x + 6y + 10, f_{y} = 2y + 6x - 6$ . Setting $f_{x} = 0$ and $f_{y} = 0$ , we get $2x + 3y + 5 = 0$ and $3x + y - 3 = 0$ . Solving simultaneously, we have $x = 2, y = -3$ . Further, $f_{xy} = 6, f_{xx} = 4, f_{yy} = 2$ . So, $\Delta = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2 = -28 < 0$ . By Problem 43.23, there is no relative maximum or minimum.
