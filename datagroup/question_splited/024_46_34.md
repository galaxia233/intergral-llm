46.34 Find a curve that passes through $(2,1)$ and has at each point $(x,y)$ a normal line with slope $\frac{2xy}{y^2 - x^2}$ .

The slope of the tangent line is the negative reciprocal of the slope of the normal line. Hence, $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2 - y^2}{2xy}$ . Let $y = vx$ . Then $v + x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{x^2 - x^2v^2}{2x(vx)} = \frac{1 - v^2}{2v}$ . Thus, $x\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{1 - v^2}{2v} - v = \frac{1 - 3v^2}{2v}$ . Now $x$ and $v$ are separable. $\int_{0}^{1} \frac{dv}{1 - 3v^2} dv = \int_{0}^{1} \frac{dx}{x}, -\frac{1}{3} \ln |1 - 3v^2| = \ln |x| + \ln C_1, |1 - 3v^2|^{-1/3} = C_1|x|$ , $1 - 3v^2 = C_2 / x^3$ , $1 - 3(y^2 / x^2) = C_2 / x^3$ , $x^3 - 3xy^2 = C_2$ . Since the curve passes through $(2, 1)$ , $8 - 6 = C_2$ . Thus, $x^3 - 3xy^2 = 2$ .
