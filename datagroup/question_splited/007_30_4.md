30.4 $\int \frac{2x^2 + 1}{(x - 1)(x - 2)(x - 3)} dx.$

$\frac{2x^2 + 1}{(x - 1)(x - 2)(x - 3)} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{B}{x - 2} + \frac{C}{x - 3}$ . Then $2x^2 + 1 = A(x - 2)(x - 3) + B(x - 1)(x - 3) + C(x - 1)(x - 2)$ . Let $x = 1$ . Then $3 = 2A$ , $A = \frac{3}{2}$ . Let $x = 2$ . Then $9 = -B$ , $B = -9$ . Let $x = 3$ . Then $19 = 2C$ , $C = \frac{19}{2}$ . Thus, $\frac{2x^2 + 1}{(x - 1)(x - 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{3}{2}\frac{1}{x - 1} \cdot \frac{9}{-2} + \frac{19}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{x - 3}$ . Hence, $\int \frac{2x^2 + 1}{(x - 1)(x - 2)(x - 3)} dx = \frac{3}{2} \ln |x - 1| - 9 \ln |x - 2| + \frac{19}{2} \ln |x - 3| + C_1 = \frac{1}{2} \ln \left| \frac{-1)^3 (x - 3)^{19}}{(x - 2)^{18}} \right| + C_1$ .
