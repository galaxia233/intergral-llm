【1828】 $\int \mathrm{e}^{\alpha x}\cos bx\mathrm{d}x.$

解 如果 $a, b$ 同时为零，积分显然为 $x + C$ ；若 $a = 0, b \neq 0$ ，积分显然为 $\frac{1}{b} \sin bx + C$ ；以下设 $a \neq 0$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int \mathrm {e} ^ {a x} \cos b x \mathrm {d} x = \frac {1}{a} \int \cos b x \mathrm {d} \left(\mathrm {e} ^ {a x}\right) = \frac {1}{a} \mathrm {e} ^ {a x} \cos b x + \frac {b}{a} \int \mathrm {e} ^ {a x} \sin x \mathrm {d} x = \frac {1}{a} \mathrm {e} ^ {a x} \cos b x + \frac {b}{a ^ {2}} \int \sin b x \mathrm {d} \left(\mathrm {e} ^ {a x}\right) \\ = \frac {1}{a} e ^ {a x} \cos b x + \frac {b}{a ^ {2}} e ^ {a x} \sin b x - \frac {b ^ {2}}{a ^ {2}} \int e ^ {a x} \cos b x d x. \\ \end{array}
$$

于是，得 $\int \mathrm{e}^{ax}\cos bx\mathrm{d}x = \frac{a^2}{a^2 + b^2}\left[\frac{1}{a}\mathrm{e}^{ax}\cos bx + \frac{b}{a^2}\mathrm{e}^{ax}\sin bx\right] + C = \frac{\mathrm{e}^{ax}(a\cos bx + b\sin bx)}{a^2 + b^2} +C.$
