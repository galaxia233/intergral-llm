20.60 The region above the $x$ -axis and under the curve $y = \sin x$ , between $x = 0$ and $x = \pi$ , is divided into two parts by the line $x = c$ . If the area of the left part is one-third the area of the right part, find $c$ .

$\int_0^c\sin xdx = \frac{1}{3}\int_c^\pi \sin xdx, - \cos x]_0^c = \frac{1}{3} (-\cos x)]_c^\pi , - (\cos c - \cos 0) = -\frac{1}{3} (\cos \pi -\cos c),$ $\cos c - 1 = \frac{1}{3} (-1 - \cos c), 3\cos c - 3 = -1 - \cos c, 4\cos c = 2, \cos c = \frac{1}{2}, c = \pi /3.$
