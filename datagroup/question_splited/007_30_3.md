30.3 $\int \frac{x^4 - 4x^2 + x + 1}{x^2 - 4} dx.$

Since the degree of the numerator is at least as great as that of the denominator, carry out the long division, obtaining $\frac{x^4 - 4x^2 + x + 1}{x^2 - 4} = x^2 +\frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 4}$ . Thus, $\int \frac{x^4 - 4x^2 + x + 1}{x^2 - 4} dx = \frac{1}{3} x^3 +\int \frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 4} dx$ . But $\frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 4} = \frac{x + 1}{(x + 2)(x - 2)} = \frac{A}{x + 2} +\frac{B}{x - 2}$ . Then $x + 1 = A(x - 2) + B(x + 2)$ . Let $x = 2$ . Then $3 = 4B$ , $B = \frac{3}{4}$ . Let $x = -2$ . Then $-1 = -4A$ , $A = \frac{1}{4}$ . Thus, $\frac{x + 1}{(x + 2)(x - 2)} = \frac{1}{4}\frac{1}{x + 2} +\frac{3}{4}\frac{1}{x - 2}$ , and $\int \frac{x + 1}{(x + 2)(x - 2)} dx = \frac{1}{4}\ln |x + 2| + \frac{3}{4}\ln |x - 2| + C = \frac{1}{4}\ln |(x + 2)(x - 2)^3| + C$ . Hence, the complete answer is $\frac{1}{3} x^3 +\frac{1}{4}\ln |(x + 2)(x - 2)^3| + C$ .
