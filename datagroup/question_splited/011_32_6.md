32.6 Evaluate $\int_{a}^{\infty}xe^{-x}dx$

By integration by parts, we find $\int xe^{-x}dx = -e^{-x}(x + 1)$ . Hence, $\int_{a}^{\infty}xe^{-x}dx = \lim_{v\to +\infty}(-e^{-x}(x + 1))_a^v = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\left[-e^{-v}(v + 1) + e^{-a}(a + 1)\right] = e^{-a}(a + 1)$ . [In the last step, we used L'HûÇpital's rule to evaluate $\lim_{v\to +\infty}\frac{1}{v + 1} / e^{v} = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\frac{1}{v + 1} / e^{v} = 0$ .]
