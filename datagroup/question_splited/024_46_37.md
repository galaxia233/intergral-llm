46.37 Find a curve in the $xy$ -plane passing through the point $(1, 1)$ and for which the normal line at any point $(x, y)$ has slope $-xy^2$ .

The tangent line has slope $\frac{1}{xy^2}$ . So $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{xy^2}$ . Hence, $\int y^2 dy = \int \frac{1}{x} dx$ , $\frac{1}{3} y^3 = \ln |x| + C_1$ , $y^3 = 3\ln |x| + C$ . Since the curve passes through $(1,1)$ , $1 = 3\ln 1 + C$ , $1 = 3 \cdot 0 + C$ , $C = 1$ . Therefore, $y^3 = 3\ln |x| + 1$ , or $x = e^{(y^3 - 1) / 3}$ . [This is the branch through $(1,1)]$ .
