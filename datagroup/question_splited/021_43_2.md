43.2 Show that the direction of the gradient $\nabla f$ is the direction in which the derivative achieves its maximum, $|\nabla f|$ , and the direction of $-\nabla f$ is the direction in which the derivative achieves its minimum, $-|\nabla f|$ .

The derivative in the direction of a unit vector $\mathbf{u}$ is $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = |\nabla f| \cos \theta$ , where $\theta$ is the angle between $\nabla f$ and $\mathbf{u}$ . Since $\cos \theta$ takes on its maximum value 1 when $\theta = 0$ , the maximum value of $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u}$ is obtained when $\mathbf{u}$ is the unit vector in the direction of $\nabla f$ . That maximum value is $|\nabla f|$ . Similarly, since $\cos \theta$ takes on its minimum value $-1$ when $\theta = \pi$ , the minimum value of $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u}$ is attained when $\mathbf{u}$ has the direction of $-\nabla f$ . That minimum value is $-|\nabla f|$ .
