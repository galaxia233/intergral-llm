44.75 Find the mass of a circular plate $\mathcal{R}$ of radius $a$ whose density is numerically equal to the distance from the center.

Let the circle be $r = a$ . Then $M = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} r \, dA = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{a} r \cdot r \, dr \, d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \frac{1}{3} r^3 \bigg]_{0}^{a} \, d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \frac{1}{3} a^3 \, d\theta = \frac{1}{3} a^3 \cdot 2\pi = \frac{2}{3}\pi a^3.$
