39.22 How large may the angle $x$ be taken if the values of $\cos x$ are to be computed using three terms of the Taylor series about $\pi / 3$ and if the computation is to be correct to four decimal places?

Since $f^{(4)}(x) = \sin x$ , the Lagrange remainder $|R_3(x)| = \frac{|\sin c|}{3!} |x - \pi / 3|^3 \leq \frac{1}{6} |x - \pi / 3|^3 = 0.00005$ . Then, $|x - \pi / 3| \leq \sqrt[3]{0.0003} \approx 0.0669$ . Hence, $x$ can lie between $\pi / 3 + 0.0669$ and $\pi / 3 - 0.0669$ . (0.0669 radian is about $3^\circ 50'$ .)
