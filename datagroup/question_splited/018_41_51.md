41.51 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^4}$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then

$$
\frac {2 x y ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {4}} = \frac {2 x \left(m ^ {2} x ^ {2}\right)}{x ^ {2} + m ^ {4} x ^ {4}} = \frac {2 m ^ {2} x}{1 + m ^ {4} x ^ {2}} \rightarrow \frac {0}{1} = 0
$$

as $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ . However, if we let $x = y^2$ , then $\frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^4} = \frac{2y^4}{y^4 + y^4} = 1$ . Hence, as $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ along the parabola $x = y^2$ , $\frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^4} \to 1$ . Therefore, $\lim_{(x, y) \to (0, 0)} \frac{2xy^2}{x^2 + y^4}$ does not exist.
