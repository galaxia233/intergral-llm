33.7 Use vector methods to find an equation of the line $M$ through the point $P_{1}(2, 3)$ that is perpendicular to the line $L: x + 2y + 5 = 0$ .

By Problem 33.6, $\mathbf{A} = (1,2)$ is perpendicular to the line $L$ . Let $P(x,y)$ be any point on the line $M$ . $\overrightarrow{P_1P} = (x - 2,y - 3)$ is parallel to $M$ . So, $(x - 2,y - 3) = c(1,2)$ for some scalar $c$ . Hence, $x - 2 = c$ , $y - 3 = 2c$ . So, $y - 3 = 2(x - 2)$ , $y = 2x - 1$ .
