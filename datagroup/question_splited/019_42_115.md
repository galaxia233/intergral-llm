42.115 If a surface has an equation of the form $z = x f(x / y)$ , show that all of its tangent planes have a common point.

A normal vector to the surface at $(x,y,z)$ is

$$
\left(\partial z / \partial x, \partial z / \partial y, - 1\right) = \left(x f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right) \left(\frac {1}{y}\right) + f \left(\frac {x}{y}\right), x f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x}{y}\right) \left(- \frac {x}{y ^ {2}}\right), - 1\right).
$$

Hence, the tangent plane at $(x_0,y_0,z_0)$ has an equation

$$
\left[ \frac {x _ {0}}{y _ {0}} f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x _ {0}}{y _ {0}}\right) + f \left(\frac {x _ {0}}{y _ {0}}\right) \right] x - \left[ \frac {x _ {0} ^ {2}}{y _ {0} ^ {2}} f ^ {\prime} \left(\frac {x _ {0}}{y _ {0}}\right) \right] y - z = 0
$$

Thus, the plane goes through the origin.
