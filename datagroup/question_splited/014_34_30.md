34.32 Find the arc length of the circle $x = a\cos \theta, y = a\sin \theta, 0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi.$

Recall that the arc length $s = \int_{a}^{b} \sqrt{(dx / du)^2 + (dy / du)^2} du$ , where $u$ is the parameter. In this case, $dx / d\theta = -a \sin \theta$ , $dy / d\theta = a \cos \theta$ , and $s = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \sqrt{a^2 \sin^2 \theta + a^2 \cos^2 \theta} d\theta = a \int_{0}^{2\pi} d\theta = a(2\pi - 0) = 2\pi a$ , the standard formula for the circumference of a circle of radius $a$ .
