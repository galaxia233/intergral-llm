42.26 Find the slope of the tangent line to the curve that is the intersection of the surface $z = x^2 - y^2$ with the plane $x = 2$ , at the point (2, 1, 3).

In the plane $x = 2$ , $x$ is constant. Hence, the slope of the tangent line to the curve is the derivative $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -2y = -2(1) = -2$ .
