34.90 Show that there is a scalar function $f(t)$ such that $\mathbf{N}'(t) = f(t)\mathbf{T}(t)$ , and find a formula for $f(t)$ .

Parameterize the curve as $\mathbf{R}(t) = (t, t^3)$ .

Then $\mathbf{R}'(t) = (1, 3t^2), \quad |\mathbf{R}'(t)| = \sqrt{1 + 9t^4}, \quad \mathbf{T}(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 9t^4}} (1, 3t^2).$

$$
\mathbf {T} ^ {\prime} (t) = \frac {1}{\sqrt {1 + 9 t ^ {4}}} (0, 6 t) - \frac {1 8 t ^ {3}}{(1 + 9 t ^ {4}) ^ {3 / 2}} (1, 3 t ^ {2})
$$

So, $\mathbf{T}'(0) = \mathbf{0}$ and, therefore, $\mathbf{N}(0)$ is not defined.
