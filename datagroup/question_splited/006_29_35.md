29.35 Find $\int (1 + \cos ax)^{3 / 2}dx$

From the identity $\cos^2 x = \frac {1 + \cos 2x}{2}$ , we get $\cos^2\frac {ax}{2} +\frac {1 + \cos ax}{2}$ . Hence, $\int (1 + \cos ax)^{3 / 2}dx = 2\sqrt{2}\int \cos^3{\frac {ax}{2}}dx = 2\sqrt{2}\left(\int \cos {\frac {ax}{2}}dx - \int \sin^2{\frac {ax}{2}}\cos {\frac {ax}{2}}dx\right) = 2\sqrt{2}\left(\frac {2}{a}\sin {\frac {ax}{2}} - \frac {2}{a}\cdot \frac {1}{3}\sin^3{\frac {ax}{2}} + C\right) = \frac {4\sqrt{2}}{a}\sin {\frac {ax}{2}}\left(1 - \frac {1}{3}\sin^2{\frac {ax}{2}}\right) + C.$
