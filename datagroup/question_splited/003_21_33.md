21.33 Find the length of the arc of $6xy = x^4 + 3$ from $x = 1$ to $x = 2$ .

$y = (x^{4} + 3) / 6x = \frac{1}{6} (x^{3} + 3x^{-1})$ Then $y^\prime = \frac{1}{6} (3x^2 -3x^{-2}) = \frac{1}{2} [x^2 -(1 / x^2)] = (x^4 -1) / 2x^2.$ So,

$$
(y ^ {\prime}) ^ {2} = \frac {x ^ {8} - 2 x ^ {4} + 1}{4 x ^ {4}} \quad \text {a n d} \quad 1 + (y ^ {\prime}) ^ {2} = \frac {4 x ^ {4} + x ^ {8} - 2 x ^ {4} + 1}{4 x ^ {4}} = \frac {x ^ {8} + 2 x ^ {4} + 1}{4 x ^ {4}} = \left(\frac {x ^ {4} + 1}{2 x ^ {2}}\right) ^ {2}
$$

Then $L = \frac{1}{2}\int_{1}^{2}\frac{x^4 + 1}{x^2} dx = \frac{1}{2}\int_{1}^{2}(x^2 +x^{-2})dx = \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{3} x^3 -x^{-1})]_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} [(\frac{8}{3} -\frac{1}{2}) - (\frac{1}{3} -1)] = \frac{17}{12}.$
