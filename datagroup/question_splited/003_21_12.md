21.12 The bounded region in the first quadrant between the curves $4y + 3x = 7$ and $y = x^{-2}$ .

See Fig. 21-12. First we find the points of intersection: $\frac{4}{x^2} + 3x = 7$ , $4 + 3x^3 = 7x^2$ , $3x^3 - 7x^2 + 4 = 0$ . $x = 1$ is an obvious root. Dividing $3x^3 - 7x^2 + 4$ by $x - 1$ , we obtain $3x^2 - 4x - 4 = (3x + 2)(x - 2)$ . Hence, the other roots are $x = 2$ and $x = -\frac{2}{3}$ . So, the intersection points in the first quadrant are $(1, 1)$ and $(2, \frac{1}{4})$ . The upper boundary is the line and the lower boundary is $y = x^{-2}$ . The area is given by $\int_{1}^{2} \left[\frac{1}{4}(7 - 3x) - x^{-2}\right] dx = \left(\frac{1}{4}\left(7x - \frac{3}{2}x^2\right) + x^{-1}\right)\bigg|_{1}^{2} = \left[\frac{1}{4}(14 - 6) + \frac{1}{2}\right] - \left[\frac{1}{4}\left(7 - \frac{3}{2}\right) + 1\right] = \frac{1}{8}$ .
