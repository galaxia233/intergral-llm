44.21 Find the volume $V$ of the solid bounded by the right circular cylinder $x^{2} + y^{2} = 1$ , the $xy$ -plane, and the plane $x + z = 1$ .

As seen in Fig. 44-10, the base is the circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = 1$ in the $xy$ -plane, the top is the plane $x + z = 1$ . $V = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} (1 - x) \, dA = \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-\sqrt{1 - x^2}}^{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} (1 - x) \, dy \, dx = \int_{-1}^{1} (1 - x)y \bigg]_{-\sqrt{1 - x^2}}^{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} dx = \int_{-1}^{1} 2(1 - x)\sqrt{1 - x^2} \, dx = 2\int_{-1}^{1}\sqrt{1 - x^2} \, dx = 2(\pi / 2) + \frac{2}{3}(1 - x^2)^{3/2}]_{-1}^{1} = \pi + \frac{2}{3}(0) = \pi.$ (Note: We know that $\int_{-1}^{1}\sqrt{1 - x^2} \, dx = \pi / 2$ , since the integral is the area of the unit semicircle.)
