35.66 Find the area inside the cardioid $r = 1 + \cos \theta$ and outside the circle $r = 1$ .

In Fig. 35-15, area $ABC =$ area $OBC -$ area $OAC$ is one-half the required area. Thus, the area is $2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \left[ (1 + \cos \theta)^2 - (1)^2 \right] d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} (2 \cos \theta + \cos^2 \theta) d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} [2 \cos \theta + \frac{1}{2} (1 + \cos 2\theta)] d\theta = (\frac{1}{2}\theta + 2\sin \theta + \frac{1}{4}\sin 2\theta) \int_{0}^{\pi/2} (\pi/4) + 2.$
