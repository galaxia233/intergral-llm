34.69 Show that, if $\mathbf{B} \neq \mathbf{0}$ , then $\mathbf{R}(t) = \mathbf{A} + t\mathbf{B}$ represents a straight line and has constant velocity vector and zero acceleration vector.

It is clear from the parallelogram law (Fig. 34-14) that $\mathbf{R}(t)$ generates the line $\mathcal{L}$ that passes through the endpoint of $\mathbf{A}$ and is parallel to $\mathbf{B}$ . We have:

$$
\mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (t) = \frac {d}{d t} (\mathbf {A}) + \frac {d}{d t} (t \mathbf {B}) = \mathbf {0} + t \frac {d \mathbf {B}}{d t} + \mathbf {B} = \mathbf {0} + t \mathbf {0} + \mathbf {B} = \mathbf {B}
$$

Further, $\mathbf{R}''(t) = d\mathbf{B} / dt = \mathbf{0}$ .
