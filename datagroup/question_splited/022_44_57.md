44.56 Find the surface area of the part of the sphere $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = 25$ between the planes $z = 2$ and $z = 4$ .

The surface lies above the ring-shaped region $\mathcal{R}$ : $3 \leq r \leq \sqrt{21}$ . $S = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} \sqrt{1 + \frac{x^2}{z^2} + \frac{y^2}{z^2}} dA = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} \sqrt{\frac{25}{z^2}} dA = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} \frac{5}{z} dA = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_3^{\sqrt{21}} \frac{5}{\sqrt{25 - r^2}} r dr d\theta$ , since $r^2 + z^2 = 25$ . Hence, $S = \int_0^{2\pi} -5\sqrt{25 - r^2}\bigg|_3^{\sqrt{21}}d\theta = -5\int_0^{2\pi}(2 - 4)d\theta = 10\int_0^{2\pi}d\theta = 20\pi$ .
