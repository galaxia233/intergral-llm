43.10 Show that the sum of the squares of the directional derivatives of $z = f(x, y)$ at any point is constant for any two mutually perpendicular directions and is equal to the square of the gradient.

This is simply the Pythagorean theorem: If $\pmb{u}$ and $\pmb{v}$ are mutually perpendicular unit vectors in the plane, then Problem 33.4 shows that $\nabla f = (\nabla f \cdot \pmb{u})\pmb{u} + (\nabla f \cdot \pmb{v})\pmb{v}$ . A direct computation of $\nabla f \cdot \nabla f$ then gives $|\nabla f|^2 = (\nabla f \cdot \pmb{u})^2 + (\nabla f \cdot \pmb{v})^2$ .
