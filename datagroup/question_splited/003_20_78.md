20.78 Find the distance traveled by an object moving along a line with velocity $v = (2 - t) / \sqrt{t}$ from $t = 4$ to $t = 9$ .

The distance is $s = \int_{4}^{9} |v| dt$ . Between $t = 4$ and $t = 9$ , $v = (2 - t) / \sqrt{t}$ is negative. Hence, $s = \int_{4}^{9} \frac{t - 2}{\sqrt{t}} dt = \int_{4}^{9} (t^{1/2} - 2t^{-1/2}) dt = (\frac{2}{3} t^{3/2} - 4t^{1/2})]_{4}^{9} = (18 - 12) - (\frac{16}{3} - 8) = \frac{26}{3}$ .
