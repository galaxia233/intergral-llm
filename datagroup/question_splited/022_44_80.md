44.79 Find the mass of a spherical surface $\mathcal{S}$ whose density is equal to the distance from a fixed diametral plane.

Let $\mathcal{S}$ be $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = a^{2}$ , and let the fixed diametral plane be the $xy$ -plane. Then $M = \iint_{\mathcal{C}} |z| \frac{dS}{dA} dA$ , where $\mathcal{C}$ is the disk $x^{2} + y^{2} = a^{2}$ . Then, if we double the mass of the upper hemisphere, $M = 2 \iint_{\mathcal{C}} z \sqrt{1 + \left( \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \right)^{2} + \left( \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \right)^{2}} dA$ . $\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -\frac{x}{z}$ and $\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -\frac{y}{z}$ . Then $1 + \left( \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \right)^{2} + \left( \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \right)^{2} = 1 + \frac{x^{2}}{z^{2}} + \frac{y^{2}}{z^{2}} = \frac{x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2}}{z^{2}} = \frac{a^{2}}{z^{2}}$ . Hence, $M = 2 \iint_{\mathcal{C}} a dA = 2a \iint_{\mathcal{C}} dA = 2a$ (area of a circle of radius $a$ ) = $2a(\pi a^{2}) = 2\pi a^{3}$ .
