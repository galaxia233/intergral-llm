40.47 If $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = 0$ , what can be concluded about the configuration of $\mathbf{A}$ , $\mathbf{B}$ , and $\mathbf{C}$ ?

One possibility is that $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ , which, by Problem 40.46, means that $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{0}$ or $\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ or $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ are parallel. Another possibility is that $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{0}$ . If $\mathbf{A} \neq \mathbf{0}$ and $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} \neq \mathbf{0}$ , then $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = 0$ means that $\mathbf{A} \perp (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$ , which is equivalent to $\mathbf{A}$ lying in the plane determined by $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ (and therefore not codetermining a parallelepiped with $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ ).
