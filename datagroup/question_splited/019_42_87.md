42.87 A point $P$ is moving along the curve of intersection of the paraboloid $\frac{x^2}{16} - \frac{y^2}{9} = z$ and the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 5$ . If $x$ is increasing at the rate of $5\mathrm{cm/s}$ , how fast is $z$ changing when $x = 2\mathrm{cm}$ and $y = 1\mathrm{cm}$ ?

Apply the chain rule to $z = \frac{x^2}{16} - \frac{y^2}{9}$ . $\frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} \frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} \frac{dy}{dt} = \frac{x}{8} \frac{dx}{dt} - \frac{2y}{9} \frac{dy}{dt}$ . From $x^2 + y^2 = 5$ , $x \frac{dx}{dt} + y \frac{dy}{dt} = 0$ . Since $dx/dt = 5$ , $5x + y \frac{dy}{dt} = 0$ . So, when $x = 2$ and $y = 1$ , $dy/dt = -10$ , and $dz/dt = \frac{2}{8}(5) - \frac{2}{9}(-10) = 10(\frac{1}{8} + \frac{2}{9}) = \frac{125}{36}\mathrm{cm/s}$ .
