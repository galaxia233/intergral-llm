41.94 Find the graph of the spherical equation $\rho = 2a\sin \phi$ where $a > 0$

Since $\theta$ is not present in the equation, the surface is obtained by rotating about the $z$ -axis the intersection of the surface with the $yz$ -plane. In the latter plane, $\rho = 2a\sin \phi$ becomes $\sqrt{y^2 + z^2} = 2a\frac{y}{\sqrt{y^2 + z^2}}$ , $y^2 + z^2 = 2ay$ , $(y - a)^2 + z^2 = a^2$ . This is a circle with center $(a,0)$ and radius $a$ . So, the resulting surface is obtained by rotating that circle about the $z$ -axis. The result can be thought of as a doughnut (torus) with no hole in the middle.
