42.17 If $z = \ln \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$ , show that $x\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} +y\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = 1$

Because $z = \frac{1}{2}\ln (x^{2} + y^{2})$ , we have $\partial z / \partial x = \frac{1}{2}[2x / (x^{2} + y^{2})] = x / (x^{2} + y^{2})$ and $\partial z / \partial y = \frac{1}{2}[2y / (x^{2} + y^{2})] = y / (x^{2} + y^{2})$ . Therefore,

$$
x \frac {\partial z}{\partial x} + y \frac {\partial z}{\partial y} = \frac {x ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} + \frac {y ^ {2}}{x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}} = 1
$$
