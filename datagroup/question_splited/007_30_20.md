30.20 $\int \frac{x^2 + 2}{x(x^2 + 5x + 6)} dx.$

Since $x^{2} + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)$ , we have $\frac{x^2 + 2}{x(x + 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x + 2} + \frac{C}{x + 3}$ . Then $x^{2} + 2 = A(x + 2)(x + 3) + Bx(x + 3) + Cx(x + 2)$ . Let $x = 0$ . Then $2 = 6A$ , $A = \frac{1}{3}$ . Let $x = -2$ . Then $6 = -2B$ , $B = -3$ . Let $x = -3$ . Then $11 = 3C$ , $C = \frac{11}{3}$ . Thus, $\frac{x^2 + 2}{x(x + 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{1}{3} \frac{1}{x} - \frac{3}{x + 2} + \frac{11}{3} \frac{1}{x + 3}$ . Hence, $\int \frac{x^2 + 2}{x(x + 2)(x + 3)} dx = \frac{1}{3} \ln |x| - 3 \ln |x + 2| + \frac{11}{3} \ln |x + 3| + C_1.$
