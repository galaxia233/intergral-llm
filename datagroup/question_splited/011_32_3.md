32.3 For what values of $p$ is $\int_1^\infty (1 / x)^p dx$ convergent?

By Problem 32.1, we know that the integral is divergent when $p = 1$ .

$$
\int_ {1} ^ {\infty} \frac {1}{x ^ {p}} d x = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \int_ {1} ^ {v} \frac {1}{x ^ {p}} d x = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \left(- \frac {1}{p - 1} \cdot \frac {1}{x ^ {p - 1}}\right) \Bigg ] _ {1} ^ {v} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow + \infty} \left[ - \frac {1}{p - 1} \left(\frac {1}{v ^ {p - 1}} - 1\right)\right]
$$

The last limit is $1 / (p - 1)$ if $p > 1$ , and $+\infty$ if $p < 1$ . Thus, the integral converges if and only if $p > 1$ .
