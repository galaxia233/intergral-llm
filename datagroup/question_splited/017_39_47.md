39.47 Find the Taylor series for $\sqrt{x}$ about 2.

$\sqrt{x} = \sqrt{2 + (x - 2)} = \sqrt{2}\sqrt{1 + [(x - 2) / 2]}$ . Recall from Problem 38.104 that

$$
\sqrt {1 + x} = 1 + \sum_ {n = 1} ^ {\infty} \frac {\frac {1}{2} (\frac {1}{2} - 1) \cdots (\frac {1}{2} - n + 1)}{n !} x ^ {n}
$$

Hence, $\sqrt{1 + [(x - 2) / 2]} = 1 + \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty}\frac{\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2} - 1)\cdots(\frac{1}{2} - n + 1)}{n!}\frac{(x - 2)^n}{2^n}$

Thus, $\sqrt{x} = \sqrt{2} +\sqrt{2}\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty}\frac{1\cdot(1 - 2)\cdot(1 - 4)\cdot\cdots\cdot[1 - 2(n - 1)]}{n! \cdot 2^{2n}} (x - 2)^n$

$$
= \sqrt {2} + \sqrt {2} \sum_ {n = 1} ^ {\infty} (- 1) ^ {n - 1} \frac {1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot \cdots \cdot (2 n - 3)}{2 ^ {2 n} n !} (x - 2) ^ {n}
$$
