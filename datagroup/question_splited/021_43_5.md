43.5 Find the derivative of $f(x, y) = xy^2$ at (1, 3) in the direction toward (4, 5).

The indicated direction is that of the vector $(4,5) - (1,3) = (3,2)$ . The unit vector in that direction is $\mathbf{u} = (1 / \sqrt{13})$ (3,2); the gradient $\nabla f = (y^2, 2xy) = (9,6)$ . So, $\nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = (9,6) \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{13}}$ (3,2) = $\frac{1}{\sqrt{13}} 39 = 3\sqrt{13}$ .
