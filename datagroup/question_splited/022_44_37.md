44.37 Use polar coordinates to find the area of the region inside the circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = 9$ and to the right of the line $x = \frac{3}{2}$ .

![](images/9d820b9fc1a2310d358b6d18d145171a2929c0c2eb5a5e53e1787ce97aaeb565.jpg)  
Fig. 44-21

It suffices to double the area in the first quadrant. $x^{2} + y^{2} = 9$ becomes $r = 3$ in polar coordinates, and $x = \frac{3}{2}$ is equivalent to $r\cos \theta = \frac{3}{2}$ . From Fig. 44-21, $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi / 3$ . So the area is $2\int_{0}^{\pi / 3}\int_{3 / (2\cos \theta)}^{3}r dr d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi / 3}r^{2}\big]_{(3\sec \theta) / 2}^{3}d\theta = \int_{0}^{\pi / 3}\left(9 - \frac{9}{4}\sec^{2}\theta\right)d\theta = (9\theta - \frac{9}{4}\tan \theta)\big]_{0}^{\pi / 3} = 3\pi - (9\sqrt{3} / 8)$ .
