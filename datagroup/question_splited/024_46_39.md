46.39 Find the curve that passes through $(0, 4)$ such that every tangent line to the curve passes through $(1, 2)$ .

The slope of the tangent line at $(x, y)$ is $\frac{y - 2}{x - 1}$ . Hence, $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y - 2}{x - 1}$ . Thus, $\int \frac{dy}{y - 2} = \int \frac{dx}{x - 1}$ , $\ln |y - 2| = \ln |x - 1| + C$ , $\ln \left| \frac{y - 2}{x - 1} \right| = C$ , $\frac{y - 2}{x - 1} = C_1$ , $y - 2 = C_1(x - 1)$ . Since the curve passes through $(0, 4)$ , $2 = C_1(-1)$ , $C_1 = -2$ . Hence, $y - 2 = -2(x - 1)$ , $y = -2x + 4$ . Thus, the curve is the straight line
