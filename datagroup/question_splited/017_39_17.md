39.17 State Taylor's formula with Lagrange's form of the remainder and indicate how it is used to show that a function is represented by its Taylor series.

If $f(x)$ and its first $n$ derivatives are continuous on an open interval containing $a$ , then, for any $x$ in this interval, there is a number $c$ between $a$ and $x$ such that

$$
f (x) = f (a) + f ^ {\prime} (a) (x - a) + \frac {f ^ {\prime \prime} (a)}{2 !} (x - a) ^ {2} + \dots + \frac {f ^ {(n - 1)} (a)}{(n - 1) !} (x - a) ^ {n - 1} + R _ {n} (x)
$$

where $R_{n}(x) = \frac{f^{(n)}(c)}{n!} (x - a)^{n}$ . If $f(x)$ has continuous derivatives of all orders, then, for those $x$ for which $\lim_{n\to +\infty}R_n(x) = 0$ , $f(x)$ is equal to its Taylor series.
