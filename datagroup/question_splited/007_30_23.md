30.23 Evaluate $\int \frac{dx}{\sin x + \cos x}$

Use the substitution of Problem 29.45, followed by the method of partial fractions. Thus, let $z = \tan (x / 2)$ , $x = 2 \tan^{-1} z$ , $dx = \frac{2}{1 + z^2} dz$ , $\sin x = \frac{2z}{1 + z^2}$ , $\cos x = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 x} = \frac{1 - z^2}{1 + z^2}$ . Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int \frac {d x}{\sin x + \cos x} = \int \frac {- 2}{z ^ {2} - 2 z - 1} d z = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \int \frac {d z}{z - (1 - \sqrt {2})} - \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \int \frac {d z}{z - (1 + \sqrt {2})} \\ = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \ln | z - (1 - \sqrt {2}) | - \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \ln | z - (1 + \sqrt {2}) | + C \\ = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \ln \left| \frac {z - (1 - \sqrt {2})}{z - (1 + \sqrt {2})} \right| + C \\ = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2}} \ln \left| \frac {\tan (x / 2) - (1 - \sqrt {2})}{\tan (x / 2) - (1 + \sqrt {2})} \right| + C \\ \end{array}
$$
