44.50 Use spherical coordinates to find the volume of a sphere of radius $a$ .

In spherical coordinates a sphere of radius $a$ is characterized by $0 \leq \rho \leq a$ , $0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi$ , $0 \leq \phi \leq \pi$ . Recall that the volume element is given by $dV = \rho^2 \sin \phi d\rho d\theta d\phi$ . $V = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^a \rho^2 \sin \phi d\rho d\theta d\phi = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1}{3} \rho^3 \sin \phi \big]_0^a d\theta d\phi = \int_0^\pi \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1}{3} a^3 \sin \phi d\theta d\phi = \int_0^\pi \frac{1}{3} a^3 \sin \phi \cdot \theta \big]_0^{2\pi} d\phi = \int_0^\pi \frac{1}{3} a^3 \sin \phi \cdot 2\pi d\phi = \frac{2}{3} \pi a^3 \int_0^\pi \sin \phi d\phi = -\frac{2}{3} \pi a^3 \cos \phi \big]_0^\pi = -\frac{2}{3} \pi a^3 (-1 - 1) = \frac{4}{3} \pi a^3$ .
