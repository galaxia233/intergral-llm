40.55 Show that $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{C}$ (exchange of dot and cross).

$(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{C} = \mathbf{C} \cdot (\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) = \mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$ . The first equality follows from the definition of the dot product; the second is established by making two row interchanges in the determinant of Problem 40.49.
