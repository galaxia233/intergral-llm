21.6 The region bounded by the curves $y = \sqrt{x}, \quad y = 1, \quad \text{and} \quad x = 4$ .

See Fig. 21-6. The region is bounded above by $y = \sqrt{x}$ and below by $y = 1$ . Hence, the area is given by $\int_{1}^{4} (\sqrt{x} - 1) dx = \left( \frac{2}{3} x^{3/2} - x \right) \int_{1}^{4} \left( \frac{16}{3} - 4 \right) - \left( \frac{2}{3} - 1 \right) = \frac{14}{3} - 3 = \frac{5}{3}$ .
