43.39 Find an equation of the plane through $(1,1,2)$ that cuts off the least volume in the first octant.

Let $a, b, c$ be the intercepts of a plane through $(1, 1, 2)$ . Then an equation of the plane is $\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} + \frac{z}{c} = 1$ . The volume $V = \frac{1}{6} abc$ . We can think of $c$ as a function of $a$ and $b$ , and, therefore, of $V$ as a function of $a$ and $b$ . Thus, $V_{a} = \frac{1}{6} b\left(a\frac{\partial c}{\partial a} + c\right)$ , $V_{b} = \frac{1}{6} a\left(b\frac{\partial c}{\partial b} + c\right)$ . Since the plane contains $(1, 1, 2)$ , $\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} + \frac{2}{c} = 1$ . Hence, by differentiation with respect to $a$ , $-\frac{1}{a^2} - \frac{2}{c^2}\frac{\partial c}{\partial a} = 0$ , and, by differentiation with respect to $b$ , $-\frac{1}{b^2} - \frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial c}{\partial b} = 0$ . Therefore, $\frac{\partial c}{\partial a} = -c^2 / 2a^2$ and $\frac{\partial c}{\partial b} = -c^2 / 2b^2$ . Set $V_{a} = 0$ and $V_{b} = 0$ . Then $a\frac{\partial c}{\partial a} + c = 0$ , $b\frac{\partial c}{\partial b} + c = 0$ , or $a[-(c^2 / 2a^2)] + c = 0$ and $b[-(c^2 / 2b^2)] + c = 0$ , that is, $c = c^2 / 2a$ and $c = c^2 / 2b$ . Therefore, $2a = 2b$ , $b = a$ . From $c = c^2 / 2a$ , $c = 2a$ . Substitute in $\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} + \frac{2}{c} = 1$ . Then, $\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{a} = 1$ , and so, $a = 3$ . Hence, $b = 3$ , $c = 6$ . Thus the desired equation is $\frac{x}{3} + \frac{y}{3} + \frac{z}{6} = 1$ , or $2x + 2y + z = 6$ .
