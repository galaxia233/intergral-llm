26.7 If a population is increasing exponentially at the rate of 2 percent per year, what will be the percentage increase over a period of 10 years?

In the notation of Problem 26.6, $r = 2$ , $K = \ln (1.02) \approx 0.0198$ (by a table of logarithms). Hence, after 10 years, $y = y_0 e^{Kt} = y_0 e^{(0.0198)10} = y_0 e^{0.198} \approx (1.219) y_0$ (using a table for the exponential function). Hence, over 10 years, there will be an increase of about 21.9 percent.
