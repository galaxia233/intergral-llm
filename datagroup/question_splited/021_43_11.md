43.11 Find the derivative of $z = x \ln y$ at the point $(1, 2)$ in the direction making an angle of $30^\circ$ with the positive $x$ -axis.

The unit vector in the given direction is $\mathbf{u} = (\cos 30^{\circ},\sin 30^{\circ}) = (\sqrt{3} /2,\frac{1}{2})$ $\nabla f = (\ln y,x / y) = (\ln 2,\frac{1}{2}).$ So, the derivative is $\nabla f\cdot \mathbf{u} = (\ln 2,\frac{1}{2})\cdot \frac{1}{2} (\sqrt{3},1) = \frac{1}{2} (\sqrt{3}\ln 2 + \frac{1}{2}).$
