32.7 For positive $p$ , show that $\int_{1}^{\infty} x^{p} e^{-x} dx$ converges.

By Problem 32.6, $\int_{1}^{\infty}xe^{-x}dx = 2e^{-1}$ . For $0 < p < 1$ , $\int_{1}^{v}x^{p}e^{-x}dx\leq \int_{1}^{v}xe^{-x}dx$ . Hence, $\int_{1}^{\infty}x^{p}e^{-x}dx$ converges. Now let us consider $p > 1$ . By the reduction formula of Problem 28.42, $\int x^{p}e^{-x}dx = -x^{p}e^{-x} + p\int x^{p - 1}e^{-x}dx$ . Hence, $\int_{1}^{\infty}x^{p}e^{-x}dx = \lim_{v\to +\infty}\int_{1}^{v}x^{p}e^{-x}dx = \lim_{v\to +\infty}(-x^{p}e^{-x}]_{1}^{v} + p\int_{1}^{v}x^{p - 1}e^{-x}dx = \lim_{v\to +\infty}(-v^{p}e^{-v} + e^{-1} + p\int_{1}^{v}x^{p - 1}e^{-x}dx = e^{-1} + p\int_{1}^{\infty}x^{p - 1}e^{-x}dx$ . (Note that we used L'Hôpital's rule to show that $\lim_{v\to +\infty}v^{p}e^{-v} = 0$ .) Hence, the question eventually reduces to the case of $p\leq 1$ . Thus, we have convergence for all positive $p$ .
