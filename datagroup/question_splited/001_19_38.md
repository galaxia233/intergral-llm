19.38 Find an equation of the curve passing through the point (3, 2) and having slope $2x^{2} - 5$ at any point $(x, y)$ .

1 $D_{x}y = 2x^{2} - 5.$ Hence, $y = \frac{2}{3} x^3 -5x + C$ Since (3, 2) is on the curve, $2 = \frac{2}{3} (3)^{3} - 5(3) + C,\quad C = -1.$ Thus, $y = \frac{2}{3} x^3 -5x - 1$ is an equation of the curve.
