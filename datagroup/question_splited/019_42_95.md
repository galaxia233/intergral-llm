42.95 If $w = f\left(\frac{y - x}{xy}, \frac{z - y}{yz}\right)$ , show that $x^2 \frac{\partial w}{\partial x} + y^2 \frac{\partial w}{\partial y} + z^2 \frac{\partial w}{\partial z} = 0$ .

Denote the partial derivatives of $f$ with respect to its two arguments as $f_{1}$ and $f_{2}$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial w}{\partial x} = f _ {1} \frac {\partial ((y - x) / x y)}{\partial x} + f _ {2} \frac {\partial ((z - y) / y z)}{\partial x} = f _ {1} \cdot \left(- \frac {1}{x ^ {2}}\right) + f _ {2} \cdot 0 = - \frac {1}{x ^ {2}} f _ {1} \\ \frac {\partial w}{\partial y} = f _ {1} \frac {\partial ((y - x) / x y)}{\partial y} + f _ {2} \frac {\partial ((z - y) / y z)}{\partial y} = f _ {1} \cdot \left(\frac {1}{y ^ {2}}\right) - f _ {2} \cdot \left(\frac {1}{y ^ {2}}\right) = \frac {1}{y ^ {2}} \left(f _ {1} - f _ {2}\right) \\ \frac {\partial w}{\partial z} = f _ {1} \frac {\partial ((y - x) / x y)}{\partial z} + f _ {2} \frac {\partial ((z - y) / y z)}{\partial z} = 0 + f _ {2} \cdot \left(\frac {1}{z ^ {2}}\right) = \frac {1}{z ^ {2}} f _ {2} \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus, $x^{2} \frac{\partial w}{\partial x} + y^2 \frac{\partial w}{\partial y} + z^2 \frac{\partial w}{\partial z} = -f_{1} + f_{1} - f_{2} + f_{2} = 0.$
