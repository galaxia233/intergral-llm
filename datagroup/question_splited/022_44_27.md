44.27 Express $I = \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \int_{0}^{\cos x} x^{2} dy dx$ as double integral with the order of integration reversed and compute its value.

The region of integration is bounded by $y = \cos x$ , $y = 0$ , and $x = 0$ (Fig. 44-15). So $I = \int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{\cos^{-1}y} x^{2} dx dy$ . The original form is easier to calculate. $I = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} x^{2} y \, \mathrm{d}x = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} x^{2} \cos x \, \mathrm{d}x$ . Two integrations by parts yields $\int x^{2} \cos x \, \mathrm{d}x = x^{2} \sin x + 2x \cos x - 2 \sin x$ . Hence, $I = (x^{2} \sin x + 2x \cos x - 2 \sin x) \int_{0}^{\pi/2} = \pi^{2}/4 - 2$ .
