29.23 $\int \frac{dx}{x^2\sqrt{x^2 - 9}}.$

Since $\sqrt{x^2 - 9}$ is present, let $x = 3\sec \theta$ , $dx = 3\sec \theta \tan \theta d\theta$ , and (Fig. 29-4) $\sqrt{x^2 - 9} = 3\tan \theta$ . Then $\int \frac{dx}{x^2\sqrt{x^2 - 9}} = \int \frac{3\sec\theta\tan\theta d\theta}{9\sec^2\theta\cdot 3\tan\theta} = \frac{1}{9}\int \cos \theta d\theta = \frac{1}{9}\sin \theta +C = \frac{1}{9}\frac{\sqrt{x^2 - 9}}{x} +C.$
