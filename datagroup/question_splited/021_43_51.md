43.51 Prove that the geometric mean of three nonnegative numbers is not greater than their arithmetic mean; that is, $\sqrt[3]{abc} \leq (a + b + c) / 3$ for $a, b, c \geq 0$ .

This is clear when $a = 0$ or $b = 0$ or $c = 0$ . So, we may assume $a, b, c > 0$ . Let $d = a + b + c$ , and let $x = 18a / d$ , $y = 18b / d$ , $z = 18c / d$ . Then $x, y$ , and $z$ are positive and $x + y + z = 18$ . By Problem 43.32, $xyz \leq 6^3$ . So, $\frac{a}{d} \frac{b}{d} \frac{c}{d} \leq \frac{6^3}{18^3} = \frac{1}{3^3}$ , $abc \leq (d / 3)^3$ , $\sqrt[3]{abc} \leq d / 3 = (a + b + c) / 3$ . In general, for non-negative $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ , we have $\sqrt[n]{a_1 \cdots a_n} \leq (a_1 + \cdots + a_n) / n$ , with equality if and only if $a_1 = \cdots = a_n$ .
