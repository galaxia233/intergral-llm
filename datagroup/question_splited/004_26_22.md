26.22 If $y$ represents the amount by which the temperature of a body exceeds that of the surrounding air, then the rate at which $y$ decreases is proportional to $y$ (Newton's law of cooling). If $y$ was initially 8 degrees and was 7 degrees after 1 minute, what will it be after 2 minutes?

Since $D_{t}y = Ky$ , $y = y_{0}e^{Kt} = 8e^{Kt}$ . The given facts tell us that $7 = 8e^{K}, \quad e^{K} = \frac{7}{8}$ . When $t = 2, \quad y = 8e^{2K} = 8(e^{K})^{2} = 8\left(\frac{7}{8}\right)^{2} = 6.125$ degrees.
