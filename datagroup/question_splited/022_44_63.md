44.62 Use a triple integral to find the volume cut from the cone $\phi = \pi / 4$ by the sphere $\rho = 2a\cos \phi$ .

![](images/2224208a763e1425437a2f0860d238f5f507ab79306240cff8c887d17640d344.jpg)  
Fig. 44-34

Refer to Fig. 44-34. Note that $\rho = 2a\cos \phi$ has the equivalent forms $\rho^2 = 2a\rho\cos \phi$ , $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 2az$ , $x^2 + y^2 + (z - a)^2 = a^2$ . Thus, it is the sphere with center at $(0,0,a)$ and radius $a$ . Then, $V = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi /4}\int_{0}^{2a\cos \phi}\rho^2\sin \phi d\rho d\phi d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi /4}\frac{1}{3}\rho^3\sin \phi \Bigg]_0^{2a\cos \phi}d\phi d\theta = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi /4}\frac{8a^3}{3}\sin \phi \cos^3\phi d\phi d\theta = \frac{8a^3}{3}\int_{0}^{2\pi}\left(-\frac{\cos^4\phi}{4}\right)\Bigg]_0^{\pi /4}d\theta = -\frac{2a^3}{3}\int_{0}^{2\pi}\left(\frac{1}{4} -1\right)d\theta = \frac{1}{2} a^3\int_{0}^{2\pi}d\theta = \frac{1}{2} a^3\cdot 2\pi = \pi a^3.$
