31.2 Find the surface area of a sphere of radius $r$ .

Revolve the upper semicircle $y = \sqrt{r^2 - x^2}$ about the $x$ -axis. Since $x^2 + y^2 = r^2$ , $2x + 2yy' = 0$ , $y' = -x / y$ , $(y')^2 = x^2 / y^2$ , $1 + (y')^2 = 1 + x^2 / y^2 = (y^2 + x^2) / y^2 = r^2 / y^2$ . Hence, the surface area $S = 2\pi \int_{-r}^{r} y \cdot \frac{r}{y} dx = 2\pi \int_{-r}^{r} r \, dx = 2\pi rx \big]_{-r}^{r} = 4\pi r^2$ .
