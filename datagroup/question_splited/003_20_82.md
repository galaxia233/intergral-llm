20.82 Find $\int_0^{\pi /4}\frac{dx}{1 + \cot^2x}.$

$\frac{1}{1 + \cot^2 x} = \frac{1}{\csc^2 x} = \sin^2 x = \frac{1}{2} (1 - \cos 2x).$ Hence, $\int_0^{\pi /4}\frac{dx}{1 + \cot^2x} = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^{\pi /4}(1 - \cos 2x)dx = \frac{1}{2} (x - \frac{1}{2})\pi /4.$ $\left[\frac{1}{2}\sin 2x\right]_0^{\pi /4} = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{4} -\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{8} (\pi -2).$
