27.64 Use integration to show that the circumference of a circle of radius $r$ is $2\pi r$ .

Find the arc length of the part of the circle $x^{2} + y^{2} = r^{2}$ in the first quadrant and multiply it by 4. Since $y = \sqrt{r^{2} - x^{2}}$ , $y' = -x / \sqrt{r^{2} - x^{2}}$ and $(y')^{2} = x^{2} / (r^{2} - x^{2})$ . So $1 + (y')^{2} = 1 + x^{2} / (r^{2} - x^{2}) = (r^{2} - x^{2} + x^{2}) / (r^{2} - x^{2}) = r^{2} / (r^{2} - x^{2})$ . Thus, $L = 4\int_{0}^{r}\sqrt{1 + (y')^{2}} dx = 4\int_{0}^{r}\frac{r}{\sqrt{r^{2} - x^{2}}} dx = 4r\sin^{-1}\frac{x}{r}\int_{0}^{r} = 4r(\sin^{-1}1 - \sin^{-1}0) = 4r(\pi / 2 - 0) = 2\pi r$ .
