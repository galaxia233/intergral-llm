28.39 Find a reduction formula for $\int \sec^n x dx$ for $n\geq 2$

Let $u = \sec^{n - 2}x$ , $dv = \sec^2 x dx$ , $du = (n - 2)\sec^{n - 3}x$ sec $x$ tan $x dx$ , $v = \tan x$ . Then $\int \sec^n x dx = \tan x\sec^{n - 2}x - (n - 2)\int \sec^{n - 2}x\tan^2 x dx = \tan x\sec^{n - 2}x - (n - 2)\int \sec^{n - 2}x(\sec^2 x - 1)dx = \tan x\sec^{n - 2}x - (n - 2)\int \sec^n x dx + (n - 2)\int \sec^{n - 2}x dx$ . Solving for $\int \sec^n x dx$ ,

$$
\int \sec^ {n} x d x = \frac {\tan x \sec^ {n - 2} x}{n - 1} + \frac {n - 2}{n - 1} \int \sec^ {n - 2} x d x.
$$
