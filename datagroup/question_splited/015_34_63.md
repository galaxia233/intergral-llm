34.105 For a given curve $\mathbf{R}(t)$ , show that $\mathbf{T}'(t) = (v / \rho)\mathbf{N}(t)$ .

By definition of $\mathbf{N}(t)$ , $\mathbf{T}'(t) = |\mathbf{T}'(t)|\mathbf{N}(t)$ . We must show that $|\mathbf{T}'(t)| = v / \rho$ . By the chain rule, $\mathbf{T}'(t) = \frac{d\mathbf{T}}{d\phi}\frac{d\phi}{dt}$ . By Problem 34.89, $|d\mathbf{T} / d\phi| = 1$ . Hence, $|\mathbf{T}'(t)| = |d\phi / dt|$ . But, $\frac{d\phi}{dt} = \frac{d\phi}{ds}\frac{ds}{dt}$ , and, therefore, $\left|\frac{d\phi}{dt}\right| = \left|\frac{d\phi}{ds}\right|\left|\frac{ds}{dt}\right|$ . Since $\kappa = d\phi / ds$ , $|d\phi / ds| = 1 / \rho$ . Thus, $|\mathbf{T}'(t)| = v / \rho$ .
