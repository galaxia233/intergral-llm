42.62 Let $f(x, y) = \int_{0}^{y} (x^2 + tx) \, dt$ . Find $f_x$ and $f_y$ and verify that $f_{xy} = f_{yx}$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} f _ {x} = \int_ {0} ^ {y} \frac {\partial}{\partial x} (x ^ {2} + t x) d t = \int_ {0} ^ {y} (2 x + t) d t = (2 x t + \frac {1}{2} t ^ {2}) ] _ {0} ^ {y} = 2 x y + \frac {1}{2} y ^ {2}, \\ f _ {y x} = 2 x + y. \end{array} \qquad \begin{array}{l} f _ {y} = x ^ {2} + y x, \\ f _ {x y} = 2 x + y, \end{array}
$$
