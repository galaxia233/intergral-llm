39.42 Find the Maclaurin series for $f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2 + x + 1}$ .

$f(x) = \frac{1 - x}{1 - x^3}$ . From $\frac{1}{1 - x} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n$ , we obtain $\frac{1}{1 - x^3} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^{3n}$ , and so $f(x) = \frac{1}{1 - x^3} - x \frac{1}{1 - x^3} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^{3n} - \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^{3n+1}$ .
