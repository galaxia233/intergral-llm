42.69 If $w = x^{2} + 3xy - 2y^{2}$ , and $x = r\cos \theta$ , $y = r\sin \theta$ , find $\partial w / \partial r$ and $\partial w / \partial \theta$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial w}{\partial r} = \frac {\partial w}{\partial x} \frac {\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac {\partial w}{\partial y} \frac {\partial y}{\partial r} = (2 x + 3 y) \cos \theta + (3 x - 4 y) \sin \theta \\ = (2 r \cos \theta + 3 r \sin \theta) \cos \theta + (3 r \cos \theta - 4 r \sin \theta) \sin \theta = r (2 \cos^ {2} \theta + 6 \sin \theta \cos \theta - 4 \sin^ {2} \theta) \\ \end{array}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial w}{\partial \theta} = \frac {\partial w}{\partial x} \frac {\partial x}{\partial \theta} + \frac {\partial w}{\partial y} \frac {\partial y}{\partial \theta} = (2 x + 3 y) (- r \sin \theta) + (3 x - 4 y) (r \cos \theta) \\ = (2 r \cos \theta + 3 r \sin \theta) (- r \sin \theta) + (3 r \cos \theta - 4 r \sin \theta) (r \cos \theta) \\ = r ^ {2} (- 2 \cos \theta \sin \theta - 3 \sin^ {2} \theta + 3 \cos^ {2} \theta - 4 \sin \theta \cos \theta) = r ^ {2} (3 \cos^ {2} \theta - 6 \cos \theta \sin \theta - 3 \sin^ {2} \theta) \\ = 3 r ^ {2} \left(\cos^ {2} \theta - 2 \cos \theta \sin \theta - \sin^ {2} \theta\right) = 3 r ^ {2} (\cos 2 \theta - \sin 2 \theta) \\ \end{array}
$$
