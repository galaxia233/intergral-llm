35.57 Find the area inside one loop of the lemniscate $r^2 = \cos 2\theta$

From Problem 35.32, we see that the area of one loop is double the area in the first quadrant. The latter area is swept out from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = \pi /4$ . Hence, the required area is $2\cdot \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi /4}\cos 2\theta d\theta = \frac{1}{2}\sin 2\theta \big]_{0}^{\pi /4} = \frac{1}{2} (1 - 0) = \frac{1}{2}$ .
