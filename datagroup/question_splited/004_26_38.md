26.38 The world population at the beginning of 1970 was 3.6 billion. The weight of the earth is $6.586 \times 10^{21}$ tons. If the population continues to increase exponentially, with a growth constant $K = 0.02$ and with time measured in years, in what year will the weight of all people equal the weight of the earth, if we assume that the average person weighs 120 pounds?

The earth weighs $6.586 \times 10^{21} \times 2000$ pounds. When this is equal to the weight of $y$ billion people, $120 \times 10^{9} y = 6.586 \times 10^{21} \times 2000$ , or $y \approx 1.1 \times 10^{14}$ . Thus we must solve $1.1 \times 10^{14} = 3.6 e^{0.02t}$ for $t$ . Taking logarithms, $\ln 1.1 + 14 \ln 10 = \ln 3.6 + 0.02t$ , $0.02t \approx 31.05$ , $t \approx 1552.5$ years. The date would be $1970 + 1552 = 3522$ .
