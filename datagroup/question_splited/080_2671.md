【2671】 $\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty}\sin (\pi \sqrt{n^2 + k^2}).$

解 $\sin (\pi \sqrt{n^2 + k^2}) = \sin \left[n\pi \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{k}{n}\right)^2}\right] = \sin n\pi \left[1 + \frac{k^2}{2n^2} + O\left(\frac{1}{n^4}\right)\right] = \sin \left[n\pi + \frac{k^2\pi}{2n} + O\left(\frac{1}{n^3}\right)\right]$

$$
= (- 1) ^ {n} \sin \left[ \frac {k ^ {2} \pi}{2 n} + O \left(\frac {1}{n ^ {3}}\right) \right] = (- 1) ^ {n} \frac {k ^ {2} \pi}{2 n} + O \left(\frac {1}{n ^ {3}}\right),
$$

由于级数 $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n}\frac{k^{2}\pi}{2n}$ 条件收敛， $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^{3}}$ 收敛，故原级数收敛.
