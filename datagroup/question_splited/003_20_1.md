20.1 Evaluate $\int_{2}^{5} 4 \, dx$ by the direct (Riemann) definition of the integral.

Let $2 = x_{0} < x_{1} < \dots < x_{n - 1} < x_{n} = 5$ be any partition of [2, 5], and let $\Delta_{i}x = x_{i} - x_{i - 1}$ . Then an approximating sum for $\int_{2}^{5}4dx$ is

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} f \left(x _ {i} ^ {*}\right) \Delta_ {i} x = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} 4 \Delta_ {i} x = 4 \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} \Delta_ {i} x = 4 \left[ \left(x _ {1} - x _ {0}\right) + \left(x _ {2} - x _ {1}\right) + \dots + \left(x _ {n} - x _ {n - 1}\right) \right] \\ = 4 \left(x _ {n} - x _ {0}\right) = 4 (5 - 2) = 4 \cdot 3 = 1 2. \\ \end{array}
$$

Hence, the integral, which is approximated arbitrarily closely by the approximating sums, must be 12.
