19.86 If $y'' = 24 / x^3$ at all points of a curve, and, at the point $(1,0)$ , the tangent line is $12x + y = 12$ , find the equation of the curve.

$y^{\prime \prime} = 24x^{-3}$ $y^\prime = \int y''dx = -12x^{-2} + C.$ Since the slope of $12x + y = 12$ is $-12$ , when $x = 1$ , $y^\prime = -12$ . Thus, $C = 0$ , $y^\prime = -12x^{-2}$ . Then $y = \int y^{\prime}dx = 12x^{-1} + C_{1}$ . Since the curve passes through $(1,0)$ , $C_1 = -12$ . So, the equation of the curve is $y = 12\left(\frac{1}{x} -1\right)$ , or $xy = 12(1 - x)$ .
