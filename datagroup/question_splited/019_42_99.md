42.99 Assume $\partial f / \partial x = x$ for all $(x,y)$ . Show that $f(x,y) = \frac{1}{2} x^{2} + h(y)$ for some function $h(y)$ .

Let $F(x, y) = f(x, y) - \frac{1}{2} x^2$ . Then, $\frac{\partial F}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} - x = 0$ . By Problem 42.98, $F(x, y) = h(y)$ for a suit-able $h$ .
