35.58 Find the area inside one petal of the four-leaved rose $r = \sin 2\theta$

From Problem 35.33, we see that the area of one petal is swept out from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = \pi /2$ . Hence, the area is

$$
\frac {1}{2} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \sin^ {2} 2 \theta d \theta = \frac {1}{2} \int_ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} \frac {1 - \cos 4 \theta}{2} d \theta = \frac {1}{4} \left(\theta - \frac {1}{4} \sin 4 \theta\right) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {\pi / 2} = \frac {1}{4} \left(\frac {\pi}{2}\right) = \frac {\pi}{8}
$$
