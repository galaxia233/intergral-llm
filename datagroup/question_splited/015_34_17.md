34.59 If $|\mathbf{R}(t)|$ is a constant $c > 0$ , show that the tangent vector $\mathbf{R}'(t)$ is perpendicular to the position vector $\mathbf{R}(t)$ .

$\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t) = |\mathbf{R}(t)|^2 = c^2.$ So, $\frac{d}{dt}[\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t)] = 0.$ But, by Problem 34.57, $\frac{d}{dt}[\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t)] = \mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}'(t) + \mathbf{R}'(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}(t) = 2\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}'(t).$ Hence, $\mathbf{R}(t)\cdot \mathbf{R}'(t) = 0.$
