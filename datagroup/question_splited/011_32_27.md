32.27 Evaluate $\int_0^a\frac{dx}{a^2 - x^2}$ where $a > 0$

$\int \frac{dx}{a^2 - x^2} = \frac{1}{2a}\int \frac{dx}{a - x} +\frac{1}{2a}\int \frac{dx}{a + x} = -\frac{1}{2a}\ln |a - x| + \frac{1}{2a}\ln |a + x|$ Thus,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {0} ^ {a} \frac {d x}{a ^ {2} - x ^ {2}} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow a ^ {-}} \int_ {0} ^ {v} \frac {d x}{a ^ {2} - x ^ {2}} = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow a ^ {-}} \left(- \frac {1}{2 a} \ln | a - x | + \frac {1}{2 a} \ln | a + x |\right) \Bigg ] _ {0} ^ {v} \\ = \lim  _ {v \rightarrow a} \left(- \frac {1}{2 a} \ln | a - v | + \frac {1}{2 a} \ln | a + v | + \frac {\ln a}{2 a}\right) = + \infty + 2 a \ln 2 a + \frac {\ln a}{2 a} = + \infty \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus, the integral diverges.
