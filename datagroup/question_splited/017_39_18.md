39.18 Show that $e^x$ is represented by its Maclaurin series.

Let $f(x) = e^{x}$ . Then the Lagrange remainder $R_{n}(x) = \frac{f^{(n)}(c)}{n!} x^{n} = e^{c} \frac{x^{n}}{n!}$ , for some $c$ between $x$ and 0. Thus, $e^{c} \leq e^{|x|}$ and $|R_{n}(x)| \leq e^{|x|} \frac{|x|^{n}}{n!}$ . But $\lim_{n \to +\infty} \frac{|x|^{n}}{n!} = 0$ , by Problem 36.13. Therefore, $\lim_{n \to +\infty} R_{n}(x) = 0$ for all $x$ . So $e^{x}$ is equal to its Maclaurin series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n}}{n!}$ (which was found in Problem 39.1). Note that this was proved in a different manner in Problem 38.39.
