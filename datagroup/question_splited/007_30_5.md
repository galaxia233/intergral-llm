30.5 $\int \frac{x^2 - 4}{x^3 - 3x^2 - x + 3} dx.$

We must factor the denominator. Clearly $x = 1$ is a root. Dividing the denominator by $x - 1$ we obtain $x^2 - 2x - 3 = (x - 3)(x + 1)$ . Hence, the denominator is $(x - 1)(x - 3)(x + 1)$ . $\frac{x^2 - 4}{(x - 1)(x - 3)(x + 1)} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{B}{x - 3} + \frac{C}{x + 1}$ . Then $x^2 - 4 = A(x - 3)(x + 1) + B(x - 1)(x + 1) + C(x - 1)(x - 3)$ . Let $x = 1$ . Then $-3 = -4A$ , $A = \frac{3}{4}$ . Let $x = 3$ . Then $5 = 8B$ , $B = \frac{5}{8}$ . Let $x = -1$ .

Then $-3 = 8C, \quad C = -\frac{3}{8}$ . Thus,

$$
\frac {x ^ {2} - 4}{(x - 1) (x - 3) (x + 1)} = \frac {3}{4} \frac {1}{x - 1} + \frac {5}{8} \frac {1}{x - 3} - \frac {3}{8} \frac {1}{x + 1},
$$

$$
\int \frac {x ^ {2} - 4}{(x - 1) (x - 3) (x + 1)} d x = \frac {3}{4} \ln | x - 1 | + \frac {5}{8} \ln | x - 3 | - \frac {3}{8} \ln | x + 1 | + C _ {1} = \frac {1}{8} \ln \left| \frac {(x - 1) ^ {6} (x - 3) ^ {5}}{(x + 1) ^ {3}} \right| + C _ {1}
$$
