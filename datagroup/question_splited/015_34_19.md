34.61 For any vector function $\mathbf{F}(u)$ and scalar function $h(u)$ , prove a chain rule: $\frac{d}{du}\mathbf{F}(h(u)) = h'(u)\mathbf{F}'(h(u))$ .

Let $\mathbf{F}(u) = (f(u), g(u))$ . Then $\mathbf{F}(h(u)) = (f(h(u)), g(h(u)))$ . Hence, by Problem 34.43 and the regular chain rule, $\frac{d}{du} [\mathbf{F}(h(u)] = \left(\frac{d}{du} f(h(u)), \frac{d}{du} g(h(u))\right) = (f'(h(u))h'(u), g'(h(u))h'(u)) = h'(u)(f'(h(u)), g'(h(u))h'(u))$ .
