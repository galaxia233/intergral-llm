27.80 Show geometrically that $\sin^{-1}(x / \sqrt{x^2 + 1}) = \tan^{-1}x$ for $x\geq 0$

Consider (Fig. 27-11) a right triangle with legs of length 1 and $x$ , and let $\theta$ be the angle opposite the side of length $x$ . Then $\tan \theta = x$ and $\sin \theta = x / \sqrt{x^2 + 1}$ . Hence, $\tan^{-1} x = \theta = \sin^{-1}(x / \sqrt{x^2 + 1})$ .
