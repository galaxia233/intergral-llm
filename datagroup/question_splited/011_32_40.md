32.41 Find the surface area of the volume in Problem 32.40.

$S = 2\pi \int_{1}^{\infty}y\sqrt{1 + (y')^{2}} dx.$ Note that $y = 9 / x,y' = -9 / x^2,$

$$
S = 2 \pi \int_ {1} ^ {\infty} \frac {9}{x} \frac {\sqrt {x ^ {4} + 8 1}}{x ^ {2}} d x = 1 8 \pi \int_ {1} ^ {\infty} \frac {\sqrt {x ^ {4} + 8 1}}{x ^ {3}} d x.
$$

But

$$
\frac {\sqrt {x ^ {4} + 8 1}}{x ^ {3}} > \frac {\sqrt {x ^ {4}}}{x ^ {3}} = \frac {1}{x}
$$

so by Problem 32.9, the integral diverges.
