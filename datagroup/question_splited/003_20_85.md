20.85 Let $y = f(x)$ be a function whose graph consists of straight lines connecting the points $P_{1}(0,3), P_{2}(3, - 3), P_{3}(4,3)$ , and $P_{4}(5,3)$ . Sketch the graph and find $\int_0^5 f(x)dx$ by geometry.

See Fig. 20-6. The point $B$ where $P_{1}P_{2}$ intersects the $x$ -axis is $\left(\frac{3}{2}, 0\right)$ . $\int_{0}^{5} f(x) dx = A_{1} - A_{2} + A_{3}$ . The area $A_{1}$ of $\triangle OBP_{1}$ is $\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{3}{2} \cdot 3 = \frac{9}{4}$ . The area $A_{2}$ of $\triangle BP_{2}C$ is $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot 3 = 3$ . [Note that $C = \left(\frac{7}{2}, 0\right)$ .] The area $A_{3}$ of trapezoid $CP_{3}P_{4}D$ is $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot \left(\frac{3}{2} + 1\right) = \frac{15}{4}$ . Hence, $\int_{0}^{5} f(x) dx = \frac{9}{4} - 3 + \frac{15}{4} = 3$ .
