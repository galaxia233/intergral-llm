41.32 Describe the level curves (contour map) of $f(x, y) = x^2 + y^2$ .

In general, the level curves of a function $f(x,y)$ are the family of curves $f(x,y) = k, \quad z = 0$ . Here, the level curves are the circles $x^{2} + y^{2} = r^{2}$ (write $k = r^2 > 0$ ) of radius $r$ with center at the origin (Fig. 41-16). There is one through every point of the $xy$ -plane except the origin (unless we consider the origin as the level curve $x^{2} + y^{2} = 0$ ).
