45.21 Find the curvature $\kappa$ of the curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (\sin t, \cos t, \frac{1}{2} t^2)$ at $t = 0$ .

$\kappa = \left|\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{ds}\right|$ . First, $\mathbf{R}' = (\cos t, -\sin t, t), \frac{ds}{dt} = |\mathbf{R}'(t)| = \sqrt{1 + t^2}$ , and $\mathbf{T} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + t^2}}$ ( $\cos t, -\sin t, t$ ). Hence, $\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{dt} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + t^2}}$ (- $\sin t, -\cos t, 1$ ) - $\frac{t}{(1 + t^2)^{3/2}}$ ( $\cos t, -\sin t, t$ ). At $t = 0$ , $\frac{ds}{dt} = 1$ and $\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{dt} = (0, -1, 1)$ ; therefore, $\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{ds} = \frac{d\mathbf{T}/dt}{ds/dt} = (0, -1, 1)$ and $\kappa = \left|\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{ds}\right| = \sqrt{2}$ .
