43.47 Find the shortest distance from the point $(-1,2,4)$ to the plane $3x - 4y + 2z + 32 = 0$ .

It suffices to minimize the square of the distance between $(-1, 2, 4)$ and an arbitrary point $(x, y, z)$ in the plane: $f(x, y, z) = (x + 1)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 4)^2$ . Think of $z$ as a function of $x$ and $y$ . $f_x = 2(x + 1) + 2(z - 4)(\partial z / \partial x)$ , $f_y = 2(y - 2) + 2(z - 4)(\partial z / \partial y)$ . From the equation of the plane, by differentiation with respect to $x$ and $y$ , respectively, we get $3 + 2(\partial z / \partial x) = 0$ and $-4 + 2(\partial z / \partial y) = 0$ . Hence, $\partial z / \partial x = -\frac{3}{2}$ and $\partial z / \partial y = 2$ . Set $f_x = f_y = 0$ , whence

$$
x + 1 + (z - 4) \left(- \frac {3}{2}\right) = 0 \tag {1}
$$

$$
y - 2 + (z - 4) (2) = 0 \tag {2}
$$

Therefore, $\frac{2}{3}(x + 1) = (2 - y)/2$ , which yields $4x + 4 = 6 - 3y$ , $x = -\frac{3}{4}y + \frac{1}{2}$ . From (2), $z = -\frac{1}{2}y + 5$ . Substituting in the equation of the plane, we have $3(-\frac{3}{4}y + \frac{1}{2}) - 4y + (-y + 10) + 32 = 0$ , $-\frac{29}{4}y + \frac{23}{2} + 32 = 0$ , $y = 6$ . Then $x = -\frac{3}{4}(6) + \frac{1}{2} = -4$ , and $z = -\frac{1}{2}(6) + 5 = 2$ . So, the closest point in the plane is $(-4, 6, 2)$ . Hence, the shortest distance from $(-1, 2, 4)$ to the plane is $\sqrt{(-3)^2 + 4^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{29}$ . (This can be checked against the formula obtained in Problem 40.88.)
