21.25 Find the area bounded by the curves $y = 3x^{2} - 2x$ and $y = 1 - 4x$ .

See Fig. 21-20. $y = 3x^{2} - 2x = 3(x^{2} - \frac{2}{3} x) = 3[(x - \frac{1}{3})^{2} - \frac{1}{9}] = 3(x - \frac{1}{3})^{2} - \frac{1}{3}$ . Thus, that curve is a parabola with vertex $\left(\frac{1}{3}, -\frac{1}{3}\right)$ . To find the intersection, let $3x^{2} - 2x = 1 - 4x$ , $3x^{2} + 2x - 1 = 0$ , $(3x - 1)(x + 1) = 0$ , $x = \frac{1}{3}$ or $x = -1$ . Hence, the intersection points are $\left(\frac{1}{3}, -\frac{1}{3}\right)$ and $(-1,5)$ . Thus, the area is $\int_{-1}^{1/3} [(1 - 4x) - (3x^{2} - 2x)] dx = \int_{-1}^{1/3} (1 - 2x - 3x^{2}) dx = (x - x^{2} - x^{3})]_{-1}^{1/3} = \left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{27}\right) - (-1 - 1 + 1) = \frac{32}{27}$ .
