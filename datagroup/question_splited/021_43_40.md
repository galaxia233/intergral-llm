43.40 Determine the values of $p$ and $q$ so that the sum $S$ of the squares of the vertical distances of the points $(0, 2), (1, 3),$ and $(2, 5)$ from the line $y = px + q$ shall be a minimum [method of least squares].

We must minimize $S = (q - 2)^{2} + (p + q - 3)^{2} + (2p + q - 5)^{2}$ . $S_{q} = 2(q - 2) + 2(p + q - 3) + 2(2p + q - 5) = 6q + 6p - 20$ . $S_{p} = 2(p + q - 3) + 2(2p + q - 5)(2) = 10p + 6q - 26$ . Let $S_{q} = 0$ , $S_{p} = 0$ . Then $3q + 3p - 10 = 0$ , $5p + 3q - 13 = 0$ . So, $2p - 3 = 0$ , $p = \frac{3}{2}$ , $q = \frac{11}{6}$ .
