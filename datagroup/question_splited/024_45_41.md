45.41 Evaluate the line integral $\int_{\mathcal{C}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{T} ds$ for $\mathbf{F} = (y, 0, zy)$ , where $\mathcal{C}$ is the helix ( $\cos t, \sin t, 3t$ ), $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$ . (Here, $\mathbf{T}$ denotes the unit tangent vector along the curve.)

If $s$ denotes the arc length, $\mathbf{T} = \left(\frac{dx}{ds},\frac{dy}{ds},\frac{dz}{ds}\right)$ , then $\mathbf{F}\cdot \mathbf{T} = (y,0,zy)\cdot \left(\frac{dx}{ds},\frac{dy}{ds},\frac{dz}{ds}\right) = y\frac{dx}{ds} +zy\frac{dz}{ds}$ . Hence, $\int_{\mathcal{C}}\mathbf{F}\cdot \mathbf{T}ds = \int_{\mathcal{C}}\left(y\frac{dx}{ds} +zy\frac{dz}{ds}\right)ds = \int_{\mathcal{C}}y dx + zy dz = \int_0^{2\pi}\left(y\frac{dx}{dt} +zy\frac{dz}{dt}\right)dt = \int_0^{2\pi}[(\sin t)(-\sin t) + (3t\sin t)(3)]dt = \int_0^{2\pi}(9t\sin t - \sin^2 t)dt = \left[9(\sin t - t\cos t) - \frac{1}{2}\left(t - \frac{\sin 2t}{2}\right)\right]\Bigg]_0^{2\pi} = 9(-2\pi) - \frac{1}{2} (2\pi) = -19\pi .$
