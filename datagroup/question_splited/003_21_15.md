21.15 The region bounded by the parabolas $y = x^{2} - x$ and $y = x - x^{2}$ .

See Fig. 21-15. Let us find the points of intersection: $x^{2} - x = x - x^{2}, \quad 2x^{2} - 2x = 0, \quad x(x - 1) = 0, \quad x = 0$ or $x = 1$ . Thus, the intersection points are $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 0)$ . The parabola $y = x^{2} - x = (x - \frac{1}{2})^{2} - \frac{1}{4}$ has its vertex at $\left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{4}\right)$ and opens upward, while $y = x - x^{2}$ has its vertex at $\left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{4}\right)$ and opens downward. The latter parabola is the upper boundary, and the first parabola is the lower boundary. Hence, the area is $\int_{0}^{1} \left[(x - x^{2}) - (x^{2} - x)\right] dx = 2\int_{0}^{1}(x - x^{2}) dx = 2\left(\frac{1}{2}x^{2} - \frac{1}{3}x^{3}\right)\bigg]_{0}^{1} = 2\left(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3}\right) = \frac{1}{3}$ .
