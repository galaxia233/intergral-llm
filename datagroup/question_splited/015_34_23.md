34.65 At $t = -1$ , $\mathbf{G}(t) = (3,0)$ and $\mathbf{G}'(t) = (2,3)$ . Find $\frac{d}{dt} [t^3\mathbf{G}(t)]$ at $t = -1$ .

By Problem 34.54, $\frac{d}{dt} [t^3\mathbf{G}(t)] = t^3\mathbf{G}'(t) + 3t^2\mathbf{G}(t)$ . At $t = -1$ , $\frac{d}{dt} [t^3\mathbf{G}(t)] = -(2,3) + 3(3,0) = (7, -3)$ .
