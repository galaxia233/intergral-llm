44.49 Evaluate $I = \int_0^\infty x^2 e^{-x^2}dx$

Consider $I_w = \int_0^w x^2 e^{-x^2} dx$ . Use integration by parts. Let $u = x$ , $du = xe^{-x^2} dx$ , $du = dx$ , $v = -\frac{1}{2} e^{-x^2}$ . Then $I_w = -\frac{1}{2} xe^{-x^2} \bigg]_0^w + \frac{1}{2} \int_0^w e^{-x^2} dx$ . Hence, $I = \lim_{w \to +\infty} I_w = \lim_{w \to +\infty} (-\frac{1}{2} we^{-w^2}) + \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\infty e^{-x^2} dx = 0 + \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\pi}/2 = \sqrt{\pi}/4$ , by Problem 44.47.
