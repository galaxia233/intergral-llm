40.18 Describe the intersection of the graphs of $x^{2} + y^{2} = 1$ and $z = 2$ .

As shown in Fig. 40-1, $x^{2} + y^{2} = 1$ is a cylinder of radius 1 with the $z$ -axis as its axis of symmetry. $z = 2$ is a plane two units above and parallel to the $xy$ -plane. Hence, the intersection is a circle of radius 1 with center at $(0,0,2)$ in the plane $z = 2$ .
