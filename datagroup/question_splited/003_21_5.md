21.5 The bounded region between the parabola $y = 4x^{2}$ and the line $y = 6x - 2$ .

See Fig. 21-5. First we find the points of intersection: $4x^{2} = 6x - 2$ , $2x^{2} - 3x + 1 = 0$ , $(2x - 1)(x - 1) = 0$ , $x = \frac{1}{2}$ or $x = 1$ . So, the points of intersection are $\left(\frac{1}{2}, 1\right)$ and $(1, 4)$ . Hence, the area is $\int_{1/2}^{1} [(6x - 2) - 4x^2] dx = (3x^2 - 2x - \frac{4}{3} x^3)]_{1/2}^{1} = (3 - 2 - \frac{4}{3}) - (\frac{3}{4} - 1 - \frac{1}{6}) = \frac{1}{12}$ .
