42.96 Prove Leibniz's formula: For differentiable functions $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ ,

$$
\frac {d}{d x} \int_ {u} ^ {v} f (x, y) d y = - f (x, u) \frac {d u}{d x} + f (x, v) \frac {d v}{d x} + \int_ {u} ^ {v} \frac {\partial f}{\partial x} d y.
$$

Let $w = \int_{u}^{v} f(x, y) \, dy$ . By the chain rule, $\frac{dw}{dx} = \frac{\partial w}{\partial u} \frac{du}{dx} + \frac{\partial w}{\partial v} \frac{dv}{dx} + \frac{\partial w}{\partial x}$ . Now, $\frac{\partial w}{\partial u} = -f(x, u)$ and $\frac{\partial w}{\partial v} = f(x, v)$ , and (Problem 42.60) $\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} = \int_{u}^{v} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \, dy$ , yielding Leibniz's formula.
