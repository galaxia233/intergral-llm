19.73 A block of ice slides down a 60-meter chute with an acceleration of $4\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{s}^2$ . What was the initial velocity of the block if it reaches the bottom in 5 s?

$v = \int a dt = \int 4 dt = 4t + v_0,$ where $v_{0}$ is the initial velocity. Then the position $s = \int vdt = 2t^{\epsilon} + v_{0}t.$ [We let $s = 0$ at the top of the chute.] Then $60 = 2(25) + 5v_{0},\quad v_{0} = 2\mathrm{m / s}.$
