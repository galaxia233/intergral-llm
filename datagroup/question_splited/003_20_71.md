20.71 Use geometric reasoning to calculate $\int_{-a}^{a}\sqrt{a^2 - x^2} dx$ .

The graph of $y = \sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$ is the upper half of the circle $x^2 + y^2 = a^2$ with center at the origin and radius $a$ . $\int_{-a}^{a} \sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$ is, therefore, the area of the semicircle, that is, $\pi a^2 / 2$ .
