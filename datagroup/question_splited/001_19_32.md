19.32 Find $\int \sqrt{1 - x} x^2 dx$ .

Let $u = 1 - x$ , $du = -dx$ . Note that $x = 1 - u$ . Then $\int \sqrt{1 - x} x^2 dx = \int \sqrt{u}(1 - u)^2 \cdot (-1) du = -\int \sqrt{u}(1 - 2u + u^2) du = -\int (u^{1/2} - 2u^{3/2} + u^{5/2}) du = -\left[\frac{2}{3} u^{3/2} - 2\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) u^{5/2} + \frac{2}{7} u^{7/2}\right] + C = -2u^{3/2}\left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{2}{3} u + \frac{1}{7} u^2\right) + C = -2\left(\sqrt{1 - x}\right)^3\left[\frac{1}{3} - \frac{2}{5}(1 - x) + \frac{1}{7}(1 - x)^2\right] + C = -\frac{2}{105}\left(\sqrt{1 - x}\right)^3(8 + 12x + 15x^2) + C.$
