34.77 Let $\mathbf{R}(t) = (t, 1 / t)$ . Find $\mathbf{v}(t)$ , the speed $|\mathbf{v}(t)|$ , and $\mathbf{a}(t)$ . Describe what happens as $t \to +\infty$ .

$\mathbf{v}(t) = (1, -1 / t^2)$ , $|\mathbf{v}(t)| = \sqrt{1 + (1 / t^4)}$ , and $\mathbf{a}(t) = (0, 2 / t^3)$ . As $t \to +\infty$ , the direction of $\mathbf{R}(t)$ approaches that of the positive $x$ -axis and its length approaches $+\infty$ . The velocity vector approaches the unit vector $\mathbf{i} = (1, 0)$ , and the speed approaches 1. The acceleration vector always points along the positive $y$ -axis, and its length approaches 0.
