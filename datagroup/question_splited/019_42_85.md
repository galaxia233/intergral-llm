42.85 If $z = f(x,y) = xy^{2} + x^{2}y$ and $y = \ln x$ , find $dz / dx$ and $dz / dy$ .

First, think of $z$ as a composite function of $x$ . By the chain rule, $\frac{dz}{dx} = f_x + f_y \left( \frac{dy}{dx} \right) = y^2 + 2xy + (2xy + x^2)(1/x) = y^2 + 2xy + 2y + x = (\ln x)^2 + 2(x + 1)\ln x + x$ . Next, think of $z$ as a composite function of $y$ (by virtue of $x = e^y$ ). Then, $\frac{dz}{dy} = f_x \frac{dx}{dy} + f_y = (y^2 + 2xy)x + (2xy + x^2) = x(y^2 + 2xy + 2y + x) = e^x (y^2 + 2ye^y + 2y + e^y)$ .
