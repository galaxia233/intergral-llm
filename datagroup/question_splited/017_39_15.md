39.15 Obtain the Maclaurin series for $\cos^2 x$

$\cos^2 x = \frac{1 + \cos 2x}{2}$ . Now, by Problem 38.59, $\cos x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$ , and, therefore, $\cos 2x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n 2^{2n} x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$ . Since the latter series has constant term $1$ , $1 + \cos 2x = 2 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n 2^{2n} x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$ , and $\frac{1 + \cos 2x}{2} = 1 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n 2^{2n-1} x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$ . By Problem 39.11, this is the Maclaurin series for $\cos^2 x$ .
