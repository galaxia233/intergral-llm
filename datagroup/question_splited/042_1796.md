【1796】 $\int x^{2}\mathrm{e}^{-2x}\mathrm{d}x$

解 $\int x^{2} \mathrm{e}^{-2x} \mathrm{d}x = -\frac{1}{2} \int x^{2} \mathrm{~d}\left(\mathrm{e}^{-2x}\right) = -\frac{1}{2} x^{2} \mathrm{e}^{-2x} + \frac{1}{2} \int \mathrm{e}^{-2x} 2x \mathrm{~d}x = -\frac{1}{2} x^{2} \mathrm{e}^{-2x} - \frac{1}{2} \int x \mathrm{~d}\left(\mathrm{e}^{-2x}\right)$

$$
= - \frac {1}{2} x ^ {2} e ^ {- 2 x} - \frac {1}{2} x e ^ {- 2 x} + \frac {1}{2} \int e ^ {- 2 x} d x = - \frac {1}{2} e ^ {- 2 x} \left(x ^ {2} + x + \frac {1}{2}\right) + C.
$$
