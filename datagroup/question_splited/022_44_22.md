44.22 Find the volume $V$ of the solid bounded above by the plane $z = 3x + y + 6$ , below by the $xy$ -plane, and on the sides by $y = 0$ and $y = 4 - x^2$ .

Since $-2 \leq x \leq 2$ and $y \geq 0$ , we have $z = 3x + y + 6 \geq 0$ . Then $V = \int_{-2}^{2} \int_{0}^{4 - x^2} (3x + y + 6) \, dy \, dx = \int_{-2}^{2} (3xy + \frac{1}{2}y^2 + 6y)\int_{0}^{4 - x^2} dx = \int_{-2}^{2} [3x(4 - x^2) + \frac{1}{2}(4 - x^2)^2 + 6(4 - x^2)] dx = \int_{-2}^{2} (32 + 12x - 10x^2 - 3x^3 + \frac{1}{2}x^4) dx = (32x + 6x^2 - \frac{10}{3}x^3 - \frac{3}{4}x^4 + \frac{1}{10}x^5)\int_{-2}^{2} = (64 + 24 - \frac{80}{3} - 12 + \frac{32}{10}) - (-64 + 24 + \frac{80}{3} - 12 - \frac{32}{10}) = \frac{1216}{15}$ .
