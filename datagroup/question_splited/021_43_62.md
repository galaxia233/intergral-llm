43.62 If a rectangular box has three faces in the coordinate planes and one vertex in the first octant on the paraboloid $z = 4 - x^2 - y^2$ , find the maximum volume $V$ of such a box.

We must maximize $V = xyz$ subject to the constraint $g(x, y, z) = x^2 + y^2 + z - 4 = 0$ . $\nabla V = (yz, xz, xy), \nabla g = (2x, 2y, 1)$ . Let $\nabla V = \lambda \nabla g$ , that is, $(yz, xz, xy) = \lambda (2x, 2y, 1)$ . This is equivalent to $yz = 2\lambda x$ , $xz = 2\lambda y$ , $xy = \lambda$ . Since $\lambda = xy$ , we have $yz = 2(xy)x$ , $xz = 2(xy)y$ , or $z = 2x^2$ , $z = 2y^2$ , $x^2 = y^2$ . Substitute in the constraint equation: $x^2 + x^2 + 2x^2 = 4$ , $4x^2 = 4$ , $x^2 = 1$ , $x = 1$ , $y = 1$ , $z = 2$ . Hence, the maximum volume $V = xyz = 1(1)(2) = 2$ .
