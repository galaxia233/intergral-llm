41.67 Give the equations connecting rectangular and cylindrical coordinates of a point in space.

For a point with rectangular coordinates $(x, y, z)$ , corresponding cylindrical coordinates are $(r, \theta, z)$ , where $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$ and $\tan \theta = y/x$ . Conversely, $x = r \cos \theta$ and $y = r \sin \theta$ . Thus, $(r, \theta)$ are “polar” coordinates corresponding to $(x, y)$ .
