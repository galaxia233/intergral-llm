35.18 Describe the graph of the polar equation $r = 6(\sin \theta +\cos \theta)$

Multiply both sides by $r$ : $r^2 = 6r\sin \theta + 6r\cos \theta$ , $x^2 + y^2 = 6y + 6x$ , $x^2 - 6x + y^2 - 6y = 0$ , $(x - 3)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 18$ . Thus, the graph is the circle with center at (3, 3) and radius $3\sqrt{2}$ .
