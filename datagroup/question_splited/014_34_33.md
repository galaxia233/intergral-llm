34.35 Find the arc length of $x = 2\cos \theta +\cos 2\theta +1,\quad y = 2\sin \theta +\sin 2\theta ,\quad \mathrm{for}\quad 0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi .$

\(
\begin{array}{l}
dx / d\theta = -2 \sin \theta - 2 \sin 2\theta, \quad (dx / d\theta)^2 = 4 (\sin^2 \theta + 2 \sin \theta \sin 2\theta + \sin^2 2\theta). \\
(dy / d\theta)^2 = 4 (\cos^2 \theta + 2 \cos \theta \cos 2\theta + \cos^2 2\theta).
\end{array}
\)

$$
s = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \sqrt {\left(d x / d \theta\right) ^ {2} + \left(d y / d \theta\right) ^ {2}} d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} 2 \sqrt {2 + 2 \sin \theta \sin 2 \theta + 2 \cos \theta \cos 2 \theta} d \theta = 2 \sqrt {2} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \sqrt {1 + \cos \theta} d \theta .
$$

[Note that $\sin \theta \sin 2\theta + \cos \theta \cos 2\theta = \cos (2\theta - \theta) = \cos \theta$ .] Since $1 + \cos \theta = 2\cos^2(\theta / 2)$ , $\sqrt{1 + \cos \theta} = \sqrt{2} |\cos (\theta / 2)|$ . Thus, we have: $2\sqrt{2} \left[ \int_{0}^{\pi} \sqrt{2} \cos (\theta / 2) d\theta + \int_{\pi}^{2\pi} -\sqrt{2} \cos (\theta / 2) d\theta \right] = 4 \left\{ 2 \sin (\theta / 2) \right\}_{0}^{\pi} - 2 \sin (\theta / 2) \big]_{\pi}^{2\pi} \big\} = 8[(1 - 0) - (0 - 1)] = 16$ .
