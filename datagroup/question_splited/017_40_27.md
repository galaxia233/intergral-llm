40.27 Find the direction cosines of the vector $\overrightarrow{PQ}$ of Problem 40.25.

In general, if $\mathbf{A} = (a,b,c)$ , then the direction cosines of $\mathbf{A}$ are $\cos \alpha = a / |\mathbf{A}|$ , $\cos \beta = b / |\mathbf{A}|$ , $\cos \gamma = c / |\mathbf{A}|$ , where $\alpha$ , $\beta$ , and $\gamma$ are the direction angles between $\mathbf{A}$ and the positive $x$ -axis, $y$ -axis, and $z$ -axis, respectively. These angles are between 0 and $\pi$ (inclusive). Therefore, $\mathbf{A} = (|\mathbf{A}| \cos \alpha, |\mathbf{A}| \cos \beta, |\mathbf{A}| \cos \gamma)$ = $|\mathbf{A}| (\cos \alpha, \cos \beta, \cos \gamma)$ . In this case, $\overrightarrow{PQ} = (2,6,-1)$ and $|\overrightarrow{PQ}| = \sqrt{41}$ . So, $\cos \alpha = 2 / \sqrt{41}$ , $\cos \beta = 6 / \sqrt{41}$ , $\cos \gamma = -1 / \sqrt{41}$ . Thus, $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are acute and $\gamma$ is obtuse.
