31.20 A conical tank, 10 meters deep and 8 meters across at the top, is filled with water to a depth of 5 meters. The tank is emptied by pumping the water over the top edge. How much work is done in the process?

![](images/fdedb8cb99c285a198f7a4020536df104dcffa5765dbe4dee5b8fcb3a961e4e2.jpg)

See Fig. 31-2. Let $x$ be the distance from the bottom of the tank. Consider a slab of water of thickness $\Delta x$ at depth $x$ . By similar triangles, we see that $r / x = \frac{4}{10}$ , where $r$ is the radius of the slab. Then $r = 2x / 5$ . The weight of the slab is approximately $w\pi r^2 \Delta x$ , where $w$ is the weight-density of water (about $9.8 \, \text{kN/m}^3$ ) and the slab is raised a distance of $10 - x$ . Hence, the work is $\int_0^5 w\pi r^2 (10 - x) dx = w\pi \int_0^5 \frac{4x^2}{25} (10 - x) dx = \frac{4\pi w}{25} \left( \frac{10}{3} x^3 - \frac{1}{4} x^4 \right) \int_0^5 = \frac{4\pi w}{25} \left( \frac{1250}{3} - \frac{625}{4} \right) = \frac{4\pi w}{25} \left( \frac{3125}{12} \right) = \frac{125}{3} \pi w \approx 1283 \, \text{kJ}$ .
