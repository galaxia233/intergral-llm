40.63 Show that the points $(0,0,0)$ , $(a_{1},a_{2},a_{3})$ , $(b_{1},b_{2},b_{3})$ , and $(c_{1},c_{2},c_{3})$ are coplanar if and only if

$$
\left| \begin{array}{l l l} a _ {1} & a _ {2} & a _ {3} \\ b _ {1} & b _ {2} & b _ {3} \\ c _ {1} & c _ {2} & c _ {3} \end{array} \right| = 0
$$

Let $\mathbf{A} = (a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3})$ , $\mathbf{B} = (b_{1}, b_{2}, b_{3})$ , $\mathbf{C} = (c_{1}, c_{2}, c_{3})$ . By Problems 40.45 and 40.49, the determinant above is equal to $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$ , which is equal in magnitude to the nonzero volume of the parallelepiped formed by $\mathbf{A}$ , $\mathbf{B}$ , $\mathbf{C}$ , when the given points are not coplanar. If those points are coplanar, either $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \mathbf{0}$ , and, therefore, $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = 0$ ; or $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} \neq \mathbf{0}$ and $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}$ (because $\mathbf{A}$ is in the plane of $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ ), so that again $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = 0$ .
