45.7 If $\mathbf{G}(t) = (t, t^3, \ln t)$ and $\mathbf{F}(t) = t^2\mathbf{G}(t)$ , find $\mathbf{F}'(t)$ .

In general, $\frac{d}{dt} [g(t)\mathbf{G}(t)] = g(t)\mathbf{G}'(t) + g'(t)\mathbf{G}(t)$ . (The proof in Problem 34.53 is valid for arbitrary vector functions.) Hence, $\mathbf{F}'(t) = t^2 (1,3t^2,1 / t) + 2t(t,t^3,\ln t) = (3t^2,5t^4,t + 2t\ln t)$ .
