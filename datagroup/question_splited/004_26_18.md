26.18 If 20 percent of a radioactive element disappears in 1 year, compute its half-life.

Let $y_0$ be the original amount, and let $T$ be the half-life. Then $0.8y_0$ remains when $t = 1$ . Thus, $0.8y_0 = y_0e^K$ , $0.8 = e^K$ , $K = \ln 0.8 \approx -0.2231$ (from a table of logarithms). But $KT = -\ln 2 \approx -0.6931$ . So $-0.2231T \approx -0.6931$ , $T \approx 3.1067$ years.
