34.51 Find the tangent vector $\mathbf{v}$ and acceleration vector $\mathbf{a}$ for the ellipse $\mathbf{R}(t) = (a\cos t, b\sin t)$ , and show that $\mathbf{a}$ is opposite in direction to $\mathbf{R}(t)$ and of the same length.

$$
\mathbf {v} = \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime} (t) = (- a \sin t, b \cos t), \quad \mathbf {a} = \mathbf {R} ^ {\prime \prime} (t) = (- a \cos t, - b \sin t) = - (a \cos t, b \sin t) = - \mathbf {R} (t).
$$
