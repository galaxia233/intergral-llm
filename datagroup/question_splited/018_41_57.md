41.57 Find $\lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)}\frac{x^3 + y^3}{x^2 + y}$ if it exists.

Let $y = mx$ . Then

$$
\frac {x ^ {3} + y ^ {3}}{x ^ {2} + y} = \frac {x ^ {3} + m ^ {3} x ^ {3}}{x ^ {2} + m x} = \frac {x ^ {2} (1 + m ^ {3})}{x + m} \rightarrow 0 \quad \text {a s} \quad (x, y) \rightarrow (0, 0)
$$

[This is also true when $m = 0$ . In that case, $y = 0$ and $(x^3 + y^3) / (x^2 + y) = x \to 0$ ]. However, let $(x, y) \to (0, 0)$ along $y = -x^2 e^x$ . Then

$$
\frac {x ^ {3} + y ^ {3}}{x ^ {2} + y} = \frac {x ^ {3} - x ^ {6} e ^ {3 x}}{x ^ {2} - x ^ {2} e ^ {x}} = \frac {x - x ^ {4} e ^ {3 x}}{1 - e ^ {x}}
$$

By L'Hopital's rule,

$$
\lim  _ {x \rightarrow 0} \frac {x - x ^ {4} e ^ {3 x}}{1 - e ^ {x}} = \lim  _ {x \rightarrow 0} \frac {1 - 3 x ^ {4} e ^ {3 x} - 4 x ^ {3} e ^ {3 x}}{- e ^ {x}} = - 1.
$$

Hence, the limit cannot exist.
