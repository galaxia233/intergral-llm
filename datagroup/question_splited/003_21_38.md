21.38 Find the area of the bounded region between $y = x^2$ and $y = 2x$ (see Fig. 21-26).

Setting $x^{2} = 2x$ , we find $x = 0$ or $x = 2$ . Hence, the curves intersect at $(0,0)$ and $(2,4)$ . For $0 \leq x \leq 2$ , $x^{2} \leq 2$ , and, therefore, $y = 2x$ is the upper curve. The area is $\int_{0}^{2} (2x - x^{2}) dx = (x^{2} - \frac{1}{3} x^{3}) \bigg]_{0}^{2} = 4 - \frac{8}{3} = \frac{4}{3}$ .
