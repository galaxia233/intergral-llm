21.30 Find the length of the arc of the curve $x = 3y^{3/2} - 1$ from $y = 0$ to $y = 4$ .

The arc length $L = \int_0^4\sqrt{1 + (dx / dy)^2} dy,$ $dx / dy = \frac{9}{2} y^{1 / 2},$ $1 + (dx / dy)^2 = 1 + 81y / 4 = (4 + 81y) / 4.$ So, $L = \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{4}\sqrt{4 + 81y} dy.$ Let $u = 4 + 81y,$ $du = 81dy.$ Then $L = \frac{1}{2}\int_{4}^{328}u^{1 / 2}\cdot \frac{1}{81} du = \frac{1}{162}\left(\frac{2}{3} u^{3 / 2}\right)\big]_4^{328} =$ $\frac{1}{243}(328\cdot 2\sqrt{82} -8) = \frac{8}{243}(82\sqrt{82} -1).$
