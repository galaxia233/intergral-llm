29.36 Find $\int \frac {1}{\sqrt{1 - \sin 2x}} dx.$

From the identity in the solution of Problem 29.34, $\sqrt{1 - \sin 2x} = \sqrt{1 - \cos(\pi / 2 - 2x)} = \sqrt{2}\sin (\pi /4 - x)$ . Hence, $\int \frac {dx}{\sqrt{1 - \sin 2x}} = \frac {\sqrt{2}}{2}\int \frac {dx}{\sin(\pi / 4 - x)} = \frac {\sqrt{2}}{2}\int \csc (\pi /4 - x)dx = \frac {\sqrt{2}}{2}\ln |\csc (\pi /4 - x) - \cot (\pi /4 - x)| + C.$
