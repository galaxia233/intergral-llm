41.21 Show that the plane $2x - y - 2z = 10$ intersects the paraboloid $2z = \frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{4}$ at a single point, and find the point.

Solve the equations simultaneously. $2x - y - 10 = \frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{4}, \quad 4x^2 + 9y^2 - 72x + 36y = -360, \quad 4(x - 9)^2 + 9(y + 2)^2 = -360 + 324 + 36 = 0$ . Hence, the only solution is $x = 9, \quad y = -2$ . Then $z = 5$ . Thus, the only point of intersection is $(9, -2, 5)$ , where the plane is tangent to the paraboloid.
