44.41 Use integration in cylindrical coordinates to find the volume of a right circular cone of radius $a$ and height $h$ .

The base is the disk of radius $a$ , given by $r \leq a$ . For given $r$ , the corresponding value of $z$ on the cone is determined by $z / (a - r) = h / a$ (obtained by similar triangles; see Fig. 44-25.) Then $V = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^a (h / a)(a - r)r dr d\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} [\frac{1}{2} hr^2 - (h / 3a)r^3]_0^a d\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} (\frac{1}{2} ha^2 - \frac{1}{3} ha^2)d\theta = \frac{1}{6} ha^2 \int_0^{2\pi} d\theta = \frac{1}{6} ha^2(2\pi) = \frac{1}{3} \pi a^2 h$ , the standard formula.
