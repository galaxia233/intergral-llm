45.20 Find an equation of the osculating plane of the curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (3t^2, 1 - t, t^3)$ at $(12, -1, 8)$ corresponding to $t = 2$ .

$\mathbf{R}'(t) = (6t, -1, 3t^2) = (12, -1, 12)$ , and $\mathbf{R}''(t) = (6, 0, 6t) = (6, 0, 12)$ . Hence, $\mathbf{R}' \times \mathbf{R}'' = (12, -1, 12) \times (6, 0, 12) = (-12, -72, 72) = -12(1, 6, -6)$ . By Problem 45.19, $-12(1, 6, -6)$ , or more simply $(1, 6, -6)$ , is a normal vector to the osculating plane. Hence, an equation of that plane is $x - 12 + 6(y + 1) - 6(z - 8) = 0$ or equivalently, $x + 6y - 6z + 42 = 0$ .
