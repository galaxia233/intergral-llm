43.44 Find the point in the plane $2x - y + 2x = 16$ nearest the origin.

It suffices to minimize $x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2}$ over all points $(x, y, z)$ in the plane, or, equivalently, to minimize $f(x, y) = x^{2} + y^{2} + \left(\frac{16 - 2x + y}{2}\right)^{2}$ . $f_{x} = 2x + \frac{1}{2}(16 - 2x + y)(-2)$ , $f_{y} = 2y + \frac{1}{2}(16 - 2x + y)$ . Set $f_{x} = f_{y} = 0$ . $2x = 16 - 2x + y$ , $-4y = 16 - 2x + y$ . Then $y = 4x - 16$ , $-5y = 16 - 2x$ . Hence, $-5(4x - 16) = 16 - 2x$ , $-20x + 80 = 16 - 2x$ , $64 = 18x$ , $x = \frac{32}{9}$ . Then $y = 4\left(\frac{32}{9}\right) - 16 = -\frac{16}{9}$ , $z = \frac{16 - 2x + y}{2} = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{144 - 64 - 16}{9}\right) = \frac{32}{9}$ . Thus, the nearest point is $\left(\frac{32}{9}, -\frac{16}{9}, \frac{32}{9}\right)$ .
