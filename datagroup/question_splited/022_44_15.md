44.15 Let $\mathcal{R}$ be the region bounded by the curve $y = \sqrt{x}$ and the line $y = x$ (Fig. 44-6). Let $f(x, y) = \frac{\sin y}{y}$ if $y \neq 0$ and $f(x, 0) = 1$ . Compute $I = \iint_{\mathcal{R}} f(x, y) \, dA$ .

\(
I = \int_{0}^{1}\int_{y^2}^{y}\frac{\sin y}{y} dx dy = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\sin y}{y} x\Bigg]_{y^2}^{y}dy = \int_{0}^{1}(\sin y - y\sin y)dy. \text{Integration by parts yields $\int y\sin y dy = \sin y - y\cos y$. Hence, $I = (-\cos y + y\cos y - \sin y)]_0^1 = (-\sin 1) - (-1) = 1 - \sin 1$.}
\)
