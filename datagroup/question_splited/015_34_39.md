34.81 Let $\mathbf{T}(t)$ be the unit tangent vector to a curve $\mathbf{R}(t)$ . Show that, wherever $\mathbf{T}'(t) \neq \mathbf{0}$ , the principal unit normal vector $\mathbf{N} = \mathbf{T}' / |\mathbf{T}'|$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{T}$ .

The argument of Problem 34.59 applies to any differentiable vector function. Therefore, $\mathbf{T} \cdot \mathbf{T}' = \mathbf{0}$ , which in turn implies $\mathbf{T} \cdot \mathbf{N} = 0$ wherever $\mathbf{N}$ is defined (i.e., wherever $|\mathbf{T}'| > 0$ ).
