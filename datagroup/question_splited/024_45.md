45.1 For the space curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (t, t^2, t^3)$ , find a tangent vector and the tangent line at the point $(1, 1, 1)$ .

A tangent vector is given by the derivative $\mathbf{R}'(t) = (1, 2t, 3t^2)$ . At $(1, 1, 1)$ , $t = 1$ . Hence, a tangent vector is $(1, 2, 3)$ . Parametric equations for the tangent line are $x = 1 + u$ , $y = 1 + 2u$ , $z = 1 + 3u$ . As a vector function, the tangent line can be represented by $(1, 1, 1) + u(1, 2, 3)$ .
