45.16 Find the principal unit normal vector $\mathbf{N}$ to the curve $\mathbf{R}(t) = (t, t^2, t^3)$ when $t = 1$ .

The unit tangent vector is $\mathbf{T} = \frac{\mathbf{R}'(t)}{|\mathbf{R}'(t)|} = \frac{(1,2t,3t^2)}{\sqrt{1 + 4t^2 + 9t^4}}$ . The principal unit normal vector is $\mathbf{N} = \frac{\mathbf{T}'(t)}{|\mathbf{T}'(t)|}$ . Now, $\mathbf{T}'(t) = \frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{\mathbf{R}'(t)}{|\mathbf{R}'(t)|}\right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 4t^2 + 9t^4}}\mathbf{R}''(t) + \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 4t^2 + 9t^4}}\right)\mathbf{R}'(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 4t^2 + 9t^4}} (0,2,6t) - \frac{4t + 18t^3}{(1 + 4t^2 + 9t^4)^{3/2}} (1,2t,3t^2)$ . When $t = 1$ , $\mathbf{T}'(t) = \frac{2(0,1,3)}{\sqrt{14}} - \frac{22}{14\sqrt{14}} (1,2,3) = \frac{1}{7\sqrt{14}} [14(0,1,3) - 11(1,2,3)] = \frac{1}{7\sqrt{14}} (-11, -8,9)$ . Therefore, $|\mathbf{T}'(t)| = \frac{1}{7\sqrt{14}}\sqrt{121 + 64 + 81} = \frac{\sqrt{266}}{7\sqrt{14}} = \frac{\sqrt{19}}{7}$ . Thus,

$$
\mathbf {N} = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2 6 6}} (- 1 1, - 8, 9).
$$
