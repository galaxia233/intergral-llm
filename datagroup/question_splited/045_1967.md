【1967】 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1 + \sqrt{1 - 2x - x^2}}.$

提示 令 $\sqrt{1 - 2x - x^2} = xz - 1$

解设 $\sqrt{1 - 2x - x^2} = xz - 1$ ，则

$$
z = \frac {1 + \sqrt {1 - 2 x - x ^ {2}}}{x}, \quad x = \frac {2 (z - 1)}{z ^ {2} + 1}, d x = \frac {2 (1 + 2 z - z ^ {2})}{(z ^ {2} + 1) ^ {2}} d z, \quad \sqrt {1 - 2 x - x ^ {2}} + 1 = \frac {2 z (z - 1)}{z ^ {2} + 1}.
$$

代入得 $\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1 + \sqrt{1 - 2x - x^2}} = \int \frac{1 + 2z - z^2}{z(z - 1)(z^2 + 1)}\mathrm{d}z = \int \left[\frac{1}{z - 1} -\frac{1}{z} -\frac{2}{z^2 + 1}\right]\mathrm{d}z$

$$
= \ln \left| \frac {z - 1}{z} \right| - 2 \arctan z + C,
$$

其中 $z = \frac{1 + \sqrt{1 - 2x - x^2}}{x}$
