34.43 If $\mathbf{F}(u) = (f(u), g(u))$ is a two-dimensional vector function, $\lim_{u \to a} \mathbf{F}(u) = (\lim_{u \to a} f(u), \lim_{u \to a} g(u))$ , where the limit on the left exists if and only if the limits on the right exist. Taking this as the definition of vector convergence, show that $\mathbf{F}'(u) = (f'(u), g'(u))$ .

$$
\begin{array}{l} I \quad F ^ {\prime} (u) = \lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \frac {F (u + \Delta u) - F (u)}{\Delta u} = \lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \frac {(f (u + \Delta u) , g (u + \Delta u)) - (f (u) , g (u))}{\Delta u} \\ = \lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \frac {(f (u + \Delta u) - f (u) , g (u + \Delta u) - g (u))}{\Delta u} = \lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \left(\frac {f (u + \Delta u) - f (u)}{\Delta u}, \quad \frac {g (u + \Delta u) - g (u)}{\Delta u}\right) \\ \end{array}
$$

$$
\text {T h i s l a s t l i m i t i s , b y t h e d e f i n i o n , e q u a l t o} \quad \left(\lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \frac {f (u + \Delta u) - f (u)}{\Delta u}, \quad \lim  _ {\Delta u \rightarrow 0} \frac {g (u + \Delta u) - g (u)}{\Delta u}\right) = \left(f ^ {\prime} (u), g ^ {\prime} (u)\right).
