33.40 For nonzero vectors $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ , find a necessary and sufficient condition that $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = |\mathbf{A}| |\mathbf{B}|$ .

$\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = |\mathbf{A}| |\mathbf{B}| \cos \theta$ , where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ . Hence, $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = |\mathbf{A}| |\mathbf{B}|$ if and only if $\cos \theta = 1$ , that is, if and only if $\theta = 0$ , which is equivalent to $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ having the same direction. (In other words, $\mathbf{A} = u\mathbf{B}$ for some positive scalar $u$ .)
