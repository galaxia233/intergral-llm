35.59 Find the area inside the limacon $r = 4 + 2\cos \theta$

From Problem 35.38, we see that the area is swept out from $\theta = 0$ to $\theta = 2\pi$ . So, the area is $\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{2\pi} (16 + 16 \cos \theta + 4 \cos^2 \theta) d\theta = \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{2\pi} [16 + 16 \cos \theta + 2(1 + \cos 2\theta)] d\theta = \frac{1}{2} (18\theta + 16 \sin \theta + \sin 2\theta) \int_{0}^{2\pi} \frac{1}{2} (36\pi) = 18\pi$ .
