35.82 Find the arc length of $r = \cos^2 (\theta / 2)$ (Problem 35.45).

$$
\begin{array}{l} \text {I} d r / d \theta = - \cos (\theta / 2) \sin (\theta / 2). \quad \text {T h e n} \quad L = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \sqrt {\cos^ {4} (\theta / 2) + \cos^ {2} (\theta / 2) \sin^ {2} (\theta / 2)} d \theta = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} | \cos (\theta / 2) | d \theta = \\ \int_ {0} ^ {\pi} \cos (\theta / 2) d \theta - \int_ {\pi} ^ {2 \pi} \cos (\theta / 2) d u = 2 \sin (\theta / 2) ] _ {0} ^ {\pi} - 2 \sin (\theta / 2) ] _ {\pi} ^ {2 \pi} = 2 [ (1 - 0) - (0 - 1) ] = 4. \end{array}
