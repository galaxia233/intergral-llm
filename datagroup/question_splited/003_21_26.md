21.26 Find the area of the region bounded by the curves $y = x^2 - 4x$ and $x + y = 0$ .

See Fig. 21-21. The parabola $y = x^2 - 4x = (x - 2)^2 - 4$ has vertex $(2, -4)$ . Let us find the intersection of the curves: $x^2 - 4x = -x$ , $x^2 - 3x = 0$ , $x(x - 3) = 0$ , $x = 0$ or $x = 3$ . So, the points of intersection are $(0, 0)$ and $(3, -3)$ . Hence, the area is $\int_0^3 [-x - (x^2 - 4x)] dx = \int_0^3 (3x - x^2) dx = \left(\frac{3}{2} x^2 - \frac{1}{3} x^3\right) \bigg]_0^3 = \frac{27}{2} - 9 = \frac{9}{2}$ .
