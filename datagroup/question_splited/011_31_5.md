31.28 A right triangle with legs $r$ and $h$ .

Let the right angle be at the origin, and let the legs $r$ and $h$ be along the positive $x$ -axis and $y$ -axis, respectively (Fig. 31-6). The hypotenuse is along the line $y = -\frac{h}{r} x + h$ . The area $A$ is $\frac{1}{2} hr$ . The moment about the $y$ -axis is $M_y = \int_0^r x \left( h - \frac{h}{r} x \right) dx = \left( \frac{1}{2} hx^2 - \frac{h}{3r} x^3 \right) \int_0^r = \frac{1}{2} hr^2 - \frac{h}{3} r^2 = \frac{1}{6} hr^2$ . Hence, $\bar{x} = \frac{\frac{1}{6} hr^2}{\frac{1}{2} hr} = \frac{1}{3} r$ . In similar manner, $\bar{y} = \frac{1}{3} h$ .
