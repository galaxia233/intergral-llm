45.9 If $\mathbf{F}(t) = (\sin t, \cos t, t)$ and $\mathbf{G}(t) = (t, 1, \ln t)$ , find $\frac{d}{dt}[\mathbf{F}(t) \cdot \mathbf{G}(t)]$ .

The product formula $\frac{d}{dt}[\mathbf{F}(t) \cdot \mathbf{G}(t)] = \mathbf{F}(t) \cdot \mathbf{G}'(t) + \mathbf{F}'(t) \cdot \mathbf{G}(t)$ of Problem 34.56 holds for arbitrary vector functions. So $\frac{d}{dt}[\mathbf{F}(t) \cdot \mathbf{G}(t)] = (\sin t, \cos t, t) \cdot (1, 0, 1 / t) + (\cos t, -\sin t, 1) \cdot (t, 1, \ln t) = \sin t + 1 + t \cos t - \sin t + \ln t = 1 + t \cos t + \ln t$ .
