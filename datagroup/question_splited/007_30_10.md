30.10 $\int \frac{x + 4}{x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x} dx.$

$\frac{x + 4}{x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x} = \frac{x + 4}{x(x + 3)^2} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x + 3} + \frac{C}{(x + 3)^2}$ . Then $x + 4 = A(x + 3)^2 + B(x + 3) + Cx$ . Let $x = 0$ . Then $4 = 9A$ , $A = \frac{4}{9}$ . Let $x = -3$ . Then $1 = -3C$ , $C = -\frac{1}{3}$ . To find $B$ , equate coefficients of $x^2$ : $0 = A + B$ , $B = -A = -\frac{4}{9}$ . Thus, $\frac{x + 4}{x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x} = \frac{4}{9}\frac{1}{x} - \frac{4}{9}\frac{1}{x + 3} - \frac{1}{3}\frac{1}{(x + 3)^2}$ , and $\int \frac{x + 4}{x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x} dx = \frac{4}{9}\ln |x| - \frac{4}{9}\ln |x + 3| + \frac{1}{3}\frac{1}{x + 3} + C_1 = \frac{4}{9}\ln \left|\frac{x}{x + 3}\right| + \frac{1}{3}\frac{1}{x + 3} + C_1$ .
